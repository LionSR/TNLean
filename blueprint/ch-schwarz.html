Schwarz Inequalities and Multiplicative Domains

This chapter develops the Schwarz-inequality theory following [ Wol12 , Chapter 5 ] . We begin with the Kadison–Schwarz inequality for Kraus maps, then describe the multiplicative domain and its algebraic structure. These two parts are used in the non-periodic Fundamental Theorem through peripheral-eigenvector rigidity and normal-block comparison. We also establish the extension to normal operators for positive subunital maps, the resulting order and spectral contractivity statements, and the standard example of a Schwarz map that is not completely positive. The operator-convexity and Jensen material from [ Wol12 , Chapter 5 ] is treated separately in Chapter ??.

Kadison–Schwarz inequality

A Kraus map is the concrete realisation of a completely positive map (Definition .4). The Kadison–Schwarz inequality and the multiplicative-domain characterisation are proved first for Kraus maps. The transfer map is a Kraus map by construction (Remark .17), so these results apply directly to transfer maps.

Definitions .1–.4 below restate the Kraus-map notions from Chapter ?? for the reader’s convenience.

Definition .1 Kraus map

KadisonSchwarz.krausMap def:cp_map Given operators \(\{ K_i\} _{i=0}^{d-1}\) with \(K_i \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\), the Kraus map is \(E(X) = \sum _{i=0}^{d-1} K_i X K_i^\dagger \). A linear map is completely positive (Definition .4) if and only if it can be written in this form.

Definition .2 Adjoint Kraus map

KadisonSchwarz.krausAdjointMap def:kraus_map The adjoint Kraus map is \(E^*(X) = \sum _{i=0}^{d-1} K_i^\dagger X K_i\). When the \(\{ K_i\} \) are the matrices of an MPS tensor \(A\), this is the transfer map of the conjugate-transposed family \(i \mapsto (A^i)^\dagger \).

Definition .3 Unital Kraus map

KadisonSchwarz.IsUnitalKraus def:kraus_map A Kraus map is unital if \(\sum _{i=0}^{d-1} K_i K_i^\dagger = \mathbb {1}\). Equivalently, \(E(\mathbb {1}) = \mathbb {1}\). In the later gauge language this is the right-canonical condition.

Definition .4 Trace-preserving Kraus map

KadisonSchwarz.IsTPKraus def:kraus_map A Kraus map is trace-preserving if \(\sum _{i=0}^{d-1} K_i^\dagger K_i = \mathbb {1}\). Equivalently, the adjoint Kraus map is unital. This is the standard MPS normalization condition. In the later gauge language it is the left-canonical condition, so Kadison–Schwarz arguments are often applied to the adjoint map.

Theorem .5 Kadison–Schwarz inequality

KadisonSchwarz.kadison_schwarz def:unital_kraus Let \(E(X) = \sum _i K_i X K_i^\dagger \) be a unital Kraus map (i.e. \(\sum _i K_i K_i^\dagger = \mathbb {1}\)). Then for all \(X \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\),

\[ E(X^\dagger X) \ge E(X)^\dagger E(X). \]

This is [ Wol12 , Equation (5.2) ] .

▼
The \(2 \times 2\) block matrix \([\begin{smallmatrix} \end{smallmatrix} X^\dagger X & X^\dagger \\ X & \mathbb {1}\end{smallmatrix}]\) is PSD (\(= v v^\dagger \) with \(v = [X^\dagger ,\, \mathbb {1}]^T\)). Applying the unital CP map blockwise preserves positive semidefiniteness (each block \(K_i (\cdot ) K_i^\dagger \) preserves PSD, and unitality ensures the \((2,2)\)-block maps to \(\mathbb {1}\)). The Schur complement of the \((2,2)\)-block \(\mathbb {1}\) gives the result.
▼

Theorem .6 Kadison–Schwarz for the adjoint channel

KadisonSchwarz.kadison_schwarz_adjoint def:tp_kraus, def:kraus_adjoint_map If \(\sum _i K_i^\dagger K_i = \mathbb {1}\) (trace-preserving), then the adjoint Kraus map satisfies \(E^*(X^\dagger X) \ge E^*(X)^\dagger E^*(X)\).

▼
thm:kadison_schwarz The adjoint operators \(\{ K_i^\dagger \} \) satisfy \(\sum _i K_i^\dagger (K_i^\dagger )^\dagger = \sum _i K_i^\dagger K_i = \mathbb {1}\), so they form a unital family. Apply Theorem .5 to \(\{ K_i^\dagger \} \).
▼

Two-positive maps and the generalized Schwarz inequality

Definition .7 \(k\)-positive map

IsNPositiveMap A linear map \(E : M_{D}(\mathbb {C}) \to M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) is \(k\)-positive if \(E \otimes \id _{k}\) is positive on \(M_{D}(\mathbb {C}) \otimes M_{k}(\mathbb {C})\).

Definition .8 Two-positive map

Is2PositiveMap A linear map is two-positive if it is \(2\)-positive.

Theorem .9 Completely positive maps are \(k\)-positive

IsCPMap.isNPositiveMap def:cp_map Every completely positive map is \(k\)-positive for all \(k \ge 1\).

Theorem .10 \((k\! +\! 1)\)-positive implies \(k\)-positive

IsNPositiveMap.mono Positivity under amplification is monotone in \(k\).

Theorem .11 Completely positive implies two-positive

IsCPMap.is2PositiveMap def:cp_map Every completely positive map is two-positive.

Theorem .12 Two-positive implies positive

Is2PositiveMap.isPositiveMap Every two-positive map is positive.

Definition .13 Unital linear map

KadisonSchwarz.IsUnitalMap A linear map \(E\) is unital if \(E(\mathbb {1}) = \mathbb {1}\).

Theorem .14 Kadison–Schwarz for unital two-positive maps

kadison_schwarz_2positive thm:kadison_schwarz If \(E\) is unital and two-positive, then for all \(X \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\),

\[ E(X^\dagger X) \ge E(X)^\dagger E(X). \]

▼
Form the \(2 \times 2\) block matrix \((\begin{smallmatrix} \end{smallmatrix} \mathbb {1}& X \\ X^\dagger & X^\dagger X \end{smallmatrix}) \ge 0\). Apply \(E \otimes \id _{2}\) (positive by \(2\)-positivity) and read off the \((2,2)\)-block Schur complement: \(E(X^\dagger X) - E(X)^\dagger E(X) \ge 0\).
▼

Theorem .15 Kraus-form Kadison–Schwarz as a special case

kadison_schwarz_from_2positive thm:kadison_schwarz The Kraus-map Kadison–Schwarz inequality is recovered from the two-positive formulation.

Douglas-type factorization lemmas

Theorem .16 Range inclusion implies right factorization

Douglas.factorization_of_range_mulVecLin_le If \(\operatorname {ran}(A) \subseteq \operatorname {ran}(B)\), then there exists \(C\) such that \(A = B C\).

▼
In finite dimensions, \(\operatorname {ran}(A) \subseteq \operatorname {ran}(B)\) implies \(A = B (B^\dagger B)^{\dagger } B^\dagger A\), where \((B^\dagger B)^{\dagger }\) denotes the Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse. Set \(C = (B^\dagger B)^{\dagger } B^\dagger A\).
▼

Theorem .17 Vectorwise range criterion implies factorization

Douglas.factorization_of_forall_mulVec_mem_range If \(A v \in \operatorname {ran}(B)\) for every vector \(v\), then there exists \(C\) with \(A = B C\).

Theorem .18 Hilbert–Schmidt contraction

KadisonSchwarz.hilbertSchmidt_contraction def:unital_kraus, def:tp_kraus If a Kraus map is both unital and trace-preserving, then \(\tr (E(X)^\dagger E(X)) \le \tr (X^\dagger X)\) for all \(X \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\).

▼
thm:kadison_schwarz By Theorem .5, \(E(X^\dagger X) - E(X)^\dagger E(X) \ge 0\), so \(\tr (E(X^\dagger X)) \ge \tr (E(X)^\dagger E(X))\). Trace preservation gives \(\tr (E(X^\dagger X)) = \tr (X^\dagger X)\).
▼

Lemma .19 Trace pairing for Kraus map and adjoint map

Kraus.trace_mul_map_eq_trace_adjointMap_mul def:kraus_map, def:kraus_adjoint_map For all \(X, Y \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\),

\[ \tr \! (X\, E(Y)) = \tr \! (E^*(X)\, Y). \]

Lemma .20 Positive-definite trace test

Kraus.posSemidef_eq_zero_of_posDef_trace_mul_eq_zero If \(A {\gt} 0\), \(X \ge 0\), and \(\tr (A X) = 0\), then \(X = 0\).

Theorem .21 Fixed-point peripheral eigenvectors satisfy KS equality

Kraus.ks_equality_of_peripheral_eigenvector_of_fixedPoint thm:kadison_schwarz, lem:trace_mul_map_eq_trace_adjointMap_mul, lem:posSemidef_eq_zero_of_posDef_trace_mul_eq_zero Let \(E\) be unital and let \(E^*\) be trace-preserving with a positive definite fixed point \(\rho {\gt} 0\). If \(E(X) = \mu X\) with \(|\mu | = 1\), then

\[ E(X^\dagger X) = E(X)^\dagger E(X). \]

Multiplicative domain

Theorem .22 KS gap decomposition

KadisonSchwarz.ks_gap_eq_sum_squares def:unital_kraus, def:kraus_map For a unital Kraus map \(E\), the Kadison–Schwarz gap decomposes as

\[ E(X^\dagger X) - E(X)^\dagger E(X) = \sum _{i=0}^{d-1} R_i^\dagger R_i, \]

where \(R_i := X K_i^\dagger - K_i^\dagger E(X)\).

▼
def:unital_kraus Expand the right-hand side \(\sum _i (X K_i^\dagger - K_i^\dagger E(X))^\dagger (X K_i^\dagger - K_i^\dagger E(X))\), distribute, and use unitality \(\sum _i K_i K_i^\dagger = \mathbb {1}\) to identify the result with the KS gap.
▼

Theorem .23 KS equality implies Kraus commutation

KadisonSchwarz.kraus_commute_of_ks_equality def:unital_kraus Let \(E\) be a unital Kraus map. If \(E(X^\dagger X) = E(X)^\dagger E(X)\) for some \(X\), then \(X K_i^\dagger = K_i^\dagger E(X)\) for all \(i\).

▼
thm:ks_gap_decomp By Theorem .22, the KS gap is \(\sum _i R_i^\dagger R_i\) with \(R_i = X K_i^\dagger - K_i^\dagger E(X)\). If the gap vanishes, \(\sum _i R_i^\dagger R_i = 0\). Each summand \(R_i^\dagger R_i\) is PSD, so each must be zero, giving \(R_i = 0\).
▼

Theorem .24 KS equality for peripheral eigenvectors

KadisonSchwarz.ks_equality_of_peripheral_eigenvector def:unital_kraus, def:tp_kraus Let \(E\) be a Kraus map that is both unital and trace-preserving. If \(E(X) = \mu X\) with \(|\mu | = 1\), then the KS gap vanishes: \(E(X^\dagger X) = E(X)^\dagger E(X)\).

▼
thm:kadison_schwarz The gap \(G := E(X^\dagger X) - E(X)^\dagger E(X)\) is PSD by Theorem .5. Its trace satisfies \(\tr (G) = \tr (E(X^\dagger X)) - \tr (E(X)^\dagger E(X))\). Trace preservation gives \(\tr (E(X^\dagger X)) = \tr (X^\dagger X)\). Using \(E(X) = \mu X\) and \(|\mu | = 1\), \(\tr (E(X)^\dagger E(X)) = |\mu |^2 \tr (X^\dagger X) = \tr (X^\dagger X)\). So \(\tr (G) = 0\), and a PSD matrix with zero trace must be zero.
▼

Theorem .25 Left multiplicative identity from KS equality

KadisonSchwarz.multiplicative_domain_left def:unital_kraus Let \(E\) be a unital Kraus map. If \(E(X^\dagger X) = E(X)^\dagger E(X)\), then \(E(X^\dagger Y) = E(X)^\dagger E(Y)\) for all \(Y \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\).

▼
thm:kraus_commute From Theorem .23, \(X K_i^\dagger = K_i^\dagger E(X)\) for all \(i\). Taking conjugate transposes: \(K_i X^\dagger = E(X)^\dagger K_i\). Then
E(X^†Y) & = ∑_i K_i X^†Y K_i^†
& = ∑_i E(X)^†K_i Y K_i^†
& = E(X)^†E(Y).
▼

Full multiplicative-domain structure

Definition .26 Right and left multiplicative domains

KadisonSchwarz.rightMultiplicativeDomain, KadisonSchwarz.leftMultiplicativeDomain def:kraus_map For a Kraus map \(E\), define

\[ \mathcal{A}_R(E) := \{ X \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C}) : \forall Y \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C}),\ E(XY) = E(X) E(Y)\} , \]

and

\[ \mathcal{A}_L(E) := \{ X \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C}) : \forall Y \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C}),\ E(YX) = E(Y) E(X)\} . \]

We follow the convention that \(\mathcal{A}_R(E)\) controls right multiplication and \(\mathcal{A}_L(E)\) controls left multiplication. These are the two one-sided multiplicative domains from [ Wol12 , Eqs. (5.11)–(5.12) ] .

Definition .27 Full multiplicative domain

KadisonSchwarz.multiplicativeDomain def:one_sided_multiplicative_domains The multiplicative domain of \(E\) is

\[ \mathcal{A}(E) := \mathcal{A}_R(E) \cap \mathcal{A}_L(E). \]

Theorem .28 Multiplicative-domain characterization

KadisonSchwarz.mem_rightMultiplicativeDomain_iff, KadisonSchwarz.mem_leftMultiplicativeDomain_iff def:one_sided_multiplicative_domains, def:unital_kraus Let \(E\) be a unital Kraus map. Then

\[ X \in \mathcal{A}_R(E) \iff E(X X^\dagger ) = E(X) E(X)^\dagger , \]

and

\[ X \in \mathcal{A}_L(E) \iff E(X^\dagger X) = E(X)^\dagger E(X). \]

Together these are the two one-sided characterizations from [ Wol12 , Theorem 5.7 ] .

▼
thm:left_multiplicative_identity If \(X\) lies in a one-sided multiplicative domain, evaluate the defining identity at \(Y = X^\dagger \). Conversely, the left-domain implication is exactly Theorem .25, and the right-domain implication follows by applying Theorem .25 to \(X^\dagger \).
▼

Theorem .29 One-sided multiplicative domains are subalgebras

KadisonSchwarz.rightMultiplicativeDomainSubalgebra, KadisonSchwarz.leftMultiplicativeDomainSubalgebra def:one_sided_multiplicative_domains, def:unital_kraus For a unital Kraus map \(E\), both \(\mathcal{A}_R(E)\) and \(\mathcal{A}_L(E)\) are unital subalgebras of \(M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\).

▼
thm:md_characterization Closure under addition follows from linearity of \(E\). Closure under multiplication: if \(X, Y \in \mathcal{A}_R(E)\), then \(E((X Y) Z) = E(X) E(Y Z) = E(X) E(Y) E(Z)\) for all \(Z\), using Theorem .28 iteratively, and the \(\mathcal{A}_L(E)\) case is analogous. Containment of \(\mathbb {1}\) follows from unitality of \(E\).
▼

Theorem .30 Multiplicative domain is a \(*\)-subalgebra

KadisonSchwarz.multiplicativeDomainStarSubalgebra def:full_multiplicative_domain, thm:one_sided_md_subalgebras For a unital Kraus map \(E\), the full multiplicative domain \(\mathcal{A}(E)\) is a \(*\)-subalgebra of \(M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\). This is the algebraic content of [ Wol12 , Theorem 5.7 ] .

▼
thm:md_characterization, thm:one_sided_md_subalgebras If \(X \in \mathcal{A}(E) = \mathcal{A}_R(E) \cap \mathcal{A}_L(E)\), then \(E(X^\dagger X) = E(X)^\dagger E(X)\) and \(E(X X^\dagger ) = E(X) E(X)^\dagger \). The characterization of Theorem .28 shows \(X^\dagger \in \mathcal{A}(E)\). Combined with Theorem .29, this gives a \(*\)-subalgebra.
▼

Theorem .31 Restriction to multiplicative domain is a \(*\)-homomorphism

KadisonSchwarz.krausMapStarAlgHom thm:md_star_subalgebra, def:kraus_map If \(E\) is unital, then the restricted map \(E|_{\mathcal{A}(E)} : \mathcal{A}(E) \to M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) is a \(*\)-algebra homomorphism. This is the homomorphism conclusion of [ Wol12 , Theorem 5.7 ] .

▼
thm:kraus_commute Multiplicativity on \(\mathcal{A}(E)\) is immediate from the definition. For \(X \in \mathcal{A}(E)\), the Kraus commutation relation from Theorem .23 gives \(E(X^\dagger ) = E(X)^\dagger \), so the restriction preserves adjoints.
▼

Schwarz inequality for normal operators

Theorem .32 CP Schwarz inequality for normal operators

KadisonSchwarz.kadison_schwarz_normal_cp, KadisonSchwarz.kadison_schwarz_normal_cp_le def:tp_kraus, def:kraus_adjoint_map Let \(E^*(X) = \sum _i K_i^\dagger X K_i\) be the adjoint Kraus map of a trace-preserving Kraus family, and let \(A \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) be normal. Then the Schwarz gap

\[ E^*(A^\dagger A) - E^*(A^\dagger ) E^*(A) \]

is positive semidefinite. Equivalently,

\[ E^*(A^\dagger ) E^*(A) \le E^*(A^\dagger A). \]

This is the completely positive case of [ Wol12 , Proposition 5.1 ] .

▼
thm:kadison_schwarz_adjoint The positivity statement is exactly Theorem .6. The second formulation is just the corresponding Loewner-order reformulation.
▼

Theorem .33 Schwarz inequality for normal operators

KadisonSchwarz.schwarz_inequality_normal_operator def:positive_map Let \(T : M_{D}(\mathbb {C}) \to M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) be a positive linear map with \(T(\mathbb {1}) \le \mathbb {1}\). If \(A \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) is normal, then

\[ T(A^\dagger A) - T(A^\dagger ) T(A) \ge 0. \]

This is [ Wol12 , Proposition 5.1 ] .

▼
Since \(A\) is normal, diagonalize \(A = U D U^\dagger \) with \(D\) diagonal and \(U\) unitary. Express \(A^\dagger A = U |D|^2 U^\dagger \) as a sum \(\sum _{i,j} \bar{d}_i d_j P_i P_j\) where \(P_i = U e_i e_i^\dagger U^\dagger \). Each \(P_i\) is rank-one PSD, and the images \(T(P_i)\) pairwise commute because they are images of mutually orthogonal projectors under a positive map. The Schwarz gap then reduces to a block quadratic form in the images, which is shown non-negative by a simultaneous-diagonalization argument: positivity of \(T\) ensures the scalar PSD matrix \((\langle v_k, T(P_i P_j) v_k \rangle )_{i,j}\) dominates the product \((\langle v_k, T(P_i) v_k \rangle \langle v_k, T(P_j) v_k \rangle )_{i,j}\), and combining over a common eigenbasis of the \(T(P_i)\) yields the result.
▼

Remark .34

The proof follows the positivity-on-abelian argument of [ Wol12 , Proposition 1.6 ] : a positive map restricted to the commutative \(*\)-subalgebra generated by the spectral projectors of \(A\) satisfies the Schwarz inequality directly, without invoking the Arveson extension theorem.

Schwarz inequality for subnormal and commuting-dominant operators

Theorem .35 Schwarz inequality for subnormal operators

KadisonSchwarz.schwarz_inequality_subnormal_operator def:positive_map Let \(T : M_{D}(\mathbb {C}) \to M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) be a positive linear map with \(T(\mathbb {1}) \le \mathbb {1}\). If \(A \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) is subnormal (i.e. there exists a normal operator on a larger space whose compression to \(\mathbb {C}^D\) is \(A\)), then

\[ T(A^\dagger A) - T(A^\dagger ) T(A) \ge 0. \]

This is [ Wol12 , Theorem 5.5 ] .

▼
thm:schwarz_normal_operator Embed \(A\) as the northwest corner of a normal operator \(N\) on \(\mathbb {C}^D \oplus \mathbb {C}^E\). Extend \(T\) to a positive subunital map \(\tilde{T}\) on the larger space (acting as \(T\) on the northwest block, zero elsewhere). Apply Theorem .33 to \(\tilde{T}\) and \(N\), then compress the resulting inequality back to \(\mathbb {C}^D\).
▼

Theorem .36 Schwarz inequality for commuting-dominant operators

KadisonSchwarz.schwarz_inequality_commuting_dominant_operator def:positive_map Let \(T : M_{D}(\mathbb {C}) \to M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) be a positive linear map with \(T(\mathbb {1}) \le \mathbb {1}\). If \(A \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) admits a positive semidefinite dominant \(B \ge 0\) with \(A^\dagger A \le B\) and \(B\) commuting with \(A\) (in the sense \(BA = AB\), \(BA^\dagger = A^\dagger B\)), then

\[ T(A^\dagger ) T(A) \le T(B). \]

This is [ Wol12 , Theorem 5.6 ] .

▼
thm:schwarz_subnormal Let \(B^{1/2}\) be the positive square root of \(B\). Since \(B\) commutes with \(A\) and \(A^\dagger \), the contraction \(C = B^{-1/2} A\) (defined on the range of \(B^{1/2}\)) is subnormal. An \(\varepsilon \)-regularisation \(B_\varepsilon = B + \varepsilon \mathbb {1}\) makes \(B_\varepsilon {\gt} 0\) and the contraction \(C_\varepsilon \) globally defined. Apply Theorem .35 to \(C_\varepsilon \), multiply both sides by \(T(B_\varepsilon )\), use positivity and monotonicity, and take \(\varepsilon \to 0\).
▼

Theorem .37 CP variant: Kadison–Schwarz for commuting-dominant inputs

KadisonSchwarz.kadison_schwarz_commuting_dominant_cp def:tp_kraus, def:kraus_adjoint_map Let \(E^*\) be the adjoint of a trace-preserving Kraus map. If \(A \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) admits a positive semidefinite dominant \(B\) commuting with \(A\), \(A^\dagger \), and satisfying \(A^\dagger A \le B\), then \(E^*(A^\dagger ) E^*(A) \le E^*(B)\).

▼
thm:schwarz_commuting_dominant Apply Theorem .36 to the adjoint Kraus map (which is unital under the trace-preservation hypothesis).
▼

Positive maps preserve order and spectral intervals

Theorem .38 Positive maps are monotone

IsPositiveMap.map_le_map def:positive_map If \(T : M_{D}(\mathbb {C}) \to M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) is positive and \(A \le B\), then \(T(A) \le T(B)\).

▼
Since \(B - A \ge 0\), positivity gives \(T(B - A) \ge 0\). By linearity, this is \(T(B) - T(A) \ge 0\).
▼

Theorem .39 Positive maps preserve adjoints

IsPositiveMap.map_conjTranspose def:positive_map If \(T : M_{D}(\mathbb {C}) \to M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) is positive, then \(T(A^\dagger ) = T(A)^\dagger \) for all \(A \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\).

▼
Write \(A = B + i C\) with \(B\) and \(C\) Hermitian. Positive maps send Hermitian matrices to Hermitian matrices, so they preserve this decomposition and commute with taking adjoints.
▼

Theorem .40 Order intervals are preserved

IsPositiveMap.image_bounded thm:positive_map_monotone, def:positive_map Let \(T : M_{D}(\mathbb {C}) \to M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) be positive with \(T(\mathbb {1}) \le \mathbb {1}\). If \(a \le 0 \le b\) and \(a \mathbb {1}\le A \le b \mathbb {1}\), then

\[ a \mathbb {1}\le T(A) \le b \mathbb {1}. \]

This is the matrix form of [ Wol12 , Equation (5.21) ] .

▼
thm:positive_map_monotone By monotonicity, \(T(a \mathbb {1}) \le T(A) \le T(b \mathbb {1})\). For the lower bound, \(T(a \mathbb {1}) = a T(\mathbb {1})\). Since \(a \le 0\) and \(T(\mathbb {1}) \le \mathbb {1}\), we have \(a T(\mathbb {1}) \ge a \mathbb {1}\). For the upper bound, \(T(b \mathbb {1}) = b T(\mathbb {1}) \le b \mathbb {1}\) since \(b \ge 0\). Therefore \(a \mathbb {1}\le T(A) \le b \mathbb {1}\).
▼

Theorem .41 Spectral interval contractivity

IsPositiveMap.spectrum_contractivity thm:positive_map_image_bounded Let \(T : M_{D}(\mathbb {C}) \to M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) be positive with \(T(\mathbb {1}) \le \mathbb {1}\), and let \(A \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) be Hermitian. If \(\spec (A) \subseteq [a,b]\) with \(a \le 0 \le b\), then

\[ \spec (T(A)) \subseteq [a,b]. \]

This is [ Wol12 , Equation (5.21) ] .

▼
thm:positive_map_image_bounded For Hermitian matrices, the inclusion \(\spec (A) \subseteq [a,b]\) is equivalent to the order bounds \(a \mathbb {1}\le A \le b \mathbb {1}\). Apply Theorem .40 and translate the resulting inequalities back into spectral bounds.
▼

A positive Schwarz map that is not completely positive

Example .42 Transpose-trace map on \(M_{2}(\mathbb {C})\)

wolfExample53 Consider the linear map \(T : M_{2}(\mathbb {C}) \to M_{2}(\mathbb {C})\) defined by

\[ T(A) = \frac{1}{2} A^T + \frac{1}{4} \tr (A) \mathbb {1}. \]

This is the map from [ Wol12 , Example 5.3 ] .

Theorem .43 The transpose-trace map is positive

wolfExample53_isPositive def:wolf_example53, def:positive_map The map from Example .42 is positive.

▼
It is a non-negative linear combination of the transpose map and the map \(A \mapsto \tr (A) \mathbb {1}\), and both of these maps send positive semidefinite matrices to positive semidefinite matrices.
▼

Theorem .44 The transpose-trace map satisfies the Schwarz inequality

wolfExample53_satisfies_schwarz def:wolf_example53 For every \(A \in M_{2}(\mathbb {C})\),

\[ T(A^\dagger A) - T(A^\dagger ) T(A) \ge 0, \]

where \(T\) is the map from Example .42.

▼
Write the Schwarz gap as \(F(A)\). One checks that \(F(A + \lambda \mathbb {1}) = F(A)\) for every scalar \(\lambda \), so it is enough to treat the case \(\tr (A) = 0\). In that case a direct \(2 \times 2\) computation shows that \(F(A)\) dominates \(\frac{1}{2} (A^\dagger A)^T\), hence is positive semidefinite.
▼

Theorem .45 The transpose-trace map is not completely positive

wolfExample53_not_cp def:wolf_example53, def:cp_map The map from Example .42 is not completely positive.

▼
Compute the Choi matrix of \(T\) and test it on the antisymmetric vector \(|01\rangle - |10\rangle \). The resulting expectation value is negative, so the Choi matrix is not positive semidefinite.
