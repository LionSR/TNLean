Matrix Product Vectors

Fix a physical dimension \(d\) and a bond dimension \(D\). This chapter studies the tensors that generate matrix product vector (MPV) families. All tensors here are translation-invariant with periodic boundary conditions (PBC).

Basic definitions

Definition .1 MPS tensor

MPSTensor A (translation-invariant, PBC) MPS tensor with physical dimension \(d\) and bond dimension \(D\) is a collection of matrices

\[ \{ A^i\} _{i=0}^{d-1}, \qquad A^i \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C}), \]

indexed by a physical index \(i \in \{ 0, \ldots , d{-}1\} \). Such a tensor defines an MPV family. Diagrammatically,

The black node denotes the tensor \(A\), the horizontal legs are virtual, and the upper leg is the physical index \(i\).

Definition .2 Word evaluation

MPSTensor.evalWord def:mps_tensor Given a word \(w = (i_1, \ldots , i_L) \in \{ 0,\ldots ,d{-}1\} ^L\), the word evaluation is the matrix product

\[ A^w := A^{i_1} A^{i_2} \cdots A^{i_L} \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C}), \]

with the convention that the empty word evaluates to the identity: \(A^\varnothing = \mathbb {1}_D\). In tensor-network notation,

in which each black node denotes the same local tensor \(A\), the virtual legs remain open, and the physical legs are labelled by the word \((i_1,\ldots ,i_L)\).

Lemma .3 Multiplicativity of word evaluation

MPSTensor.evalWord_append def:eval_word For any words \(w_1, w_2\), \(A^{w_1 \cdot w_2} = A^{w_1} A^{w_2}\).

▼
def:eval_word Immediate from the definition of \(A^w\).
▼

Definition .4 Matrix product vector

MPSTensor.mpv def:eval_word The matrix product vector (MPV) of a tensor \(A\) at system size \(N\) is the vector

\[ |V^{(N)}(A)\rangle = \sum _{i_1, \ldots , i_N = 0}^{d-1} \tr \! (A^{i_1} A^{i_2} \cdots A^{i_N}) |i_1, \ldots , i_N\rangle \in (\mathbb {C}^d)^{\otimes N}. \]

Equivalently, for a configuration \(\sigma = (i_1, \ldots , i_N) \in \{ 0,\ldots ,d{-}1\} ^N\), we write

\[ V^{(N)}(A)_\sigma := \tr \! (A^{i_1} A^{i_2} \cdots A^{i_N}). \]

The coefficient function \(\sigma \mapsto V^{(N)}(A)_\sigma \) gives the components; the displayed ket is the corresponding vector in \((\mathbb {C}^d)^{\otimes N}\). For a general word \(w\), we also write

\[ c_w(A) := \tr (A^w). \]

The MPV family generated by \(A\) is the collection \(\mathcal{V}(A) = \bigl\{ |V^{(N)}(A)\rangle \bigr\} _{N \ge 1}\). The coefficient \(V^{(N)}(A)_\sigma \) is the periodic contraction

of \(N\) copies of the local tensor \(A\), with the outer virtual legs closed by the trace.

Definition .5 Transfer map

MPSTensor.transferMap def:mps_tensor The transfer map associated to a tensor \(A\) is the linear map \(\mathcal{E}_A : M_{D}(\mathbb {C}) \to M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) defined by

\[ \mathcal{E}_A(X) = \sum _{i=0}^{d-1} A^i X (A^i)^\dagger . \]

Diagrammatically, the transfer map is the double-layer contraction

in which the upper node denotes \(A\), the lower node denotes \(A^\dagger \), and the physical index is summed over between them.

Remark .6

The transfer map is automatically positive: if \(X \ge 0\) then \(\mathcal{E}_A(X) \ge 0\), because each summand \(A^i X (A^i)^\dagger \) is positive semidefinite. The abstract positive-map framework appears in Chapter ??.

Site-periodic tensor families

Definition .7 Site-periodic tensor family

MPSTensor.PeriodicMPSTensor For a fixed period \(m\), a site-periodic tensor family is a family of local tensors indexed by \(\{ 0,\ldots ,m{-}1\} \), with local physical dimension \(d\) and bond dimension \(D\).

Definition .8 Periodic same-state and gauge-equivalence relations

MPSTensor.PeriodicMPSTensor.SameState MPSTensor.PeriodicMPSTensor.GaugeEquiv def:periodic_mps_tensor For site-periodic tensors \(A,B\) at fixed period \(m\):

  • equality of the induced site-periodic states;

  • cyclic gauge equivalence.

Lemma .9 Equivalence structures for periodic relations

MPSTensor.PeriodicMPSTensor.instEquivalenceSameState MPSTensor.PeriodicMPSTensor.instEquivalenceGaugeEquiv def:periodic_relations Both periodic relations are equivalence relations (reflexive, symmetric, transitive).

Gauge equivalence and same MPV

Definition .10 Gauge equivalence

MPSTensor.GaugeEquiv def:mps_tensor Two tensors \(A\) and \(B\) of the same bond dimension \(D\) are gauge equivalent if there exists an invertible matrix \(X \in \mathrm{GL}_D(\mathbb {C})\) such that, for every \(i \in \{ 0,\ldots ,d{-}1\} \),

\[ B^i = X A^i X^{-1}. \]

In tensor-network notation,

the black node denotes the tensor named in the surrounding formula, the upper leg is the physical index \(i\), and the two red side nodes denote the gauge matrices acting on the virtual legs.

Definition .11 Same MPV

MPSTensor.SameMPV def:mpv Two tensors \(A\) and \(B\) (of the same bond dimension) generate the same MPV family if \(|V^{(N)}(A)\rangle = |V^{(N)}(B)\rangle \) for every system size \(N\).

Definition .12 Same MPV - different bond dimensions

MPSTensor.SameMPV₂ def:mpv For tensors \(A\) and \(B\) of possibly different bond dimensions \(D_1\) and \(D_2\), we write \(\mathcal{V}(A) = \mathcal{V}(B)\) if \(|V^{(N)}(A)\rangle = |V^{(N)}(B)\rangle \) for every \(N\).

Definition .13 Positive-length same MPV - different bond dimensions

MPSTensor.SameMPV₂Pos def:mpv For tensors \(A\) and \(B\) of possibly different bond dimensions, we write \(\mathcal{V}^+(A) = \mathcal{V}^+(B)\) if \(|V^{(N)}(A)\rangle = |V^{(N)}(B)\rangle \) for every \(N {\gt} 0\).

Lemma .14 Length-zero MPV coefficient

MPSTensor.mpv_zero_length def:mpv For any MPS tensor \(A\) with bond dimension \(D\), the length-zero MPV coefficient is \(D\), the trace of the identity on the bond space.

Lemma .15 Positive-length equality with equal bond dimensions

MPSTensor.SameMPV₂Pos.toSameMPV₂_of_bondDim_eq def:same_mpv2_pos, def:same_mpv2, lem:mpv_zero_length If two tensors have the same positive-length MPV family and the same bond dimension, then they have the same MPV family at every length.

Remark .16

Definition .11 is the same-bond-dimension specialization of Definition .12. We keep both because later results use both the same-dimension and different-dimension forms.

Definition .17 Proportional MPV

MPSTensor.ProportionalMPV₂ def:mpv Two tensors \(A\) and \(B\) (of possibly different bond dimensions) generate weakly proportional MPV families if for every \(N\) there exists \(c_N \in \mathbb {C}\) such that \(|V^{(N)}(A)\rangle = c_N |V^{(N)}(B)\rangle \).

Definition .18 Nonzero proportional MPV

MPSTensor.NonzeroProportionalMPV₂ def:mpv Two tensors \(A\) and \(B\) (of possibly different bond dimensions) generate nonzero proportional MPV families if for every \(N\) there is a nonzero scalar \(c_N \in \mathbb {C}\) such that \(|V^{(N)}(A)\rangle = c_N |V^{(N)}(B)\rangle \). This is the projective reading of the proportionality hypothesis in [ CPGSV16 , Theorem II.1 ] .

Definition .19 Eventually nonzero proportional MPV

MPSTensor.EventuallyNonzeroProportionalMPV₂ def:mpv Two tensors \(A\) and \(B\) are eventually nonzero proportional if, for all sufficiently large \(N\), there is a nonzero scalar \(c_N \in \mathbb {C}\) such that \(|V^{(N)}(A)\rangle = c_N |V^{(N)}(B)\rangle \). This auxiliary form is used with the eventual linear-independence statement Lemma .3.

Lemma .20 Elementary nonzero proportionality rules

MPSTensor.NonzeroProportionalMPV₂.toProportionalMPV₂, MPSTensor.NonzeroProportionalMPV₂.eventually, MPSTensor.NonzeroProportionalMPV₂.symm, MPSTensor.SameMPV₂.toNonzeroProportionalMPV₂ def:proportional_mpv, def:nonzero_proportional_mpv, def:eventually_nonzero_proportional_mpv, def:same_mpv2 Nonzero proportionality implies weak proportionality and eventual nonzero proportionality, is symmetric, and equality of MPV families is the special case with scalar \(1\).

▼
Forgetting the nonvanishing condition gives weak proportionality. The eventual form follows by viewing an all-length statement as an eventual one. Symmetry follows by inverting the scalar \(c_N\) at each length, and equality is the case \(c_N = 1\).
▼

Lemma .21 Scaling of word evaluation

MPSTensor.evalWord_smul def:eval_word Let \(\zeta A\) denote the tensor with matrices \(\{ \zeta A^i\} _{i=0}^{d-1}\). Then for any word \(w\),

\[ (\zeta A)^w = \zeta ^{|w|} A^w, \]

where \(|w|\) is the length of \(w\).

▼
def:eval_word Induction on \(w\): the base case gives \(\zeta ^0 = 1\), and each step picks up one factor of \(\zeta \).
▼

Lemma .22 Scaling of MPV components

MPSTensor.mpv_smul def:mpv, lem:eval_word_smul Let \(\zeta A\) denote the tensor with matrices \(\{ \zeta A^i\} _{i=0}^{d-1}\). Then for every \(N\) and every configuration \(\sigma = (i_1, \ldots , i_N)\),

\[ V^{(N)}(\zeta A)_\sigma = \zeta ^{N} V^{(N)}(A)_\sigma . \]

▼
lem:eval_word_smul Apply Lemma .21 to the word \(w = (i_1, \ldots , i_N)\) of length \(N\), then take the trace; the scalar \(\zeta ^{N}\) pulls out by linearity.
▼

Definition .23 Gauge-phase equivalence

MPSTensor.GaugePhaseEquiv MPSTensor.GaugeEquiv.toGaugePhaseEquiv def:mps_tensor Two tensors \(A\) and \(B\) are gauge-phase equivalent if there exist \(X \in \mathrm{GL}_D(\mathbb {C})\) and \(\zeta \in \mathbb {C}\setminus \{ 0\} \) such that, for every physical index \(i\),

\[ B^i = \zeta X A^i X^{-1}. \]

▼
Gauge equivalence is the special case \(\zeta = 1\).
▼

Remark .24

After spectral-radius normalization one may restrict \(\zeta \) to a unit-modulus phase \(e^{i\phi }\). We allow an arbitrary nonzero scalar because later canonical-form statements keep the block-scaling factors explicit.

Lemma .25 Gauge covariance of word evaluation

MPSTensor.evalWord_gauge def:eval_word If \(B^i = X A^i X^{-1}\) for all \(i\), then for every word \(w\),

\[ B^w = X A^w X^{-1}. \]

▼
def:eval_word Induction on the word length, using \(X X^{-1} = \mathbb {1}\).
▼

Theorem .26 Gauge invariance of MPVs

MPSTensor.GaugeEquiv.sameMPV def:gauge_equiv, def:same_mpv If \(A\) and \(B\) are gauge equivalent, then they generate the same MPV family.

▼
lem:eval_word_gauge Let \(B^i = X A^i X^{-1}\). By Lemma .25, \(B^w = X A^w X^{-1}\) for every word \(w\). Then \(\tr (B^w) = \tr (X A^w X^{-1}) = \tr (A^w)\) by cyclicity of the trace.
▼

Theorem .27 Gauge-phase scaling of MPVs

MPSTensor.mpv_eq_pow_mul_of_gaugePhase def:gauge_phase_equiv, def:mpv If, for every physical index \(i\),

\[ B^i = \zeta X A^i X^{-1}, \]

then for every system size \(N\) and configuration \(\sigma \) one has

\[ V^{(N)}(B)_\sigma = \zeta ^N V^{(N)}(A)_\sigma . \]

▼
lem:eval_word_smul, lem:eval_word_gauge Combine gauge covariance of word evaluation with scalar scaling, then take traces.
▼

Injectivity and normality

Definition .28 Injectivity

MPSTensor.IsInjective def:mps_tensor A tensor \(A\) is injective if its matrices span the full matrix algebra: \(\spn _{\mathbb {C}}\{ A^i : i = 0,\ldots ,d{-}1\} = M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\).

Definition .29 \(L\)-block injectivity

MPSTensor.IsNBlkInjective def:mps_tensor A tensor \(A\) is \(L\)-block injective if \(\spn _{\mathbb {C}}\{ A^{i_1} \cdots A^{i_L} : (i_1,\ldots ,i_L) \in \{ 0,\ldots ,d{-}1\} ^L\} = M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\). An injective tensor is \(1\)-block injective.

Lemma .30 One-site injectivity gives one-block injectivity

MPSTensor.isNBlkInjective_one_of_isInjective def:injective, def:l_blk_injective If the one-site matrices \(\{ A^i\} _{i=0}^{d-1}\) span \(M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\), then the length-one word products span \(M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\), so \(A\) is \(1\)-block injective.

▼
The length-one words are exactly the letters \(i \in \{ 0,\ldots ,d{-}1\} \), and evaluating a length-one word gives the matrix \(A^i\). Thus the length-one word span is the same span as in one-site injectivity.
▼

Definition .31 Normal tensor

MPSTensor.IsNormal def:l_blk_injective A tensor \(A\) is normal [ CPGSV21 ] if there exists a finite \(L_0\) such that \(A\) is \(L_0\)-block injective.

Remark .32

The algebraic characterization of normality in Definition .31 — eventual full matrix span, equivalently eventual block injectivity — is equivalent to the spectral characterization used in [ CPGSV21 , Definition IV.1 ] : after TP normalization, the transfer map \(\mathcal{E}_A\) is a primitive channel, equivalently irreducible with trivial peripheral spectrum. This equivalence is proved in [ SPGWC10 , Proposition 3 ] . In what follows, the directions of the equivalence are supplied by Theorem .68, Theorem .85, and Theorem .22. In particular, every subsequent use of “normal” here refers to this single notion; Definition .24 employs the spectral formulation, but it describes the same class of tensors.

Canonical form

Definition .33 Block-injective canonical form

MPSTensor.CanonicalForm def:mps_tensor, def:injective Here, a block-injective canonical form for a tensor consists of:

  1. a number of blocks \(r \ge 1\),

  2. bond dimensions \(D_1, \ldots , D_r\),

  3. injective tensors \(\{ A_k^i\} _{i=0}^{d-1}\) with \(A_k^i \in M_{D_k}(\mathbb {C})\) for each block \(k \in \{ 1,\ldots ,r\} \),

  4. scaling factors \(\mu _1, \ldots , \mu _r \in \mathbb {C}\).

The associated tensor is the block-diagonal tensor

\[ A^i = \bigoplus _{k=1}^{r} \mu _k A_k^i. \]

This block-injective formulation of the canonical form is used in [ PGVWC07 ] ; see also  [ CPGSV21 ] for the normal canonical form.

Remark .34

Here we use the block-injective canonical form: each block is injective in the sense of Definition .28. This is stronger than the NT-block canonical form of [ CPGSV21 ] , where the blocks are only normal in the spectral sense. Chapter ?? treats the normal canonical form needed for the blocking-based multi-block theory, while Chapter ?? develops the separate periodic irreducible-form extension. This section isolates the stronger block-injective form used in the injective and block-injective arguments.

Definition .35 Block projection

Matrix.blockProjection For a finite direct sum \(\bigoplus _k \mathbb {C}^{D_k}\), the block projection \(P_j\) is the matrix that is the identity on the \(j\)-th summand and zero on all other summands.

Definition .36 Block-diagonal matrix

Matrix.IsBlockDiagonal’ A matrix on \(\bigoplus _k \mathbb {C}^{D_k}\) is block diagonal if it is a direct sum of square matrices, one on each summand.

Lemma .37 Off-block characterization

Matrix.isBlockDiagonal’_iff_offBlock_zero def:block_diagonal_matrix A matrix on \(\bigoplus _k \mathbb {C}^{D_k}\) is block diagonal if and only if every entry from one summand to a distinct summand is zero.

▼
The forward direction is immediate from the definition of a direct sum of matrices. Conversely, if all off-block entries vanish, the diagonal blocks reconstruct the original matrix.
▼

Lemma .38 Commutation with block projections

Matrix.isBlockDiagonal’_of_commutes_blockProjection def:block_projection, def:block_diagonal_matrix, lem:block_diagonal_iff_off_block_zero If a matrix on \(\bigoplus _k \mathbb {C}^{D_k}\) commutes with every block projection \(P_j\), then it is block diagonal.

▼
lem:block_diagonal_iff_off_block_zero Compare the \((i,j)\) block of the identity \(XP_j=P_jX\) for \(i\ne j\). The right multiplication by \(P_j\) keeps that block, while the left multiplication by \(P_j\) kills it. Hence every off-block entry is zero, and Lemma .37 applies.
▼

Definition .39 Block-diagonal tensor from blocks

MPSTensor.toTensorFromBlocks def:mps_tensor Given \(r\) blocks with bond dimensions \((D_k)_{k=1}^r\), per-block tensors \(\{ A_k^i\} \), and scaling factors \((\mu _k)_{k=1}^r\), the associated block-diagonal tensor is the tensor of total bond dimension \(D = \sum _{k=1}^r D_k\) defined by

\[ A^i = \bigoplus _{k=1}^{r} \mu _k A_k^i. \]

Lemma .40 Word evaluation of a block-diagonal tensor

MPSTensor.evalWord_toTensorFromBlocks_eq_reindex_blockDiagonal def:block_diagonal_tensor, def:eval_word For a word \(w\), the word evaluation of a block-diagonal tensor remains block diagonal:

\[ A^w = \bigoplus _k \mu _k^{|w|} A_k^w. \]

▼
Induct on the word and multiply block-diagonal matrices block by block; every letter contributes one scalar factor \(\mu _k\) on the \(k\)-th block.
▼

Theorem .41 MPV decomposition

MPSTensor.mpv_toTensorFromBlocks_eq_sum def:block_diagonal_tensor, def:mpv, lem:eval_word_block_diagonal_tensor The MPV of a block-diagonal tensor decomposes as

\[ |V^{(N)}(A)\rangle = \sum _{k=1}^{r} \mu _k^N |V^{(N)}(A_k)\rangle . \]

Equivalently, for each configuration \(\sigma \) one has

\[ V^{(N)}(A)_\sigma = \sum _{k=1}^{r} \mu _k^N V^{(N)}(A_k)_\sigma . \]

▼
def:block_diagonal_tensor, def:mpv, lem:eval_word_block_diagonal_tensor By Lemma .40, the full word evaluation is block diagonal with blocks \(\mu _k^N A_k^w\) for a word \(w\) of length \(N\). Taking the trace of a block-diagonal matrix gives the sum of the block traces: \(\tr (A^w) = \sum _k \mu _k^N \tr (A_k^w)\).
▼

Blocking

Definition .42 Blocked tensor

MPSTensor.blockTensor def:mps_tensor Given a tensor \(A\) and a blocking length \(L\), the \(L\)-blocked tensor is the tensor with physical index set \(\{ 0,\ldots ,d{-}1\} ^L\) (hence physical dimension \(d^L\)) and the same bond dimension \(D\), whose matrices are indexed by words \((i_1, \ldots , i_L) \in \{ 0,\ldots ,d{-}1\} ^L\):

\[ (A^{[L]})^{(i_1, \ldots , i_L)} = A^{i_1} A^{i_2} \cdots A^{i_L}. \]

Lemma .43 Blocked word evaluation

MPSTensor.evalWord_blockTensor def:blocked_tensor, def:eval_word If \(w = (J_1, \ldots , J_m)\) is a word in the blocked alphabet, where each \(J_k\) is an \(L\)-tuple in \(\{ 0, \ldots , d{-}1\} ^L\), then concatenating the block words gives a word \(\widetilde{w}\) of length \(mL\) in the original alphabet and one has

\[ (A^{[L]})^w = A^{\widetilde{w}}. \]

▼
lem:eval_word_append Induct on the blocked word \(w\) and split off the first block word. Lemma .3 turns concatenation of blocks into multiplication of the corresponding evaluations.
▼

Lemma .44 Blocking preserves MPV coefficients

MPSTensor.mpv_blockTensor_eq_mpv def:blocked_tensor, def:mpv For each basis state \(\sigma = (\sigma _1, \ldots , \sigma _N)\) of the blocked system, where each \(\sigma _k\) is an \(L\)-tuple in \(\{ 0, \ldots , d{-}1\} ^L\), there exists a basis state \(\widetilde{\sigma } = (\widetilde{\sigma }_1, \ldots , \widetilde{\sigma }_{NL})\) of the original system such that

\[ V^{(N)}(A^{[L]})_\sigma = V^{(NL)}(A)_{\widetilde{\sigma }}. \]

▼
def:blocked_tensor, def:mpv, lem:eval_word_block Concatenate the block words and apply the blocked word evaluation lemma (Lemma .43).
▼

Theorem .45 Blocking preserves same MPV

MPSTensor.SameMPV.blockTensor def:same_mpv, def:blocked_tensor If \(A\) and \(B\) generate the same MPV family, then for any blocking length \(L\), the blocked tensors \(A^{[L]}\) and \(B^{[L]}\) also generate the same MPV family.

▼
lem:mpv_block By Lemma .44, each MPV coefficient of the blocked tensor \(A^{[L]}\) equals a coefficient of \(A\) at the corresponding concatenated configuration. The same holds for \(B^{[L]}\) (with the same concatenated configuration). Since \(A\) and \(B\) agree on all configurations, so do \(A^{[L]}\) and \(B^{[L]}\).
▼

MPV overlap

Definition .46 MPV as Hilbert-space vector

MPSTensor.mpvState def:mpv We regard \(|V^{(N)}(A)\rangle \) as an element of \(\mathbb {C}^{d^N}\) indexed by configurations \(\sigma \in \{ 0,\ldots ,d{-}1\} ^N\), with \(\sigma \)-component \(V^{(N)}(A)_\sigma \) as in Definition .4. This allows us to use the standard Euclidean inner product on \(\mathbb {C}^{d^N}\).

Definition .47 MPV inner product

MPSTensor.mpvInner def:mpv_state The MPV inner product between two tensors \(A\) and \(B\) at system size \(N\) is

\[ \langle V^{(N)}(A) | V^{(N)}(B) \rangle := \sum _{\sigma \in \{ 0,\ldots ,d{-}1\} ^N} \overline{V^{(N)}(A)_\sigma } V^{(N)}(B)_\sigma , \]

i.e. the Euclidean inner product on \(\mathbb {C}^{d^N}\) (conjugate-linear in the first argument).

Definition .48 MPV overlap

MPSTensor.mpvOverlap def:mpv The MPV overlap between two tensors \(A\) (of bond dimension \(D_1\)) and \(B\) (of bond dimension \(D_2\)) at system size \(N\) is

\[ O_{AB}(N) := \sum _{\sigma \in \{ 0,\ldots ,d{-}1\} ^N} V^{(N)}(A)_\sigma \overline{V^{(N)}(B)_\sigma }. \]

This is the bilinear overlap (without complex conjugation) used later. By Lemma .49, it is the complex conjugate of the Hilbert-space inner product from Definition .47.

Lemma .49 Overlap equals conjugate inner product

MPSTensor.mpvOverlap_eq_star_mpvInner def:mpv_overlap, def:mpv_inner \(O_{AB}(N) = \overline{\langle V^{(N)}(A) | V^{(N)}(B) \rangle }\).

▼
def:mpv_overlap, def:mpv_inner The overlap sums \(V_\sigma \overline{W_\sigma }\) while the inner product sums \(\overline{V_\sigma } W_\sigma \). Complex conjugation swaps these.
▼

Lemma .50 Overlap determined by positive-length MPV

MPSTensor.mpvOverlap_eq_of_pos_mpv_eqdef:mpv_overlap If \(V^{(N)}(A)_\sigma = V^{(N)}(A')_\sigma \) and \(V^{(N)}(B)_\sigma = V^{(N)}(B')_\sigma \) for all \(N {\gt} 0\) and all \(\sigma \), then \(O_{AB}(N) = O_{A'B'}(N)\) for all positive \(N\).

▼
def:mpv_overlap Direct pointwise rewriting: if the MPV coefficients agree for all positive chain lengths, the overlap sums are identical.
▼
