Exponential Decay of Correlations

This chapter derives the transfer-matrix expression for thermodynamic-limit correlators of a normal MPS and the resulting exponential decay estimate. The key mechanism is spectral: after removing the leading eigenvalue contribution, the connected part is governed by subleading eigenvalues of the transfer map.

When the MPS tensor generates a parent Hamiltonian (Chapter ??), the correlator quantifies spatial correlations in the ground state. The exponential decay rate is set by the spectral properties of the transfer map, i.e. by the modulus of its subleading eigenvalues.

The spectral analysis of the transfer map and the mixed transfer operator, including the eigenvalue bound \(\rho (F_{AB}) \le 1\), the strict transfer-operator gap \(\rho (F_{AB}) {\lt} 1\) for non-equivalent blocks, and the resulting overlap decay, is developed in Chapter ??. This chapter focuses on the correlator side: one-point and two-point expectations, their spectral decomposition, and quantitative bounds on decay rates.

Connected correlators from the transfer map

Definition .1 One-point expectation

MPSTensor.onePointExpectation def:transfer_map, thm:qpf Fix an MPS tensor and a right fixed point of its transfer map. Write the tensor as \(A\) and the fixed point as \(\rho ^R\). Under the hypotheses of Theorem .11, that fixed point exists and is unique up to scaling; we normalize it so that \(\tr (\rho ^R)=1\). For a local observable \(X \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) define

\[ \langle X_0 \rangle := \tr (X\rho ^R). \]

Definition .2 Two-point expectation at distance \(n\)

MPSTensor.twoPointExpectation def:transfer_map, def:one_point_expectation For observables \(X,Y \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) and \(n\ge 0\),

\[ \langle X_0 Y_n \rangle := \tr \! \left(Y\, \mathcal{E}_A^n(X\rho ^R)\right). \]

Equivalently: insert \(X\) at site \(0\), propagate by \(\mathcal{E}_A^n\), then insert \(Y\) and take the trace.

Definition .3 Connected correlator

MPSTensor.connectedCorrelator def:two_point_expectation, def:one_point_expectation The connected two-point function is

\[ C(X,Y;n) := \langle X_0Y_n\rangle - \langle X_0\rangle \langle Y_0\rangle . \]

Spectral expansion and exponential decay

Theorem .4 Sum of exponentials (spectral expansion interface)

MPSTensor.connectedCorrelator_eq_sum def:connected_correlator If coefficients \(c_j\in \mathbb {C}\) and eigenvalues \(\lambda _j\in \mathbb {C}\) (\(j=1,\dots ,D^2-1\)) satisfy, for all \(n\ge 0\),

\[ C(X,Y;n) = \sum _{j=1}^{D^2-1} c_j\, \lambda _j^n, \]

then the connected correlator equals the stated sum of exponentials. The source  [ CPGSV21 , Sec. 2.3 ] states that the connected correlator is a sum of \(D^2-1\) pure exponentials \(C(X,Y;n) = \sum _{j\ge 2}^{D^2} c_{XY}(j)\lambda _j^n\), which always exists for a normal MPS via the transfer-map spectral decomposition.

▼
Given the expansion hypothesis, the equality is immediate. The source  [ CPGSV21 , Sec. 2.3 ] derives this expansion from the spectral decomposition of \(\mathcal{E}_A^n\) restricted to the complement of the fixed-point eigenspace: the leading eigenvalue \(\lambda _1=1\) contributes \(\langle X_0\rangle \langle Y_0\rangle \), which is subtracted in the connected correlator, leaving the sum over subleading eigenvalues.
▼

Theorem .5 Exponential decay bound (interface)

MPSTensor.connectedCorrelator_bound def:connected_correlator If a constant \(C_{XY}\in \mathbb {R}\) and \(\lambda _2\in \mathbb {C}\) satisfy, for all \(n\ge 0\),

\[ |C(X,Y;n)| \le C_{XY}\, |\lambda _2|^n, \]

then the connected correlator satisfies that exponential decay bound. The source  [ CPGSV21 , Sec. 2.3 ] obtains this by combining the sum-of-exponentials expansion with the triangle inequality, and Sec. 4 provides the transfer-map gap condition \(|\lambda _j|{\lt}1\) for the subleading eigenvalues.

▼
Given the bound hypothesis, the estimate is immediate. The source  [ CPGSV21 , Sec. 2.3 ] obtains this bound by applying the triangle inequality to the sum-of-exponentials expansion and using the transfer-map gap condition \(|\lambda _j|\le |\lambda _2|{\lt}1\) for all subleading eigenvalues.
▼

Remark .6 Transfer-map spectral hypothesis

thm:correlator_exponential_bound, thm:primitive_compl_lt_one, thm:transfer_operator_gap_irr_tp, thm:transfer_map_gap_wielandt The hypothesis “\(|\lambda _2| {\lt} 1\)” in Theorem .5 is a transfer-map gap condition: all non-leading eigenvalues of \(\mathcal{E}_A\) lie strictly inside the unit disk. It is distinct from the Hamiltonian spectral gap of Chapter ??. The complementary transfer-map gap \(\rho (\mathcal{E}_A - P) {\lt} 1\) is established for primitive channels (Theorem .51, Chapter ??). The mixed-transfer gap needed for block separation is supplied for irreducible trace-preserving tensors (Theorem .29), and quantitative single-tensor transfer-map bounds for injective tensors are recorded in Theorem .11. Thus the analytical input needed to make Theorems .4 and .5 unconditional is already present; what remains is extracting the spectral expansion coefficients \(c_j,\lambda _j\) from the eigendecomposition of \(\mathcal{E}_A\) (Issue #1447).

Definition .7 Correlation length

MPSTensor.correlationLength def:transfer_map The correlation length associated with a subleading eigenvalue \(\lambda _2\) of the transfer map (\(|\lambda _2|{\lt}1\)) is

\[ \xi := -\frac{1}{\log |\lambda _2|}. \]

Lemma .8 Correlation length is positive

MPSTensor.correlationLength_pos def:correlation_length If \(|\lambda _2| {\lt} 1\) and \(\lambda _2 \ne 0\), then \(\xi {\gt} 0\).

▼
Since \(0 {\lt} |\lambda _2| {\lt} 1\), we have \(\log |\lambda _2| {\lt} 0\), so \(\xi = -1/\log |\lambda _2| {\gt} 0\).
▼

Quantitative transfer-map gap bounds

The transfer-map gap theorems in Chapter ?? establish \(\rho (\mathcal{E}_A - P) {\lt} 1\) for primitive channels qualitatively. This section gives the quantitative refinements with explicit constants and rates for the single-tensor transfer map \(\mathcal{E}_A\).

Theorem .9 Exponential convergence of injective primitive channels

MPSTensor.exponential_convergence_of_primitive thm:primitive_compl_lt_one, def:fixed_point_proj, thm:primitive_overlap Fix an injective, trace-preserving MPS tensor \(A\) with positive definite fixed point \(\rho \). Let

\[ P(X) = \frac{\tr (X)}{\tr (\rho )}\rho \]

be the corresponding fixed-point projection. Then there exist \(C {\gt} 0\) and \(0 {\lt} \delta \le 1\) such that for all \(n \ge 0\) and \(X \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\),

\[ \| \mathcal{E}_A^n(X) - P(X)\| \; \le \; C\, (1-\delta )^n\, \| X\| . \]

The transfer-map gap \(\delta \) exists by primitivity (Theorem .51, the complementary transfer-map gap).

▼
Injectivity implies primitivity of the transfer map. The complementary spectral radius \(\rho (\mathcal{E}_A - P) {\lt} 1\) then follows from the primitive transfer-map gap theorem for the complementary map. The Gelfand formula yields a geometric bound \(\| \, (\mathcal{E}_A - P)^n\| \le C\, (1-\delta )^n\) for suitable constants, giving the claimed estimate.
▼

Theorem .10 Correlation length bound

MPSTensor.correlation_length_bound def:correlation_length, thm:exponential_convergence_primitive, def:injective For an injective trace-preserving MPS tensor, there exist \(C {\gt} 0\) and \(\xi {\gt} 0\) such that for all \(n \ge 0\) and all traceless \(X \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) (i.e. \(\tr (X) = 0\)),

\[ \| \mathcal{E}_A^n(X)\| \; \le \; C\, e^{-n/\xi }\, \| X\| . \]

Traceless matrices lie in \(\ker P\), where \(P\) is the fixed-point projection. Since \(\mathcal{E}_A - P\) has spectral radius strictly less than \(1\), the Gelfand formula gives exponential decay at rate \(\xi = -1/\log \rho (\mathcal{E}_A - P)\).

▼
For traceless \(X\) we have \(P(X) = 0\), so \(\mathcal{E}_A^n(X) = (\mathcal{E}_A - P)^n(X)\). The exponential convergence theorem gives a geometric bound \(C\, (1-\delta )^n\, \| X\| \). Rewriting \((1-\delta )^n = e^{-n/\xi }\) with \(\xi = -1/\log (1-\delta )\) yields the claimed estimate.
▼

Theorem .11 Transfer-map gap from injectivity

MPSTensor.transfer_map_gap_of_injective thm:transfer_operator_gap For an injective trace-preserving MPS tensor, there exists \(\delta {\gt} 0\) such that every eigenvalue \(\mu \neq 1\) of the transfer map satisfies \(|\mu | \le 1 - \delta \).

▼
Injectivity implies eventual full Kraus rank. This yields primitivity, hence a complementary transfer-map gap. Since the transfer map has only finitely many eigenvalues, one may choose a uniform \(\delta {\gt} 0\) separating \(1\) from the remaining spectrum.
▼

Renormalization fixed points and zero correlation length

Definition .13 Renormalization fixed point

MPSTensor.IsRFP def:transfer_map An MPS tensor \(A\) is a renormalization fixed point (RFP) when its transfer map is idempotent, \(\mathcal{E}_A \circ \mathcal{E}_A = \mathcal{E}_A\). See [ CPGSV21 , Definition 3.2 ] .

Theorem .14 Kraus-isometry criterion for RFP

MPSTensor.isRFP_of_kraus_isometry def:is_rfp Suppose there exists an isometry \(V : \mathbb {C}^d \to \mathbb {C}^{d^2}\), written as coefficients \(V_{(i_1,i_2),j}\), such that

\[ A^{i_1} A^{i_2} = \sum _{j=0}^{d-1} V_{(i_1,i_2),j} A^j \]

for all \(i_1,i_2 \in \{ 0,\ldots ,d-1\} \). Then \(A\) is a renormalization fixed point. This is the backward direction of [ CPGSV21 , Theorem 3.1 ] .

▼
Expanding \(\mathcal{E}_A^2\) gives a double Kraus sum indexed by pairs \((i_1,i_2)\). Substituting the decomposition above and using the isometry relation \(V^\dagger V = 1\) collapses the double sum to the single Kraus family \(\{ A^j\} \), hence \(\mathcal{E}_A^2 = \mathcal{E}_A\).
▼

Definition .15 Local orthogonality

MPSTensor.IsLocallyOrthogonal def:is_rfp A single BNT block is locally orthogonal when its self-transfer map is idempotent. For a single tensor this coincides with the RFP condition (Definition .13); in the full multi-block BNT setting, local orthogonality additionally requires that mixed transfer operators vanish. See [ CPGSV21 , Definition 3.5 ] .

Theorem .16 RFP normal tensor is injective

MPSTensor.rfp_nt_structural def:is_rfp, def:normal, def:injective If \(A\) is a nonzero normal RFP tensor, then \(A\) is injective.

▼
The RFP condition \(\mathcal{E}_A^2 = \mathcal{E}_A\) implies that the cumulative span \(\spn \{ A^w : |w| \le n\} \) stabilises after one step. Since \(A\) is normal (hence has an injective blocked form), the one-step span already equals \(M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\), so \(A\) is injective.
▼

Theorem .17 Left-canonical normal RFP tensor is injective

MPSTensor.rfp_nt_structural_of_leftCanonical def:is_rfp, def:normal, def:injective Assume \(D {\gt} 0\). Let \(A\) be a normal renormalization fixed point such that

\[ \sum _i (A^i)^\dagger A^i = \mathbb {1}. \]

Then \(A\) is injective. This is the left-canonical injectivity step in [ CPGSV21 , Appendix B ] .

▼
thm:rfp_nt_injective The normalization \(\sum _i (A^i)^\dagger A^i = \mathbb {1}\) forces at least one matrix \(A^i\) to be nonzero, since otherwise the left-hand side would vanish. Hence \(A\) is a nonzero normal renormalization fixed point, and Theorem .16 applies.
▼

Theorem .18 Unitary diagonal fixed point for a left-canonical normal RFP

MPSTensor.rfp_nt_cfii_diagonal_fixedPoint def:is_rfp, def:normal, def:transfer_map Assume \(D {\gt} 0\). Let \(A\) be a normal renormalization fixed point such that

\[ \sum _i (A^i)^\dagger A^i = \mathbb {1}. \]

Then there exist a unitary \(U\) and a diagonal positive definite matrix \(\Lambda \) such that, for \(B^i := U^\dagger A^i U\),

\[ \sum _i (B^i)^\dagger B^i = \mathbb {1}\qquad \text{and}\qquad \mathcal{E}_B(\Lambda ) = \Lambda . \]

This is the diagonal fixed-point reduction in [ CPGSV21 , Appendix B ] .

▼
thm:rfp_nt_structural_of_left_canonical, thm:injective_irreducible, thm:form_II By Theorem .17, the tensor \(A\) is injective. Therefore its transfer map is irreducible, and hence \(A\) is irreducible as a tensor. Applying Theorem .19 gives a unitary conjugate \(B\) for which the trace-preserving normalization remains valid and a diagonal positive definite matrix \(\Lambda \) with \(\mathcal{E}_B(\Lambda ) = \Lambda \).
▼

Theorem .19 Rank-one classification for RFP transfer maps

MPSTensor.transferMap_eq_fixedPointProj_of_isRFP_injective def:is_rfp, def:injective, def:fixed_point_proj, thm:exponential_convergence_primitive Let \(A\) be an injective left-canonical RFP tensor with positive-definite fixed point \(\rho \) of the transfer map \(\mathcal{E}_A\). Then \(\mathcal{E}_A = P_\rho \), where \(P_\rho (X) = \frac{\tr (X)}{\tr (\rho )}\, \rho \) is the rank-one fixed-point projection.

▼
thm:exponential_convergence_primitive The exponential convergence bound gives \(\lVert \mathcal{E}_A^n(X) - P_\rho (X)\rVert \le C(1-\delta )^n\lVert X\rVert \). Idempotence of \(\mathcal{E}_A\) implies \(\mathcal{E}_A^{n+1} = \mathcal{E}_A\) for all \(n \ge 0\), so \(\lVert \mathcal{E}_A(X) - P_\rho (X)\rVert \le C(1-\delta )^{n+1}\lVert X\rVert \) for all \(n\). Taking \(n \to \infty \) gives \(\mathcal{E}_A = P_\rho \).
▼

Theorem .20 Full structural form for RFP tensors

MPSTensor.rfp_nt_structural_full thm:rfp_nt_structural_of_left_canonical, thm:rfp_nt_cfii_diagonal_fixed_point, thm:transferMap_eq_fixedPointProj_of_isRFP_injective A normal tensor \(A\) in canonical form II that is a renormalization fixed point admits a decomposition \(A^i = X \Lambda U^i X^{-1}\), where \(\Lambda \) is diagonal positive and the family \(U = (U^i)\) is an isometry in the physical indices.

▼
Combine the diagonal fixed-point reduction with the rank-one classification of injective left-canonical RFP tensors. One then writes the resulting rank-one projector in a canonical Kraus form, applies Kraus freedom to identify the physical-index family with an isometry \(U\), and transports the decomposition back through the conjugation.
▼

Theorem .21 Canonical-form blocks are injective

MPSTensor.rfp_cf_structural def:is_canonical_form, def:injective For a family of tensors in canonical form, every block is injective. This is a direct projection of the injectivity field of the canonical-form predicate; see [ CPGSV16 , Section 2.3 ] .

▼
Immediate from the block_injective field of IsCanonicalForm.
▼

Theorem .22 BNT basis blocks are injective

MPSTensor.rfp_bnt_structural def:bnt, def:injective Let \(P\) be a sector decomposition in BNT canonical form. Then every basis block \(P_j\) is injective as an MPS tensor. This is the basis-block injectivity part of [ CPGSV16 , Section III ] (thm:charact-MPS, lines 543–555; basis-block normality established at lines 271–301 and 317–344).

▼
thm:rfp_cf_structural Direct projection of the basis_injective field of IsBNTCanonicalForm. The result holds for arbitrary copy multiplicities, since the statement concerns only BNT basis blocks and not copy weights.
▼

Theorem .23 RG flow convergence for canonical-form tensors

MPSTensor.rg_flow_converges_of_cf def:is_canonical_form, def:transfer_map, def:fixed_point_proj, thm:exponential_convergence_primitive For a tensor in canonical form with blocks \((A_k)\) and weights \((\mu _k)\), the iterated blocking \(\mathcal{E}_{A_k}^{2^n}\) converges pointwise to an idempotent linear map \(\mathcal{E}_\infty \) as \(n\to \infty \). That is, there exists \(\mathcal{E}_\infty \) with \(\mathcal{E}_\infty ^2 = \mathcal{E}_\infty \) such that \(\mathcal{E}_{A_k}^{2^n}(\rho ) \to \mathcal{E}_\infty (\rho )\) for all density matrices \(\rho \). This is [ CPGSV21 , Appendix B ] .

▼
Each block is injective and left-canonical, so its transfer map is a primitive channel with a unique positive-definite fixed point \(\rho _0\). The exponential convergence bound \(\lVert \mathcal{E}^n(X) - P(X)\rVert \le C(1-\delta )^n \lVert X\rVert \) (Theorem .9) then gives pointwise convergence \(\mathcal{E}^n(X)\to P(X)\), and composing with the subsequence \(2^n\to \infty \) yields the result. The witness \(\mathcal{E}_\infty = P\) is the rank-one fixed-point projection, which is idempotent.
▼

Remark .24

thm:spectral_radius_le_one, def:correlation_length, def:is_rfp When the transfer map is idempotent (\(\mathcal{E}^2=\mathcal{E}\)), connected correlations become separation-independent for all separations \(n \ge 1\). Informally, this corresponds to zero correlation length (\(\xi =0\)). The full spectral equivalence (idempotence iff vanishing subleading spectrum) is deferred here.

Definition .25 Correlations independent of distance

MPSTensor.IsCID def:connected_correlator For a positive definite right fixed point \(\rho ^R {\gt} 0\), an MPS tensor has correlations independent of distance (CID) when for all local observables \(X\) and \(Y\) and all separations \(n,m \ge 1\),

\[ C(X,Y;n) = C(X,Y;m). \]

Definition .26 Zero correlation length

MPSTensor.IsZCL def:is_locally_orthogonal, def:is_cid An MPS tensor has zero correlation length when it is both locally orthogonal and correlations are independent of distance. See [ CPGSV21 , Definition 3.6 ] .

Theorem .27 CID implies RFP

MPSTensor.isCID_implies_isRFP def:is_cid, def:is_rfp, def:transfer_map If an MPS tensor admits a positive definite right fixed point of its transfer map and has correlations independent of distance, then its transfer map is idempotent. This is the reverse direction of [ CPGSV21 , Theorem 3.8 ] .

▼
Trace nondegeneracy and invertibility of the fixed point reduce idempotence to equality of the connected correlator at separations \(n=2\) and \(n=1\).
▼

Theorem .28 Zero correlation length iff idempotent transfer

MPSTensor.zcl_iff_idempotent_transfer def:is_rfp, def:is_cid, def:is_locally_orthogonal An MPS tensor has zero correlation length (i.e. is both locally orthogonal and CID) if and only if its transfer map is idempotent. This is [ CPGSV21 , Theorem 3.8 ] .

▼
The forward direction extracts local orthogonality (which equals idempotence by definition). The reverse uses \(\mathcal{E}^n = \mathcal{E}\) for \(n \ge 1\) to show the connected correlator is separation-independent.
▼

Theorem .29 Canonical-form blocks satisfy RFP iff ZCL

MPSTensor.rfp_iff_zcl def:is_canonical_form, def:is_rfp, def:is_zcl For a tensor in canonical form, each block is a renormalization fixed point if and only if it has zero correlation length. This is the RFP–ZCL part of [ CPGSV21 , Theorem 3.10 ] .
