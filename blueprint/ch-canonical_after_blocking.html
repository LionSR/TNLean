13 Canonical-form data after blocking

This chapter collects the canonical-form bridge data consumed by Chapter 12 after the initial blocking and zero-tail reductions. The statements form an interface between the canonical-form chapter and the Fundamental-Theorem comparison: they package trace-preserving gauges, primitive irreducible sector data, common blocked alphabets, and zero-tail bookkeeping.

Conceptually, this material belongs to the blocking part of the canonical-form construction in [ CPGSV16 , Section 2.3 ] and to the normal/BNT setup surrounding [ CPGSV21 , Section IV.A ] . The individual blueprint statements below are implementation bridge lemmas rather than paper-numbered theorems.

13.1 Reduction and structural after-blocking results

Theorem 13.1 Blockwise TP-gauge normalization of irreducible blocks
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 9.15

Lean declarations

  • MPSTensor.exists_tp_gauge_blockwise

Suppose \(A\) generates the same MPV family as a block-diagonal tensor \(\bigoplus _{k=1}^r B_k\) with each block \(B_k\) irreducible and containing at least one nonzero Kraus operator. Then, after rescaling the sector weights, one obtains blocks \((C_k)\) such that \(A \sim \bigoplus _k \mu _k C_k\), each \(C_k\) is irreducible, each \(C_k\) is left-canonical, all \(\mu _k \neq 0\), and all bond dimensions are positive.

Proof ▶

The Perron–Frobenius TP-gauge construction is applied separately to each irreducible block. Gauge equivalence preserves irreducibility and the MPV family, and the positive scaling factors are absorbed into the new weights.

If \(A\) is left-canonical, irreducible, primitive, and has positive bond dimension, then every positive blocking length \(P\) preserves irreducibility: \(A^{[P]}\) is again irreducible.

Proof ▶

A positive-definite fixed point of \(\mathcal{E}_A\) remains fixed by the blocked transfer map. Primitivity gives uniqueness of positive semidefinite fixed points for the blocked map, hence the blocked transfer map is irreducible; this is exactly the irreducibility criterion for the blocked tensor.
