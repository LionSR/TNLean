Transfer-Operator Gaps and Block Separation

This chapter proves that the MPV overlaps between distinct (non-gauge-phase-equivalent) MPS blocks decay to zero as system size grows. It also proves the corresponding self-overlap convergence to \(1\) under the complementary transfer-map gap hypothesis that encodes primitivity in the cases of interest. The argument is based on a spectral analysis of the mixed transfer operator, following  [ PGVWC07 ] .

This chapter concerns gaps in the spectrum of transfer operators: \(\rho (F_{AB}) {\lt} 1\) for mixed transfer operators, and \(\rho (\mathcal{E}_A - P) {\lt} 1\) after removing the fixed-point projection. The unqualified phrase spectral gap is reserved for the energy gap of a many-body Hamiltonian in Chapter ??.

The algebraic mixed-transfer identities in the first two sections do not use normalization. Starting with the spectral-radius estimates, we assume the trace-preserving normalization

\[ \sum _i (A^i)^\dagger A^i = \mathbb {1}. \]

Mixed transfer operator

Definition .1 Mixed transfer operator

MPSTensor.mixedTransferMap def:mps_tensor The mixed transfer operator (or cross transfer operator) for two MPS tensors \(A\) and \(B\) of the same bond dimension \(D\) is the linear map \(F_{AB} : M_{D}(\mathbb {C}) \to M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) defined by

\[ F_{AB}(X) = \sum _{i=0}^{d-1} A^i X (B^i)^\dagger . \]

Definition .2 Rectangular mixed transfer operator

MPSTensor.mixedTransferMap₂ def:mps_tensor For MPS tensors \(A\) of bond dimension \(D_1\) and \(B\) of bond dimension \(D_2\) (possibly \(D_1 \neq D_2\)), the rectangular mixed transfer operator is \(F_{AB}^{\mathrm{rect}} : M_{D_1 \times D_2}(\mathbb {C}) \to M_{D_1 \times D_2}(\mathbb {C})\) defined by

\[ F_{AB}^{\mathrm{rect}}(X) = \sum _{i=0}^{d-1} A^i X (B^i)^\dagger . \]

Remark .3 Self-transfer

MPSTensor.mixedTransferMap_self def:mixed_transfer, def:transfer_map Setting \(B=A\) recovers the ordinary transfer map: \(F_{AA} = \mathcal{E}_A\).

Theorem .4 Iterated mixed transfer

MPSTensor.mixedTransferMap_pow_apply def:mixed_transfer, def:eval_word For all \(X \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) and \(N \ge 0\),

\[ F_{AB}^N(X) = \sum _{\sigma \in \{ 0,\ldots ,d{-}1\} ^N} A^\sigma X (B^\sigma )^\dagger , \]

where \(A^\sigma = A^{\sigma _1} \cdots A^{\sigma _N}\) denotes the word evaluation (Definition .2).

▼
Induction on \(N\): the base case is \(F^0(X) = X\); the inductive step applies \(F_{AB}\) to each summand and re-indexes via \(\{ 0,\ldots ,d{-}1\} ^{N+1} \cong \{ 0,\ldots ,d{-}1\} \times \{ 0,\ldots ,d{-}1\} ^N\).
▼

Theorem .5 Matrix trace of iterated mixed transfer at the identity

MPSTensor.trace_mixedTransferMap_pow_identity def:mixed_transfer, def:eval_word For \(N \ge 0\),

\[ \tr (F_{AB}^N(\mathbb {1})) = \sum _{\sigma } \tr (A^\sigma (B^\sigma )^\dagger ). \]

This is the matrix trace of \(F_{AB}^N\) evaluated at the identity matrix. It should not be confused with the operator trace \(\Tr (F_{AB}^N)\); its relation to the MPV overlap is proved in the next section.

▼
thm:mixed_pow Apply the matrix trace to each summand in Theorem .4 with \(X = \mathbb {1}\): \(\tr (F_{AB}^N(\mathbb {1})) = \sum _\sigma \tr (A^\sigma \cdot \mathbb {1}\cdot (B^\sigma )^\dagger ) = \sum _\sigma \tr (A^\sigma (B^\sigma )^\dagger )\).
▼

MPV overlap as transfer trace

Recall from Definition .48 that

\[ O_{AB}(N) = \sum _{\sigma \in \{ 0,\ldots ,d{-}1\} ^N} V^{(N)}(A)_\sigma \overline{V^{(N)}(B)_\sigma }. \]

This section rewrites that scalar as the operator trace of the mixed transfer power.

Lemma .6 Trace expansion over matrix units

MPSTensor.linearMap_trace_eq_sum_apply_single For any linear endomorphism \(T\) on \(M_{D_1 \times D_2}(\mathbb {C})\), the operator trace can be expanded as

\[ \Tr (T) = \sum _{p=0}^{D_1-1} \sum _{q=0}^{D_2-1} (T(E_{pq}))_{pq}, \]

where \(E_{pq} \in M_{D_1 \times D_2}(\mathbb {C})\) is the matrix with \(1\) in position \((p,q)\) and \(0\) elsewhere.

▼
The operator trace \(\Tr (T)\) equals the sum of \(\tr (T(E_{pq}) \cdot E_{qp})\) over all \((p,q)\), and \(\tr (M \cdot E_{qp}) = M_{pq}\).
▼

Theorem .7 Overlap equals transfer trace

MPSTensor.trace_mixedTransferMap_pow_eq_mpvOverlap def:mixed_transfer, def:mpv_overlap For tensors \(A, B\) of bond dimension \(D \ge 1\),

\[ O_{AB}(N) = \Tr (F_{AB}^N). \]

▼
thm:mixed_pow, lem:trace_expansion Expand the operator trace using Lemma .6 as a sum over matrix units \(E_{pq}\). Apply Theorem .4 to evaluate \(F_{AB}^N(E_{pq})\), extract the \((p,q)\)-entry, and reassemble. The resulting double sum over \((p,q)\) and \(\sigma \) equals \(\sum _\sigma V^{(N)}(A)_\sigma \overline{V^{(N)}(B)_\sigma } = O_{AB}(N)\).
▼

Theorem .8 Rectangular overlap as transfer trace

MPSTensor.trace_mixedTransferMap₂_pow_eq_mpvOverlap def:mixed_transfer_rect, def:mpv_overlap For tensors \(A\) of bond dimension \(D_1 \ge 1\) and \(B\) of bond dimension \(D_2 \ge 1\),

\[ O_{AB}(N) = \Tr ((F_{AB}^{\mathrm{rect}})^N). \]

▼
thm:overlap_trace Expand the operator trace over the rectangular matrix-unit basis. Then apply the rectangular iterated-transfer formula and sum over the matrix-unit indices exactly as in Theorem .7.
▼

Lemma .9 Word trace as entrywise inner product

MPSTensor.mpv_inner_product_via_trace def:eval_word For a word \(\sigma \in \{ 0,\ldots ,d{-}1\} ^N\),

\[ \tr (A^\sigma (B^\sigma )^\dagger ) = \sum _{j,k = 0}^{D-1} (A^\sigma )_{jk} \overline{(B^\sigma )_{jk}}. \]

Frobenius norm estimates

The transfer-operator gap proofs use the Frobenius (Hilbert–Schmidt) norm as the natural norm for the finite-dimensional matrix algebra. We write \(\| {\cdot }\| _F\) for this norm; it coincides with, and we use it interchangeably with, the Hilbert–Schmidt norm \(\| {\cdot }\| _{\mathrm{HS}}\) on finite-dimensional spaces. We use the squared version and an isometric embedding into Euclidean space.

Definition .10 Frobenius norm squared

MPSTensor.frobSq For a (possibly rectangular) matrix \(X \in M_{m \times n}(\mathbb {C})\), the Frobenius norm squared is

\[ \| X\| _F^2 := \sum _{i=0}^{m-1}\sum _{j=0}^{n-1} |X_{ij}|^2. \]

Lemma .11 Trace formula for Frobenius norm

MPSTensor.frobSq_trace def:frob_sq \(\| X\| _F^2 = \operatorname {Re}\tr (X^\dagger X)\).

▼
Expand the matrix product and trace entry by entry.
▼

Definition .12 Euclidean-space embedding

MPSTensor.matToES The map \(\mathrm{vec} : M_{m \times n}(\mathbb {C}) \to \mathbb {C}^{mn}\) that flattens matrix entries is an isometric embedding with respect to the Frobenius norm: \(\| \mathrm{vec}(X)\| ^2 = \| X\| _F^2\).

Lemma .13 Norm of embedded matrix

MPSTensor.norm_matToES_sq def:mat_to_es, def:frob_sq \(\| \mathrm{vec}(X)\| ^2 = \| X\| _F^2\).

▼
Expand both sides as sums over entries and use \(|z|^2 = \bar{z}z\).

Eigenvalue bound and transfer-operator gap

The eigenvalue bound comes from a uniform Frobenius-norm estimate obtained directly from the trace-preserving normalization (cf.  [ Wol12 , Proposition 6.1 ] for the general operator-norm version via Russo–Dye). For the rigidity theorem, one gauges \(A\) and \(B\) separately using positive fixed points of their transfer maps to obtain unital Kraus families, and then applies the Kadison–Schwarz equality argument ( [ Wol12 , Theorem 5.3 ] ) to a block embedding of a mixed-transfer eigenvector.

Theorem .14 Eigenvalue bound

MPSTensor.eigenvalue_norm_le_one def:mixed_transfer Let \(A, B\) be normalized MPS tensors. Every eigenvalue \(\mu \) of \(F_{AB}\) satisfies \(|\mu | \le 1\).

▼
thm:mixed_pow Expand \(F_{AB}^n(X)\) as a sum over words and use Cauchy–Schwarz, together with \(\sum _i (A^i)^\dagger A^i = \sum _i (B^i)^\dagger B^i = \mathbb {1}\), to obtain a uniform Frobenius-norm bound \(\| F_{AB}^n(X)\| _{\mathrm{HS}} \le C_D \| X\| _{\mathrm{HS}}\) for all \(n\), where \(C_D\) depends only on \(D\). If \(F_{AB}(X) = \mu X\) and \(X \neq 0\), then \(|\mu |^n \| X\| _{\mathrm{HS}} = \| F_{AB}^n(X)\| _{\mathrm{HS}} \le C_D \| X\| _{\mathrm{HS}}\) for every \(n\). Hence \(|\mu | \le 1\).

The argument uses only the trace-preserving normalization and does not appeal to Perron–Frobenius theory.

▼

Theorem .15 Spectral radius bound

MPSTensor.spectralRadius_mixedTransfer_le_one def:mixed_transfer \(\rho (F_{AB}) \le 1\) for normalized tensors.

▼
thm:eigenvalue_bound The spectral radius is the supremum of \(|\mu |\) over all eigenvalues, and each satisfies \(|\mu | \le 1\) by Theorem .14.
▼

Theorem .16 Spectral radius \(\ge 1\) implies gauge-phase equivalence

MPSTensor.modulus_one_eigenvalue_implies_gauge def:mixed_transfer, def:gauge_phase_equiv, def:injective Let \(A, B\) be injective normalized MPS tensors. If \(\rho (F_{AB}) \ge 1\), then \(A\) and \(B\) are gauge-phase equivalent.

▼
thm:spectral_radius_le_one, def:gauge_phase_equiv, thm:qpf, thm:right_canonical_gauge, thm:ks_peripheral, thm:kraus_commute, thm:left_multiplicative_identity, thm:simplicity, thm:skolem_noether Since \(\rho (F_{AB}) \le 1\) (Theorem .15) and \(\rho (F_{AB}) \ge 1\) by hypothesis, there exists an eigenvalue \(\mu \) with \(|\mu | = 1\) and eigenvector \(X \neq 0\): \(F_{AB}(X) = \mu X\). The proof proceeds in five steps.

Step 1 (Separate gauging). Choose positive definite fixed points \(\rho _A, \rho _B\) of the transfer maps \(\mathcal{E}_A, \mathcal{E}_B\) (Theorem .11) and gauge each tensor separately to a unital Kraus family (Theorem .13): set \(A'^i = \rho _A^{-1/2} A^i \rho _A^{1/2}\) and similarly for \(B\). Let \(X' = \rho _A^{-1/2} X \rho _B^{-1/2}\) denote the correspondingly gauged eigenvector.

Step 2 (Block Kraus family and off-diagonal embedding). Form the block Kraus operators \(C^i = \bigl(\begin{smallmatrix} \end{smallmatrix} A'^i & 0 \\ 0 & B'^i\end{smallmatrix}\bigr)\). These constitute a unital Kraus family on \(M_{2D}(\mathbb {C})\). The off-diagonal matrix \(Y = (\begin{smallmatrix} \end{smallmatrix} 0 & X' \\ 0 & 0\end{smallmatrix})\) satisfies \(E_C(Y) = \mu Y\).

Step 3 (KS equality and Kraus commutation). Because \(|\mu | = 1\) and \(E_C\) is unital and trace-preserving, the Kadison–Schwarz equality for peripheral eigenvectors (Theorem .24) gives \(E_C(Y^\dagger Y) = E_C(Y)^\dagger E_C(Y)\). By the Kraus commutation relation (Theorem .23), \(Y\) commutes with every block Kraus operator: \(C^i Y = Y C^i\) for all \(i\).

Step 4 (Intertwining identity and invertibility). Expanding the commutation relation block by block gives \(A'^i X' = X' B'^i\) for every \(i\). Since \(\{ A'^i\} \) span \(M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) (injectivity of \(A\)), \(X'\) is a nonzero intertwiner from \(M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) to \(M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\). The left multiplicative identity (Theorem .25) gives \(E_C(Y^\dagger Y) = E_C(Y^\dagger ) E_C(Y)\), so \(X'^\dagger X'\) is a positive semi-definite fixed point of \(\mathcal{E}_{B'}\), positive definite by injectivity. Hence \(X'\) is invertible.

Step 5 (Skolem–Noether conclusion). The map \(M \mapsto X' M (X')^{-1}\) is a \(\mathbb {C}\)-algebra automorphism of \(M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) sending \(B'^i\) to \(A'^i\). By Skolem–Noether (Theorem .10) this is an inner automorphism. Undoing the separate gauges gives \(B^i = \bar{\mu } X A^i X^{-1}\) for all \(i\), establishing gauge-phase equivalence (Definition .23).

The point is that one gauges the individual transfer maps to unital form; one does not require the mixed transfer map itself to be simultaneously unital and trace-preserving.

▼

Theorem .17 Gauged peripheral eigenvector yields Kraus intertwining

MPSTensor.gauged_intertwining_core def:mixed_transfer, thm:qpf, thm:right_canonical_gauge Let \(A\) and \(B\) be normalized tensors, and let \(X \neq 0\) satisfy \(F_{AB}(X) = \mu X\) with \(|\mu | = 1\). Choose positive-definite fixed points \(\rho _A,\rho _B\) of the transfer maps and gauge to unital families \(A'^i = S_A^{-1} A^i S_A\) and \(B'^i = S_B^{-1} B^i S_B\) with \(S_A S_A^\dagger = \rho _A\) and \(S_B S_B^\dagger = \rho _B\). Then the transported matrix \(X' = S_A^{-1} X (S_B^\dagger )^{-1}\) is nonzero, the families \(A'\) and \(B'\) are unital, and for every \(i\) one has

\[ X' (B'^i)^\dagger = \mu (A'^i)^\dagger X', \qquad A'^i X' = \mu X' B'^i. \]

▼
thm:ks_peripheral, thm:kraus_commute Block-embed the gauged families and transported eigenvector into a single unital Kraus map, apply Kadison–Schwarz equality for the modulus-one eigenvalue, then extract the intertwining identities from the block structure.

Theorem .18 Intertwining yields gauge-phase equivalence

MPSTensor.gaugePhaseEquiv_of_gauged_intertwining def:gauge_phase_equiv Let \(A\) and \(B\) be tensors of the same bond dimension. Suppose there are invertible gauges taking them to families \(A'\) and \(B'\), an invertible matrix \(X'\), and a scalar \(\mu \) with \(|\mu | = 1\) such that, for every physical index \(i\),

\[ A'^i X' = \mu X' B'^i. \]

Then \(A\) and \(B\) are gauge-phase equivalent.

▼
Compose the two gauge matrices with the intertwiner to obtain a single invertible matrix conjugating \(A\) to a scalar multiple of \(B\).
▼

Remark .19 Two gauge conventions

The right-canonical gauge used here, \(A'^i = \rho ^{-1/2} A^i \rho ^{1/2}\), makes \(\{ A'^i\} \) a unital Kraus family (\(\sum _i A'^i (A'^i)^\dagger = \mathbb {1}\)). A different convention, \(\tilde{A}^i = \rho ^{1/2} A^i \rho ^{-1/2}\), makes the family trace-preserving (\(\sum _i (\tilde{A}^i)^\dagger \tilde{A}^i = \mathbb {1}\)). Both conventions appear in practice: the unital version via direct matrix square-root inversion (used in the transfer-operator gap proof above), and the trace-preserving version (used in the canonical-form reduction of Chapter ??).

Theorem .20 Strict transfer-operator gap

MPSTensor.spectralRadius_mixedTransfer_lt_one def:gauge_phase_equiv, def:injective, def:mixed_transfer Let \(A, B\) be injective normalized MPS tensors that are not gauge-phase equivalent. Then \(\rho (F_{AB}) {\lt} 1\).

thm:spectral_radius_le_one, thm:modulus_one_gauge \(\rho (F_{AB}) \le 1\) by Theorem .15. If \(\rho (F_{AB}) = 1\), Theorem .16 gives gauge-phase equivalence, contradicting the hypothesis.

Transfer-operator gap — rectangular case

Theorem .21 Rectangular intertwining forces equal bond dimension

MPSTensor.dim_eq_of_gauged_intertwining def:injective Let \(A\) and \(B\) be injective tensors of bond dimensions \(D_1\) and \(D_2\). If there exist \(X \in M_{D_1 \times D_2}(\mathbb {C}) \setminus \{ 0\} \) and \(\mu \in \mathbb {C}\) such that, for every physical index \(i\),

\[ X (B^i)^\dagger = \mu (A^i)^\dagger X, \qquad A^i X = \mu X B^i, \]

then \(D_1 = D_2\).

▼
def:injective The intertwining relation shows that \(\ker (X)\) is invariant under all generators of \(B\). Since \(B\) is injective, its generators span the full matrix algebra, so \(\ker (X)\) is trivial and \(D_2 \le D_1\). Applying the same argument to \(X^\dagger \) gives \(D_1 \le D_2\).
▼

Theorem .22 Rectangular transfer-operator gap

MPSTensor.mixedTransferSpectralRadius₂_lt_one_of_dim_ne def:mixed_transfer_rect, def:injective Let \(A\) be an injective normalized tensor of bond dimension \(D_1\) and \(B\) an injective normalized tensor of bond dimension \(D_2\), with \(D_1 \neq D_2\). Then \(\rho (F_{AB}^{\mathrm{rect}}) {\lt} 1\).

▼

Theorem .45 Complete positivity of the scalar conditional expectation

Kraus.scalarConditionalExpectation_isCPMap def:scalar_conditional_expectation For any positive semidefinite \(\sigma \), the scalar conditional expectation \(E_\sigma \) is a completely positive map.

▼

Wedderburn decomposition of the fixed-point algebra

This section proves the structure theorem for the fixed-point algebra of a trace-preserving Kraus map, following  [ Wol12 , Theorems 6.12–6.14 ] . The proof uses the Jacobson radical characterization of semisimplicity together with the Wedderburn–Artin structure theorem for finite-dimensional semisimple algebras over an algebraically closed field.

Theorem .57 Fixed-point algebra is semisimple

Kraus.fixedPointAlgebra_isSemisimpleRing thm:adjointFixedPointsStarSubalgebra The adjoint-fixed-point \(*\)-subalgebra of a trace-preserving Kraus map on \(M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) is a semisimple ring.

▼
thm:adjointFixedPointsStarSubalgebra The subalgebra is finite-dimensional (as a subalgebra of \(M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\)), hence Artinian. By  [ Wol12 , Theorem 6.14 ] , it suffices to show the Jacobson radical \(J\) vanishes. If \(x \in J\), then \(x^*x \in J\) (left-ideal closure). The radical of an Artinian ring is nilpotent, so \(x^*x\) is nilpotent. A self-adjoint nilpotent matrix satisfies \(\lVert x^*x\rVert ^{2^k} = \lVert (x^*x)^{2^k}\rVert = 0\), hence \(x^*x = 0\), and the C\(^*\)-identity gives \(x = 0\).

Multi-cycle block-permutation structure

This section develops the block-permutation structure underlying the cycle decomposition of  [ Wol12 , Theorem 6.16 ] . The multi-cycle decomposition refines the single-cycle case to handle arbitrary peripheral periods.

Definition .67 Block-permutation structure

CycleStructure Let \(T : M_{D}(\mathbb {C}) \to M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\). A block-permutation structure for \(T\) consists of a finite index set \(\iota \), a family of orthogonal projections \(P_k \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) for \(k \in \iota \), and a permutation \(\sigma \in \mathrm{Sym}(\iota )\) (not necessarily a single cycle—in general \(\sigma \) may have multiple disjoint cycles), such that

\[ T(P_{\sigma (k)}) = P_k,\quad T(P_k X) = T(P_k)\, T(X),\quad T(X P_k) = T(X)\, T(P_k) \]

for every \(k \in \iota \) and \(X \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\).

Definition .68 Multi-cycle block-permutation structure

MultiCycleDecomposition A multi-cycle decomposition of \(T : M_{D}(\mathbb {C}) \to M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) consists of a finite cycle index \(\iota \), a per-cycle period \(m : \iota \to \mathbb {N}_{{\gt}0}\), a projection family \(P_{c,k} \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) for \(c \in \iota \) and \(k \in \{ 0,\ldots ,m(c){-}1\} \) consisting of orthogonal projections. For every \(c \in \iota \) and \(k \in \{ 0,\ldots ,m(c){-}1\} \), the per-cycle cyclic action is

\[ T(P_{c,k+1}) = P_{c,k}. \]

The multiplicative-domain factorisations are \(T(P_{c,k}\, X) = T(P_{c,k})\, T(X)\) and \(T(X\, P_{c,k}) = T(X)\, T(P_{c,k})\).

Definition .71 Flattening to a single-index block-permutation structure

MultiCycleDecomposition.toCycleStructure def:multi_cycle_decomposition, def:cycle_structure A multi-cycle decomposition gives a single-index block-permutation structure on the sigma index \(\Sigma _{c \in \iota } \{ 0,\ldots ,m(c){-}1\} \): the permutation is the sigma-product of per-cycle cyclic shifts \(k \mapsto k+1\) (whose cycle decomposition has one cycle per \(c \in \iota \)), the projections are \((c, k) \mapsto P_{c,k}\), and the multiplicative-domain factorisations descend componentwise. This section proves the peripheral spectrum structure of  [ Wol12 , Theorem 6.6 ] for irreducible Schwarz maps: the peripheral eigenvalues form a finite cyclic group. The trace-preserving refinement that the order divides the bond dimension is proved in Theorem .75.

Lemma .72 Peripheral eigenvectors are invertible

MPSTensor.isUnit_peripheral_eigenvector thm:ks_peripheral Let \(E\) be an irreducible unital Kraus map on \(M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) with a positive-definite adjoint fixed point. If \(X \ne 0\) satisfies \(E(X) = \mu X\) with \(|\mu | = 1\), then \(X\) is invertible.

▼
The KS equality gives \(E(X^\dagger X) = X^\dagger X\), so \(X^\dagger X\) is a nonzero PSD fixed point. Irreducibility upgrades it to positive definite, hence invertible, so \(\det (X) \ne 0\).

Lemma .73 Peripheral eigenvalues: product closure

MPSTensor.peripheralEigenvalues_mul_mem_of_irreducible_unital_of_adjoint_fixedPoint lem:isUnit_peripheral_eigenvector,thm:ks_peripheral Let \(E\) be an irreducible unital Kraus map on \(M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) with a positive-definite adjoint fixed point. ▼

Theorem .74 Peripheral eigenvalues: cyclic structure

▼
The peripheral eigenvalues form a finite subset of \(\mathbb {C}\), contain \(1\), and are closed under multiplication and inversion, so they form a finite subgroup of \(\mathbb {C}^\times \).
