For every \(X\in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\), the conjugation filter

\[ \rho \longmapsto X\rho X^\dagger \]

is completely positive, hence positive. Therefore, if \(T:M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\to M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) is positive, then \(\rho \mapsto T(X\rho X^\dagger )\) is positive. If in addition \(X^\dagger X=\mathbb {1}\), then the conjugation filter is trace-preserving and hence is a quantum channel.

Proof ▶

\[ \operatorname{tr}(X\rho X^\dagger )=\operatorname{tr}(X^\dagger X\rho )=\operatorname{tr}(\rho ). \]

\[ X^\dagger T^*(\mathbb {1})X=\mathbb {1}, \]

Theorem 4.12 Forward Lorentz-cone trace inequality
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • Matrix.PosSemidef.trace_sq_re_le_trace_re_sq

If \(A \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) is positive semidefinite, then

\[ \operatorname {Re}\operatorname{tr}(A^2) \le \left(\operatorname {Re}\operatorname{tr}(A)\right)^2 . \]
Proof ▶

Diagonalize \(A\) with nonnegative eigenvalues \(\lambda _i\). Then \(\operatorname{tr}(A^2)=\sum _i \lambda _i^2\) and \(\operatorname{tr}(A)=\sum _i \lambda _i\), so the desired inequality is

\[ \sum _i \lambda _i^2 \le \left(\sum _i \lambda _i\right)^2 , \]

which follows because all mixed products are nonnegative.

Theorem 4.13 Trace-nonnegative Lorentz-cone converse
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • Matrix.IsHermitian.posSemidef_of_trace_re_nonneg_of_card_sub_one_mul_trace_sq_re_le

Let \(A\in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) be Hermitian and suppose that \(\operatorname {Re}\operatorname{tr}(A)\ge 0\). If

\[ (D-1)\operatorname {Re}\operatorname{tr}(A^2) \le \left(\operatorname {Re}\operatorname{tr}(A)\right)^2 , \]

then \(A\) is positive semidefinite.

This is the trace-nonnegative form of the converse in [ Wol12 , Proposition 3.9 ] . The unrestricted squared implication printed there is false without a trace sign condition; this is recorded in docs/paper-gaps/wolf_ch3_lorentz_cone_trace_sign.tex.

Proof ▶

Diagonalize \(A\) with real eigenvalues \(\lambda _i\). Suppose that some eigenvalue \(\lambda _0\) is negative. Let

\[ T=\sum _i\lambda _i,\qquad R=\sum _{i\ne 0}\lambda _i . \]

Since \(T\ge 0\) and \(T=\lambda _0+R\), one has \(T{\lt}R\), and hence \(T^2{\lt}R^2\). Cauchy’s inequality gives

\[ R^2\le (D-1)\sum _{i\ne 0}\lambda _i^2 \le (D-1)\sum _i\lambda _i^2, \]

contradicting the assumed Lorentz inequality. Thus all eigenvalues are nonnegative.

Definition 4.14 Completely positive map
✓

A linear map \(E : M_{D}(\mathbb {C}) \to M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) is completely positive (CP) if it admits a Kraus representation: there exist operators \(\{ K_i\} _{i=0}^{r-1}\) with \(K_i \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) such that, for every \(X \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\),

\[ E(X) = \sum _{i=0}^{r-1} K_i X K_i^\dagger . \]

By Choi’s theorem, this is equivalent to \(E \otimes \operatorname{id}_n\) being positive for all \(n\).

✓

Lemma 4.60 Peripheral closure via adjoint fixed point
✓

Let \(E(X) = \sum _i K_i X K_i^\dagger \) be a Kraus map that is unital (\(\sum _i K_i K_i^\dagger = \mathbb {1}\)), and assume:

  1. the adjoint Kraus map \(E^*(X) = \sum _i K_i^\dagger X K_i\) has a positive definite fixed point \(\rho {\gt} 0\), and

  2. \(E\) is irreducible.

Then \(\mathrm{peripheral}(E)\) is closed under powers: if \(\mu \in \mathrm{peripheral}(E)\) then \(\mu ^n \in \mathrm{peripheral}(E)\) for every \(n \in \mathbb {N}\).

\[ \operatorname{tr}(\rho G) = \operatorname{tr}(\rho E(X^\dagger X)) - \operatorname{tr}(\rho E(X)^\dagger E(X)) = \operatorname{tr}(E^*(\rho ) X^\dagger X) - \operatorname{tr}(\rho E(X)^\dagger E(X)) = \operatorname{tr}(\rho X^\dagger X) - |\mu |^2 \operatorname{tr}(\rho X^\dagger X) = 0. \]

Hence \(X^\dagger X\) is a PSD fixed point, which is positive definite by irreducibility (Theorem 6.4). So \(X\) is invertible. By Theorem 4.55 (KS equality implies Kraus commutation), \(X\) commutes with each Kraus operator; iterating via the left multiplicative identity (Theorem 4.57) gives \(E(X^n) = \mu ^n X^n\), so \(\mu ^n\) is peripheral.

\[ E(E(X) E(X)^\dagger ) = E(X) E(X)^\dagger . \]

The vanishing of both Kadison–Schwarz gaps, again by the argument of Theorem 4.70, places \(E(X)\) in the multiplicative domain.
