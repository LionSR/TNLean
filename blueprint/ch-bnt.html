13 Basis of Normal Tensors

This chapter introduces the basis of normal tensors (BNT) notion, the canonical-form variants with BNT separation, the cross-overlap decay theorem, and the permutation-rigidity arguments used in the proof of the Fundamental Theorem. The chapter concludes with auxiliary results on coefficient convergence and Newton–Girard identities. The main references are and  .

13.1 Basis of normal tensors

Definition 13.1 Basis of Normal Tensors (BNT)
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 2.23
  • Definition 2.4

Lean declarations

  • MPSTensor.IsBNT

A basis of normal tensors (BNT) for a total tensor \(A_{\mathrm{tot}}\) consists of \(g\) block tensors \((A_1, \ldots , A_g)\) with bond dimensions \((D_1, \ldots , D_g)\) such that:

  1. each \(A_j\) is normal (Definition 2.23),

  2. at each system size \(N\), the MPV of \(A_{\mathrm{tot}}\) lies in the span of the block MPVs \(\{ |V^{(N)}(A_j)\rangle \} _{j=1}^g\),

  3. for sufficiently large \(N\), the block MPV vectors \(\{ |V^{(N)}(A_j)\rangle \} _{j=1}^g\) are linearly independent.

Definition 13.2 Canonical form with BNT separation
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 12.9
  • Definition 2.16

Lean declarations

  • MPSTensor.IsCanonicalFormBNT

A family is in canonical form with BNT separation if it satisfies Definition 12.9 and, in addition:

  1. the moduli are strictly decreasing: \(|\mu _1| {\gt} |\mu _2| {\gt} \cdots {\gt} |\mu _r|\) (strengthened from the non-increasing ordering of Definition 12.9),

  2. distinct blocks of the same bond dimension are not gauge-phase equivalent.

The strictly decreasing condition is justified by the BNT grouping step, which merges gauge-phase-equivalent blocks with equal moduli.

Theorem 13.4 Cross-overlap decay for distinct blocks in canonical form with BNT separation
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 13.2
  • Definition 2.35
  • Theorem 9.25
  • Theorem 9.23

Lean declarations

  • MPSTensor.IsCanonicalFormBNT.cross_overlap_tendsto_zero

If \(((\mu _k),(A_k))\) is in canonical form with BNT separation and \(j \neq k\), then \(O_{A_j A_k}(N) \to 0\).

Proof ▶

Definition 13.2 supplies injectivity and TP normalization for each block, which are the hypotheses required by the spectral-gap theorems. If \(D_j \neq D_k\), the rectangular overlap-decay theorem (Theorem 9.23) gives \(O_{A_j A_k}(N) \to 0\). If \(D_j = D_k\), the separation condition says that \(A_j\) and \(A_k\) are not gauge-phase equivalent, so the same-dimension overlap-decay theorem (Theorem 9.25) again gives decay to \(0\).

Theorem 13.5 Canonical form with BNT separation yields a basis of normal tensors
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 13.2
  • Definition 13.1
  • Theorem 2.28
  • Theorem 13.4
  • Lemma 2.36

Lean declarations

  • MPSTensor.IsCanonicalFormBNT.isBNT

If \(((\mu _k), (A_k))\) is in canonical form with BNT separation, then the block tensors form a basis of normal tensors for the block-diagonal tensor \(\bigoplus _k \mu _k A_k\).

Proof ▶

Each block is injective, so it is normal by taking blocking length \(1\). The MPV decomposition (Theorem 2.28) gives

\[ |V^{(N)}(A_{\mathrm{tot}})\rangle = \sum _k \mu _k^N |V^{(N)}(A_k)\rangle , \]

so the MPV of \(A_{\mathrm{tot}}\) lies in the span of the block MPVs at each system size. For eventual linear independence, consider the Gram matrix

\[ G_{jk}(N) = \langle V^{(N)}(A_j) | V^{(N)}(A_k) \rangle . \]

By Lemma 2.36, \(G_{jk}(N)=\overline{O_{A_j A_k}(N)}\). The self-overlap \(O_{A_j A_j}(N) \to 1\) is real-valued under TP normalization, hence \(G_{jj}(N) \to \overline{1} = 1\). For \(j \neq k\), Theorem 13.4 gives \(O_{A_j A_k}(N) \to 0\), so \(G_{jk}(N) \to \overline{0} = 0\). Thus \(G(N) \to I_g\), hence the Gram matrix is eventually invertible. Therefore the vectors \(\{ |V^{(N)}(A_j)\rangle \} _{j=1}^g\) are linearly independent for all sufficiently large \(N\).

Remark 13.6 The normality gap for normal canonical form with BNT separation
#

Definition 13.3 references the normal canonical form conditions (Definition 11.31), which encodes normality via the spectral characterization (primitive transfer map). Definition 13.1 requires normality via the algebraic characterization (eventual block injectivity, Definition 2.23). These are equivalent by Remark 2.20, but the equivalence must be invoked explicitly: the Wielandt theory of Chapter 10 provides the implication from primitivity to eventual block injectivity needed here.

Theorem 13.7 Permutation rigidity for proportional MPV decompositions

  • MPSTensor.exists_eq_numBlocks_and_equiv_gaugePhase_of_proportional_decomp

Let \((A_j)_{j=1}^{g_A}\) and \((B_k)_{k=1}^{g_B}\) be injective families satisfying the TP normalization \(\sum _i (A_j^i)^\dagger A_j^i = \mathbb {1}\) and \(\sum _i (B_k^i)^\dagger B_k^i = \mathbb {1}\), whose self-overlaps converge to \(1\) and whose cross-overlaps within each family converge to \(0\). Assume moreover that there exist MPS tensors \(A_{\mathrm{tot}}\) and \(B_{\mathrm{tot}}\), coefficient arrays \(a_{N,j}\) and \(b_{N,k}\), and a sequence \(c_N\) with nonzero limits \(a_j^\infty \), \(b_k^\infty \), \(c^\infty \) such that for every \(N\),

\[ |V^{(N)}(A_{\mathrm{tot}})\rangle = \sum _{j=1}^{g_A} a_{N,j} |V^{(N)}(A_j)\rangle , \qquad |V^{(N)}(B_{\mathrm{tot}})\rangle = \sum _{k=1}^{g_B} b_{N,k} |V^{(N)}(B_k)\rangle , \]

with \(a_{N,j} \to a_j^\infty \neq 0\) and \(b_{N,k} \to b_k^\infty \neq 0\), and also

\[ |V^{(N)}(A_{\mathrm{tot}})\rangle = c_N |V^{(N)}(B_{\mathrm{tot}})\rangle \]

with \(c_N \to c^\infty \neq 0\). Then \(g_A = g_B\), and there is a permutation \(\pi \) of the common block index set such that \(A_{\pi (k)}\) and \(B_k\) have the same bond dimension and are gauge-phase equivalent for each \(k\). The canonical-form application of these hypotheses is described in Remark 13.8.

Proof ▶

Substitute the two decomposition formulas into the proportionality relation and take overlaps with the block MPVs. Passing to the limit using the convergence of \(a_{N,j}\), \(b_{N,k}\), and \(c_N\) produces a limiting mixed-overlap matrix. The asymptotically orthonormal overlap hypotheses within each family force every row and column of that matrix to contain at most one nonzero entry, hence determine a permutation \(\pi \). If one of the matched mixed overlaps came from blocks of different bond dimensions, Theorem 9.23 would force it to decay to \(0\); if the bond dimensions agree but the tensors are not gauge-phase equivalent, Theorem 9.25 does the same. Therefore every nonzero matched entry comes from blocks of the same bond dimension that are gauge-phase equivalent.

This is the overlap-and-decomposition form of the permutation step corresponding to ( .

Remark 13.8
#

In the canonical-form application, the abstract data above come from block-diagonal tensors

\[ A_{\mathrm{tot}} = \bigoplus _{j=1}^{g_A} \mu _j A_j, \qquad B_{\mathrm{tot}} = \bigoplus _{k=1}^{g_B} \nu _k B_k, \]

and Theorem 2.28 gives the coefficient arrays \(a_{N,j} = \mu _j^N\) and \(b_{N,k} = \nu _k^N\). To fit the convergence hypotheses of Theorem 13.7, one first normalizes by the dominant weight on each side: the relevant arrays are \((\mu _j/\mu _0)^N\) and \((\nu _k/\nu _0)^N\), with \((\mu _j/\mu _0)^N \to 0\) for \(j \ge 1\) and \((\nu _k/\nu _0)^N \to 0\) for \(k \ge 1\) by Theorem 13.11, while the overall factors \(\mu _0^N\) and \(\nu _0^N\) are absorbed into the proportionality constant \(c_N\). Definition 13.2 supplies injectivity, TP normalization, and the within-family overlap limits, so the theorem isolates the abstract permutation argument from this canonical-form setting.

  • MPSTensor.exists_eq_numBlocks_and_equiv_gaugePhase_of_overlapOrtho

Let \((A_j)_{j=1}^{g_A}\) and \((B_k)_{k=1}^{g_B}\) be two families of injective tensors satisfying the TP normalization \(\sum _i (A_j^i)^\dagger A_j^i = \mathbb {1}\) and \(\sum _i (B_k^i)^\dagger B_k^i = \mathbb {1}\), whose self-overlaps converge to \(1\) and whose cross-overlaps within each family converge to \(0\). If for every system size \(N\),

\[ \operatorname{span}_{\mathbb {C}}\{ |V^{(N)}(A_j)\rangle : 1 \le j \le g_A\} = \operatorname{span}_{\mathbb {C}}\{ |V^{(N)}(B_k)\rangle : 1 \le k \le g_B\} , \]

then \(g_A = g_B\), and there is a permutation \(\pi \) of the common block index set such that \(A_j\) and \(B_{\pi (j)}\) have the same bond dimension and are gauge-phase equivalent for each \(j\).

Proof ▶

For each family, the Gram matrix converges to the identity by Lemma 2.36 together with the self-overlap and off-diagonal decay hypotheses. Hence for all sufficiently large \(N\) both families of MPV vectors are linearly independent. At such an \(N\), the equality of spans in the statement forces the two common subspaces to have the same dimension, so \(g_A = g_B\). The span equality then gives change-of-basis matrices between the two families, and the same mixed-overlap matching argument shows that the mixed-overlap matrix has exactly one nonzero entry in each row and column. This produces the permutation \(\pi \), while Theorems 9.23 and 9.25 upgrade each nonzero match to equality of bond dimensions and gauge-phase equivalence.

Under the canonical form with BNT separation of Definition 13.2, indexed so that \(\mu _0\) is the (strictly) dominant weight, one has \((\mu _k / \mu _0)^N \to 0\) for every \(k \ge 1\) as \(N \to \infty \).

Remark 13.12 Strict-dominance regime
#

Theorem 13.11 applies when one modulus is strictly dominant: \(|\mu _0| {\gt} |\mu _k|\) for \(k \ge 1\). In the more general periodic setting of ( , the coefficient attached to block \(j\) can take the form \(\sum _q \mu _{j,q}^N\), where the \(\mu _{j,q}\) are the individual weights contributed by that block. The oscillatory case with \(|\mu _{j,q}| = 1\) is treated separately in the full paper.

13.4 Newton–Girard identities

Theorem 13.13 Newton–Girard trace recursion
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • Matrix.charpoly_eq_of_forall_trace_pow_eq

If \(A, B \in M_{n}(\mathbb {C})\) satisfy \(\operatorname{tr}(A^k) = \operatorname{tr}(B^k)\) for all \(k \ge 1\), then \(A\) and \(B\) have the same characteristic polynomial.

Proof ▶

Let \(e_m\) denote the \(m\)th elementary symmetric polynomial in the eigenvalues, with \(e_0 = 1\). The Newton–Girard recursion

\[ m e_m = \sum _{i=1}^m (-1)^{i-1} e_{m-i} \operatorname{tr}(A^i) \]

expresses the coefficients of the characteristic polynomial in terms of the power-sum traces \(\operatorname{tr}(A^i)\). Equal power sums for \(A\) and \(B\) therefore give equal coefficients, hence equal characteristic polynomials.

Theorem 13.14 Equal power sums imply equal multisets
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • Matrix.sum_pow_eq_implies_multiset_eq

Let \(\alpha , \beta : \{ 1, \ldots , n\} \to \mathbb {C}\). If \(\sum _i \alpha _i^k = \sum _i \beta _i^k\) for all \(k \ge 1\), then the multisets \(\{ \alpha _i\} \) and \(\{ \beta _i\} \) are equal.

Proof ▶

Construct diagonal matrices \(\operatorname{diag}(\alpha )\) and \(\operatorname{diag}(\beta )\). The power-sum hypothesis gives \(\operatorname{tr}(\operatorname{diag}(\alpha )^k) = \operatorname{tr}(\operatorname{diag}(\beta )^k)\). By Theorem 13.13, the characteristic polynomials agree: \(\prod _i (X - \alpha _i) = \prod _i (X - \beta _i)\). Extracting roots gives multiset equality.

Remark 13.15
#

Theorems 13.13 and 13.14 remain relevant for the general equal-MPV Theorem 14.5, where one expects a power-sum step to recover the multiplicity-weight data. The present explicit-coefficient special case, Theorem 14.6, instead uses linear independence of the block MPV family supplied by a basis of normal tensors.
