Wielandt Bound

This chapter proves a quantum analog of Wielandt’s inequality: for a normal MPS tensor with bond dimension \(D\), the cumulative span of matrix products stabilises after at most \(D^2\) steps. The results follow  [ SPGWC10 ] .

Cumulative span

Definition .1 Word span

MPSTensor.wordSpan def:mps_tensor, def:eval_word The word span at length \(n\) is the subspace

\[ S_n(A) = \spn _{\mathbb {C}}\{ A^w : |w| = n\} \subseteq M_{D}(\mathbb {C}). \]

This is  [ SPGWC10 , Section II ] .

Definition .2 Cumulative span

MPSTensor.cumulativeSpan def:word_span The cumulative span at level \(n\) is

\[ T_n(A) = S_0(A) + S_1(A) + \cdots + S_n(A). \]

Lemma .3 Monotonicity of cumulative span

MPSTensor.cumulativeSpan_mono def:cumulative_span \(T_n(A) \le T_{n+1}(A)\) for all \(n\).

▼
def:cumulative_span \(T_{n+1}\) includes all words of length \(\le n+1\), which contains all words of length \(\le n\).
▼

Lemma .4 Dimension bound

MPSTensor.cumulativeSpan_finrank_le def:cumulative_span \(\dim T_n(A) \le D^2\) for all \(n\).

▼
\(T_n(A) \subseteq M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) and \(\dim M_{D}(\mathbb {C}) = D^2\).
▼

Theorem .5 Cumulative span stabilisation

MPSTensor.cumulativeSpan_stable def:cumulative_span If \(T_n(A) = T_{n+1}(A)\), then \(T_m(A) = T_n(A)\) for all \(m \ge n\).

▼
If \(T_n = T_{n+1}\), then \(S_{n+1} \subseteq T_n\). Left-multiplying any element of \(S_{n+1}\) by \(A^i\) gives an element of \(S_{n+2}\), so \(S_{n+2} \subseteq T_{n+1} = T_n\). By induction, \(S_m \subseteq T_n\) for all \(m {\gt} n\), hence \(T_m = T_n\).
▼

Theorem .6 Dimension growth

MPSTensor.cumulativeSpan_finrank_strict_mono def:cumulative_span If \(T_n(A) \neq T_{n+1}(A)\), then \(\dim T_{n+1}(A) {\gt} \dim T_n(A)\).

▼
lem:cumulative_mono \(T_n \le T_{n+1}\) (Lemma .3) and \(T_n \neq T_{n+1}\) imply strict submodule inclusion, hence strict rank increase.
▼

Lemma .7 Word span characterises block injectivity

MPSTensor.wordSpan_eq_top_iff_isNBlkInjective def:word_span, def:l_blk_injective \(S_L(A) = M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) if and only if \(A\) is \(L\)-block injective.

▼
def:word_span, def:l_blk_injective Both conditions assert that the span of all products of length \(L\) equals \(M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\).
▼

Lemma .8 Zero-length word span

MPSTensor.wordSpan_zero_ne_top_of_two_le def:word_span If \(D \ge 2\), then \(S_0(A) \ne M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\).

▼
def:word_span The zero-length products consist only of the identity, so \(S_0(A)=\spn _{\mathbb {C}}\{ \mathbb {1}\} \) has dimension \(1\). Since \(\dim M_{D}(\mathbb {C})=D^2 \ge 4\), this span cannot be all of \(M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\).
▼

Corollary .9 \(0\)-block injectivity

MPSTensor.bondDim_eq_one_of_isNBlkInjective_zero lem:wordspan_blk_inj If \(D \ge 1\) and \(A\) is \(0\)-block injective, then \(D=1\).

▼
lem:wordspan_blk_inj, lem:wordspan_zero_ne_top By Lemma .7, \(0\)-block injectivity gives \(S_0(A)=M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\). Lemma .8 excludes \(D \ge 2\), hence the nonzero bond dimension must be \(1\).
▼

Lemma .10 Two-sided span from one nonzero matrix

Matrix.span_range_mul_nonzero_mul_eq_top If \(C \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) is nonzero, then

\[ \spn _{\mathbb {C}}\{ R C S : R, S \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\} =M_{D}(\mathbb {C}). \]

This is the matrix-factorization input used in  [ PGVWC07 , Lemma lem1 ] .

▼
Choose a nonzero entry \(C_{pq}\). Then every matrix unit \(E_{ij}\) is a scalar multiple of \(E_{ip} C E_{qj}\), so the displayed span contains the standard matrix basis.
▼

Lemma .11 Exact words around a nonzero middle matrix

MPSTensor.span_range_evalWord_mul_nonzero_mul_evalWord_eq_top def:l_blk_injective, lem:two_sided_nonzero_matrix_span If \(A\) is \(L\)-block injective and \(X \neq 0\), then

\[ \spn _{\mathbb {C}}\{ A^u X A^v : |u| = |v| = L\} =M_{D}(\mathbb {C}). \]

▼
lem:two_sided_nonzero_matrix_span By \(L\)-block injectivity, the length-\(L\) word products span \(M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\). Bilinearity of \((R, S)\mapsto R X S\) reduces the preceding lemma to products with \(R\) and \(S\) chosen among those word products.
▼

Fitting decomposition

Definition .12 Fitting decomposition

Wielandt.FittingDecomposition A Fitting decomposition of a linear endomorphism \(f : V \to V\) on a finite-dimensional vector space over an algebraically closed field consists of:

  1. \(f\) is nilpotent on the generalized \(0\)-eigenspace,

  2. \(f\) is invertible on each generalized \(\mu \)-eigenspace for \(\mu \neq 0\),

  3. the generalized eigenspaces span \(V\),

  4. the generalized eigenspaces are linearly independent.

Theorem .13 Fitting decomposition exists

Wielandt.fittingDecomposition def:fitting_decomp Every linear endomorphism on a finite-dimensional vector space over an algebraically closed field admits a Fitting decomposition.

▼
Nilpotency on the zero generalized eigenspace follows from the definition of generalized eigenspaces. Invertibility on nonzero generalized eigenspaces follows because \(f - \mu \) is nilpotent there, so \(f = \mu (1 - (1-f/\mu ))\) is invertible. Spanning and independence are standard results for generalized eigenspaces over algebraically closed fields. In  [ SPGWC10 ] and [ Wol12 , Lemma 6.3(b) ] , the Jordan normal form is used directly. The argument is phrased in terms of generalized eigenspaces instead.
▼

Theorem .14 Nilpotent power bound

Wielandt.nilpotent_pow_eq_zero_of_finrank If \(f\) is nilpotent on a space of dimension \(n\), then \(f^n = 0\).

▼
A nilpotent endomorphism on an \(n\)-dimensional space has nilpotency index \(\le n\).
▼

Nonzero trace product

Theorem .15 Cumulative span reaches \(M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) — explicit bound

MPSTensor.cumulativeSpan_eq_top_of_isNormal_bound def:cumulative_span, def:normal If \(A\) is normal, then there exists \(n \le D^2\) with \(T_n(A) = M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\).

▼
thm:cumulative_strict_mono, lem:cumulative_finrank_le By Theorem .6, if \(T_n \neq T_{n+1}\) then \(\dim T_{n+1} {\gt} \dim T_n\). Since \(\dim T_n \le D^2\) (Lemma .4), after at most \(D^2\) steps either \(T_n = M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) or \(T_n\) has stabilised. Stabilisation before reaching \(M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) contradicts normality (which requires \(S_{L_0} = M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) for some \(L_0\), hence \(T_{L_0} = M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\)).
▼

Theorem .16 Cumulative span reaches \(M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\)

MPSTensor.cumulativeSpan_eq_top def:cumulative_span, def:normal If \(A\) is normal, then \(T_{D^2}(A) = M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\).

▼
thm:cumulative_eq_top_bound Take \(n\) from Theorem .15; since \(n \le D^2\) and \(T\) is monotone, \(T_{D^2} = M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\).
▼

Theorem .17 Nonzero trace product

MPSTensor.exists_nonzero_trace_word def:normal If \(A\) is normal, then there exists a word \(w\) of length \(\le D^2\) such that \(\tr (A^w) \neq 0\). This is  [ SPGWC10 , Lemma 1 ] . For the trace argument below, the nontrivial case is \(D \ge 1\); when \(D = 0\) the normality hypothesis is vacuous.

▼
thm:cumulative_eq_top By Theorem .16, \(\mathbb {1}\in T_{D^2}(A)\), so \(\mathbb {1}\) is a linear combination of word products of length \(\le D^2\). In the nontrivial case \(D \ge 1\), at least one has nonzero trace (since \(\tr (\mathbb {1}) = D \neq 0\)).
▼

Theorem .18 Cumulative span reaches \(M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) — sharp bound

MPSTensor.cumulativeSpan_eq_top_of_isNormal_sharp def:cumulative_span, def:normal If \(A\) is normal, then \(T_{D^2 - d' + 1}(A) = M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\), where \(d' = \dim S_1(A) = \krausrk (A)\). This is the sharp form of  [ SPGWC10 , Lemma 1 ] : the cumulative span already reaches \(M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) by index \(D^2 - d' + 1 \le D^2\).

▼
thm:cumulative_strict_mono, lem:cumulative_finrank_le The key observation is that if \(T_n(A) \neq T_{n+1}(A)\), the dimension strictly increases. Since \(S_1(A) \subseteq T_1(A)\) and \(\dim S_1(A) = d'\), we have \(\dim T_1(A) \ge d'\). After at most \(D^2 - d'\) additional strict-growth steps the dimension reaches \(D^2 = \dim M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\). Stabilisation before reaching \(M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) contradicts normality. Hence \(T_{D^2 - d' + 1}(A) = M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\).
▼

Theorem .19 Sharp nonzero trace product

MPSTensor.exists_nonzero_trace_word_sharp def:normal If \(A\) is normal, then there exists a word \(w\) of length \(\le D^2 - \krausrk (A) + 1\) such that \(\tr (A^w) \neq 0\). This is  [ SPGWC10 , Lemma 1 ] with the sharp length bound.

▼
thm:cumulative_span_sharp By Theorem .18, the identity \(\mathbb {1}\) lies in \(T_{D^2 - d' + 1}(A)\), hence is a linear combination of word products of length \(\le D^2 - d' + 1\). At least one such product has nonzero trace.
▼

Theorem .20 Sharp nonzero trace product — positive length

MPSTensor.exists_nonzero_trace_word_sharp_pos def:normal, thm:nonzero_trace_word_sharp If \(A\) is normal and \(D \ge 2\), then there exists a word \(w\) of positive length \(1 \le |w| \le D^2 - \krausrk (A) + 1\) such that \(\tr (A^w) \neq 0\). This strengthens Theorem .19 by requiring \(|w| \ge 1\), a feature needed for the blocking argument in the general Wielandt bound below.

▼
thm:cumulative_span_sharp Define the positive-level cumulative span \(V_n = \spn \{ A^w : 1 \le |w| \le n\} \). For \(D \ge 2\), the span \(S_0(A) = \spn \{ \mathbb {1}\} \) has dimension \(1\), so \(V_1 \supseteq S_1(A)\) has dimension \(\ge d' \ge 1\). The same stabilization-or-strict-growth dichotomy shows \(V_{D^2 - d' + 1} = M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\): if \(V_n\) stabilises and is not \(M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\), then \(V_n\) is closed under left-multiplication by each \(A^i\), so \(S_{N}(A) \subseteq V_n \neq M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) for all \(N \ge 1\), contradicting normality. Since \(V_{D^2 - d' + 1} = M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\), the identity \(\mathbb {1}\) lies in \(V_{D^2 - d' + 1}\) and is therefore a linear combination of word products of positive length. At least one such product has nonzero trace (since \(\tr (\mathbb {1}) = D \ge 2 \neq 0\)).
▼

Eigenvector extraction

Theorem .21 Nonzero trace implies eigenvector

exists_eigenvector_of_trace_ne_zero If \(M \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) has \(\tr (M) \neq 0\), then \(M\) has a nonzero eigenvalue \(\mu \neq 0\) and a corresponding eigenvector \(\varphi \neq 0\) with \(M \varphi = \mu \varphi \).

▼
thm:fitting_decomp A matrix with nonzero trace has a nonzero eigenvalue (since trace = sum of eigenvalues counted with multiplicity). The Fitting decomposition (Theorem .13) ensures that the corresponding generalized eigenspace is nontrivial. On that generalized eigenspace, \(M - \mu \mathbb {1}\) is nilpotent, so its kernel is nontrivial. Any nonzero vector in \(\ker (M - \mu \mathbb {1})\) is therefore a genuine eigenvector with eigenvalue \(\mu \).
▼

Theorem .22 Eigenvalue extraction

MPSTensor.exists_word_eigenvector def:normal If \(A\) is normal, then there exist a word \(w_0\) with \(|w_0| \le D^2\), a nonzero scalar \(\mu \neq 0\), and a nonzero vector \(\varphi \neq 0\) such that \(A^{w_0} \varphi = \mu \varphi \).

▼
thm:nonzero_trace_word, thm:eigenvector_from_trace By Theorem .17, there is a word \(w_0\) with \(\tr (A^{w_0}) \neq 0\) and \(|w_0| \le D^2\). By Theorem .21, \(A^{w_0}\) has a nonzero eigenvector.
▼

Eigenvector spreading

Definition .23 Cumulative vector span

MPSTensor.cumulativeVectorSpan def:mps_tensor Given an MPS tensor \(A\) and a vector \(\varphi \in \mathbb {C}^D\), the cumulative vector span is

\[ K_n(A, \varphi ) = \spn _{\mathbb {C}}\{ A^w \varphi : |w| \le n\} \subseteq \mathbb {C}^D. \]

This is  [ SPGWC10 , proof of Lemma 2(a) ] .

Theorem .24 Vector span stabilisation

MPSTensor.cumulativeVectorSpan_stable def:cumulative_vector_span If \(K_n(A, \varphi ) = K_{n+1}(A, \varphi )\), then \(K_m(A, \varphi ) = K_n(A, \varphi )\) for all \(m \ge n\).

▼
thm:cumulative_stable Same argument as Theorem .5: left-multiplication by \(A^i\) cannot escape the stabilised span.
▼

Lemma .25 Vector span from matrix span

MPSTensor.cumulativeVectorSpan_eq_top_of_cumulativeSpan_eq_top def:cumulative_vector_span, def:cumulative_span If \(T_N(A) = M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) and \(\varphi \neq 0\), then \(K_N(A, \varphi ) = \mathbb {C}^D\).

▼
Every matrix \(M \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) is in \(T_N(A)\), so in particular every rank-one matrix sending \(\varphi \) to any target vector \(v\) is in \(T_N\). Applying such matrices to \(\varphi \) recovers all of \(\mathbb {C}^D\).
▼

Theorem .26 Eigenvector spreading

MPSTensor.eigenvector_spreading def:cumulative_vector_span, def:normal Let \(A\) be a normal MPS tensor and let \(A^{i_0} \varphi = \mu \varphi \) with \(\mu \neq 0\) and \(\varphi \neq 0\). Then \(K_{D-1}(A, \varphi ) = \mathbb {C}^D\). This is  [ SPGWC10 , Lemma 2(a) ] .

▼
thm:cumulative_eq_top, lem:vector_span_from_matrix, thm:vector_span_stable The vector span \(K_n\) is monotone and \(\dim K_n \le D\). If \(K_n = K_{n+1}\) for some \(n {\lt} D-1\), the span stabilises (Theorem .24). But \(T_{D^2}(A) = M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) by Theorem .16, so Lemma .25 gives \(K_{D^2}(A, \varphi ) = \mathbb {C}^D\), contradicting early stabilisation. Hence \(\dim K_n\) grows by \(\ge 1\) at each step, reaching \(D\) by step \(D - 1\).
▼

Proof of the cumulative Wielandt bound

Theorem .27 Wielandt chain

MPSTensor.wielandt_chain def:cumulative_vector_span, def:normal, def:cumulative_span Let \(A\) be a normal MPS tensor with bond dimension \(D \ge 1\). Then the following hold simultaneously:

  1. \(T_{D^2}(A) = M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) (cumulative span stabilises);

  2. there exists a word \(w_0\) with \(|w_0| \le D^2\) and \(\tr (A^{w_0}) \ne 0\);

  3. there exist \(w_0, \mu \ne 0, \varphi \ne 0\) with \(|w_0| \le D^2\) and \(A^{w_0} \varphi = \mu \, \varphi \);

  4. for every \(\varphi \ne 0\), \(K_{D^2}(A, \varphi ) = \mathbb {C}^D\).

▼
thm:cumulative_eq_top, thm:eigenvalue_extraction, thm:eigenvector_spreading Items (1)–(3) follow from Theorems .16 and .22. Item (4) combines these with the eigenvector spreading bound (Theorem .26).
▼

Theorem .28 Wielandt bound

MPSTensor.cumulativeSpan_eq_top def:cumulative_span, def:normal If \(A\) is a normal MPS tensor with bond dimension \(D\), then \(T_{D^2}(A) = M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\). In particular, the cumulative span stabilises at index at most \(D^2\). This is the cumulative-span part of  [ SPGWC10 , Theorem 1 ] . The cited theorem is stronger: via Lemma 2(b) it upgrades this to a fixed-length conclusion \(S_N(A) = M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\), stated below as a separate statement.

▼
thm:cumulative_eq_top Immediate from Theorem .16.

Fixed-length matrix spanning

The preceding sections establish the cumulative-spanning part of the Lemma 2(a) argument from  [ SPGWC10 ] : eigenvector spreading gives a cumulative vector span \(K_{D-1}(A,\varphi ) = \mathbb {C}^D\). The next stage is to pass to a span at one fixed length, namely to find \(n_0\) such that \(S_{n_0}(A) = M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) (i.e. word products of exactly one length span all matrices).

▼

Lemma .46 Eigenvector padding

▼

From primitivity to normality

The primitivity condition of Definition .61 combines TP normalization, a nonzero PSD fixed point, and a complementary transfer-map gap. It is connected to normality through the following results.

Theorem .65 Fixed-point uniqueness from a complementary transfer-map gap

MPSTensor.IsPrimitiveMPS.fixedPoint_unique def:primitive_mps If \(A\) is a primitive MPS tensor with PSD fixed point \(\rho \), then every fixed point \(\sigma \) of \(\mathcal{E}_A\) is proportional to \(\rho \): \(\sigma = \frac{\tr (\sigma )}{\tr (\rho )} \rho \).

▼
Set \(\sigma ' = \sigma - \frac{\tr (\sigma )}{\tr (\rho )} \rho \). Then \(\tr (\sigma ') = 0\) and \((\mathcal{E}_A - P_\rho )(\sigma ') = \sigma '\). If \(\sigma ' \neq 0\), then \(1\) is an eigenvalue of \(\mathcal{E}_A - P_\rho \), contradicting the complementary transfer-map gap \(\rho _{\mathrm{spec}}(\mathcal{E}_A - P_\rho ) {\lt} 1\).

▼

Remark .67 Primitivity: transfer-map gap vs. positive definiteness

The primitivity condition used in Section ?? has three parts: TP normalization \(\sum _i (A^i)^\dagger A^i = \mathbb {1}\), a nonzero PSD fixed point \(\rho \), and the complementary transfer-map gap \(\rho _{\mathrm{spec}}(\mathcal{E}_A - P_\rho ) {\lt} 1\). The notion of “primitive” in [ SPGWC10 , Proposition 3 ] additionally requires \(\rho \) to be positive definite (full rank). Without positive definiteness, the transfer-map gap condition alone does not imply irreducibility or normality. A counterexample is \(D = 2\), \(\rho = \diag (1,0)\).

With the additional hypothesis \(\rho {\gt} 0\), Theorem .68 gives irreducibility. Together with the Burnside argument below (Section ??) and Theorem .84, one obtains

\[ \text{primitive} + \rho \text{ positive definite} \Rightarrow \text{irreducible} \Rightarrow \algspn (A) = M_{D}(\mathbb {C}) \Rightarrow \text{normal}. \]

The last step uses the aperiodicity condition \(\mathbb {1}\in S_1(A)\) from Remark .79. After restoring the positive-definite hypothesis, [ SPGWC10 , Proposition 3 ] identifies that primitive/strongly irreducible condition with eventual full Kraus rank, i.e. with our notion of normality.

Primitive implies irreducible

Theorem .68 Primitive tensors with positive-definite fixed point are irreducible

MPSTensor.isIrreducibleTensor_of_isPrimitiveMPS_of_posDef def:primitive_mps, def:irreducible_tensor If \(A\) is a primitive MPS tensor with PSD fixed point \(\rho \) and \(\rho \) is positive definite, then \(A\) is an irreducible tensor. This is part of  [ SPGWC10 , Proposition 3 ] (see also [ CPGSV17a , Section 2.3 ] ).

▼
def:has_inv_proj, thm:complement_pow_zero By contrapositive. Assume \(A\) has an invariant projection \(P\) (\(P \neq 0\), \(P \neq \mathbb {1}\), \((\mathbb {1}- P) A^i P = 0\) for all \(i\)). Set \(\sigma = P \rho P\).

  1. Invariance of \(\sigma \) under \(\mathcal{E}_A^n\): the projection relation \((\mathbb {1}- P) A^i P = 0\) implies \((\mathbb {1}- P) \cdot \mathcal{E}_A^n(\sigma ) = 0\) for all \(n\) (by induction on \(n\)).

  2. Convergence: By Theorem .66, \(\mathcal{E}_A^n(\sigma ) \to \frac{\tr (\sigma )}{\tr (\rho )} \rho \). Hence \((\mathbb {1}- P) \cdot \frac{\tr (\sigma )}{\tr (\rho )} \rho = 0\).

  3. Nonzero scalar: Since \(P \neq 0\) and \(\rho \) is positive definite, \(\tr (P \rho P) {\gt} 0\), so \(\frac{\tr (\sigma )}{\tr (\rho )} \neq 0\). Therefore \((\mathbb {1}- P) \rho = 0\).

  4. Contradiction: Since \(\rho \) is positive definite, it is a unit in \(M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\). From \((\mathbb {1}- P) \rho = 0\) we get \(\mathbb {1}- P = 0\), i.e. \(P = \mathbb {1}\), contradicting \(P \neq \mathbb {1}\).

▼

▼

Cumulative span to word span

Cumulative spanning (\(T_N(A) = M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\), the union of word spans of all lengths \(\le N\)) is weaker than normality (\(S_L(A) = M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) for a single length \(L\)). An aperiodicity hypothesis upgrades cumulative spanning to a single-length word span.

Remark .79 Aperiodicity condition

We say \(A\) satisfies the aperiodicity condition if \(\mathbb {1}\in S_1(A)\), i.e. the identity matrix lies in the span of the Kraus operators \(\{ A^i\} _{i=0}^{d-1}\). This is an additional hypothesis. The one-sided TP normalization \(\sum _i (A^i)^\dagger A^i = \mathbb {1}\) does not imply \(\mathbb {1}\in S_1(A)\); it only gives a quadratic identity among the Kraus operators. The aperiodicity condition is kept explicit in this section for generality. For the primitive condition with positive-definite fixed point (peripheral-spectrum primitivity together with a positive-definite fixed point), normality follows directly from Wolf’s primitivity equivalence (Theorem .64) without passing through aperiodicity.

Remark .80 Counterexample: cumulative spanning does not imply normality

In \(M_{2}(\mathbb {C})\), let \(A^0 = E_{12}\) and \(A^1 = E_{21}\) (the off-diagonal matrix units). Then \(\algspn (A) = M_{2}(\mathbb {C})\) and \(T_2(A) = M_{2}(\mathbb {C})\), but the word spans alternate: \(S_n(A)\) contains only off-diagonal matrices for odd \(n\) and only diagonal matrices for even \(n\). Hence \(S_n(A) \neq M_{2}(\mathbb {C})\) for every \(n\), and \(A\) is not normal. The aperiodicity condition \(\mathbb {1}\in S_1(A)\) fails: \(S_1(A) = \spn _{\mathbb {C}}\{ E_{12}, E_{21}\} \).
