# Alignment with the Original Literature (MPV vs MPS)

The original Cirac–Pérez‑García–Schuch–Verstraete paper distinguishes:

**MPV (Matrix Product Vectors)**  
Formal family of vectors generated by a tensor across all system sizes.

**MPS (Matrix Product States)**  
Physical interpretation of those vectors as quantum states.

The fundamental theorem discussed later is fundamentally a theorem about **MPV families**, not individual states.

### Implication for the blueprint

Chapter 2 currently mixes both terms.

Recommended change:

Rename the chapter

> **Matrix Product Vectors**

Then adopt the convention:

- **MPV** for tensor-generated vector families
- **MPS** only when discussing physical states or Hamiltonians

This matches the structure of the original literature.

## Definition 2.19 — Canonical form

This is the most structurally problematic part of the chapter.

Assessment:

The blueprint defines canonical form as a block diagonal tensor with injective blocks.

However the literature defines canonical form using **normal tensors**, with the remaining ambiguity resolved via **BNT (basis of normal tensors)**.

Thus:

- the blueprint definition is not mathematically wrong
- but it is **not the standard canonical form used in the references**

Recommendation:

Either

1. rename this as a **preliminary block decomposition**, or
2. postpone canonical form until BNT is introduced later.

# Notation and Clarity Issues

## Block index notation

The blocked alphabet uses integers

J_k ∈ {0,…,d^L−1}.

This implicitly assumes an encoding of L‑letter words as integers.

Recommendation:

Use multi‑indices (tuples) instead of flattening to integers unless required.

This will make the tensor interpretation clearer.

---

## MPV inner product vs overlap

The chapter defines

Inner product:
⟨V^{(N)}(A)|V^{(N)}(B)⟩

Overlap:
O_AB(N)

Both definitions are mathematically consistent.

However the terminology “overlap” usually already refers to the Hermitian inner product in physics.

Suggestion:

Clarify that O_AB(N) is a **bilinear overlap**.
