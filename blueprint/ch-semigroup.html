Quantum Dynamical Semigroups

This chapter develops the theory of one-parameter semigroups of quantum channels and characterizes their generators via the GKSL (Gorini–Kossakowski–Sudarshan–Lindblad) theorem, following  [ Wol12 , Chapter 7 ] .

Section ?? establishes definitions and the basic exponential form (Proposition .14). Section ?? gives the Duhamel formula and perturbation bound. Section ?? develops the generator theory and proves the GKSL theorem.

Dynamical semigroups and the exponential form

Definition .1 Dynamical semigroup

IsDynSemigroupA family of linear maps \(T : \mathbb {R}\to (M_{D}(\mathbb {C}) \to _{\ell } M_{D}(\mathbb {C}))\) is a dynamical semigroup if

  1. (semigroup law) \(T_{t+s} = T_t \circ T_s\) for all \(t,s \ge 0\); and

  2. (initial condition) \(T_0 = \id \).

This is [ Wol12 , Equation (7.1) ] .

Definition .2 Continuous dynamical semigroup

IsContinuousDynSemigroupdef:dyn_semigroup_ch12 A dynamical semigroup \(T\) is norm-continuous if the map \(t \mapsto T_t\) is continuous in the operator norm topology. In finite dimension this coincides with strong continuity.

Definition .3 Exponential semigroup

expSemigroup, expSemigroupCLMGiven a generator \(L \in \mathrm{End}_{\mathbb {C}}(M_{D}(\mathbb {C}))\), the exponential semigroup is

\[ T_t := e^{tL} = \sum _{k=0}^{\infty } \frac{(tL)^k}{k!}. \]

Theorem .4 Semigroup law for the exponential

expSemigroupCLM_add, expSemigroup_compdef:exp_semigroup_ch12 For all \(t,s \in \mathbb {R}\), \(e^{(t+s)L} = e^{tL} \cdot e^{sL}\).

▼
Since \((t \cdot L)\) commutes with \((s \cdot L)\), this follows from the exponential addition formula for commuting elements in a Banach algebra.
▼

Theorem .5 Exponential semigroup at time \(0\)

expSemigroupCLM_zerodef:exp_semigroup_ch12 The continuous-linear-map exponential satisfies \(e^{0L} = \mathbb {1}\).

▼
The exponential of the zero endomorphism is the identity.
▼

Theorem .6 Continuity of the exponential semigroup

expSemigroupCLM_continuous, expSemigroup_isContinuousDynSemigroupdef:exp_semigroup_ch12 The map \(t \mapsto e^{tL}\) is continuous in the operator norm topology.

▼
The exponential is analytic (convergence radius \(= \infty \)), hence continuous.
▼

Theorem .7 Derivative of the exponential semigroup

hasDerivAt_expSemigroupCLMdef:exp_semigroup_ch12 For all \(t \in \mathbb {R}\),

\[ \frac{d}{dt} e^{tL} = e^{tL} \cdot L. \]

This is [ Wol12 , Equation (7.2) ] .

▼
Chain rule applied to the composition \(\mathbb {R}\xrightarrow {t \mapsto t \cdot L} \mathrm{End}(M_{D}(\mathbb {C})) \xrightarrow {\exp } \mathrm{End}(M_{D}(\mathbb {C}))\).
▼

Theorem .8 Derivative at the origin

hasDerivAt_expSemigroupCLM_zerothm:hasDerivAt_expSemigroupCLM_ch12, thm:expSemigroupCLM_zero_ch12 One has

\[ \left.\frac{d}{dt} e^{tL}\right|_{t=0} = L. \]

▼
This is Theorem .7 at \(t=0\) together with Theorem .5.
▼

Theorem .9 Generator uniqueness

generator_uniquedef:exp_semigroup_ch12 If \(e^{tL} = e^{tL'}\) for all \(t \ge 0\), then \(L = L'\).

▼
Both semigroups agree on \([0,\infty )\), so their derivatives within \([0,\infty )\) at \(t=0\) agree. Since \([0,\infty )\) has unique differentials at \(0\), \(L = L'\).
▼

Theorem .10 CLM and linear-map forms coincide

expSemigroup_toCLMdef:exp_semigroup_ch12 The linear-map exponential semigroup and the continuous-linear-map exponential semigroup agree under the canonical identification of endomorphisms with continuous linear endomorphisms.

▼
This is a direct unpacking of the definition of the linear-map exponential semigroup.
▼

hasDerivAt_expSemigroup_applythm:hasDerivAt_expSemigroupCLM_ch12, thm:expSemigroup_toCLM_ch12 For every \(X \in M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) and every \(t \in \mathbb {R}\),

expSemigroup_zerothm:expSemigroup_toCLM_ch12, thm:expSemigroupCLM_zero_ch12 The linear-map exponential semigroup satisfies \(e^{0L} = \mathbb {1}\).

expSemigroup_isDynSemigroupdef:dyn_semigroup_ch12, thm:expSemigroup_toCLM_ch12, thm:expSemigroup_zero_ch12, thm:expSemigroupCLM_add_ch12 The family \(t \mapsto e^{tL}\) satisfies the semigroup law and the initial condition, hence is a dynamical semigroup.

Theorem .14 Every continuous semigroup is exponential

continuousDynSemigroup_eq_expdef:continuous_dyn_semigroup_ch12, def:exp_semigroup_ch12 Every norm-continuous dynamical semigroup \(T\) on \(M_{D}(\mathbb {C})\) is of the form

\[ T_t = e^{tL} \]

for some generator \(L \in \mathrm{End}_{\mathbb {C}}(M_{D}(\mathbb {C}))\) and every \(t \ge 0\). This is [ Wol12 , Proposition 7.1 ] .

▼
Since \(T_0 = \mathbb {1}\) and \(T\) is continuous, the integral \(M_\varepsilon = \int _0^\varepsilon T_s\, ds\) is invertible for small \(\varepsilon {\gt} 0\). Using the semigroup property, \(T_t = M_\varepsilon ^{-1}(M_{t+\varepsilon } - M_t)\) is differentiable for \(t \ge 0\). ODE uniqueness then gives \(T_t = e^{tL}\) for all \(t \ge 0\).
▼

Perturbation theory

Theorem .15 Duhamel formula

duhamel_formuladef:exp_semigroup_ch12, thm:hasDerivAt_expSemigroupCLM_ch12 Let \(\Delta = L' - L\). For \(t \ge 0\),

\[ e^{tL'} - e^{tL} = \int _0^t e^{(t-s)L} \Delta \, e^{sL'}\, ds. \]

This is [ Wol12 , Lem. 7.1 ] .

▼
Define \(f(s) = e^{(t-s)L} e^{sL'}\). Then \(f'(s) = e^{(t-s)L} \Delta \, e^{sL'}\) (Theorem .7), and \(e^{tL'} - e^{tL} = f(t) - f(0) = \int _0^t f'(s)\, ds\).
▼

Theorem .16 Perturbation bound

perturbation_boundthm:duhamel_formula_ch12 For \(t \ge 0\),

\[ \bigl\| e^{tL'} - e^{tL}\bigr\| \le t\, \| \Delta \| \cdot \sup _{s \in [0,t]}\| e^{sL}\| \cdot \sup _{s \in [0,t]}\| e^{sL'}\| , \]

where \(\Delta = L' - L\). This is [ Wol12 , Corollary 7.1 ] .

▼
Apply the triangle inequality and submultiplicativity of the operator norm to the Duhamel integral (Theorem .15).
▼

Corollary .17 Perturbation bound under unit-norm control

perturbation_bound_unit_normthm:perturbation_bound_ch12 For \(t \ge 0\), if \(\| e^{sL}\| \le 1\) and \(\| e^{sL'}\| \le 1\) for every \(s \in [0,t]\), then

\[ \| e^{tL'} - e^{tL}\| \le t\, \| L' - L\| . \]

▼
Apply Theorem .16 and bound both suprema by \(1\).
▼

Lemma .18 Dyson-series summability from factorial majorant

summable_dysonTerm_of_factorial_boundAssume a factorial majorant of Dyson–Phillips iterates:

\[ \| D_n(t)\| \le M\, \frac{(t\, \| L'-L\| \, M)^n}{n!}. \]

Then the Dyson series is summable in operator norm.

▼
This is the Weierstrass M-test step, using the scalar convergence of \(\sum _n a^n/n!\) and lifting via norm-bounded summability.
▼

Theorem .19 Factorial norm bound for Dyson iterates

norm_dysonTerm_ledef:exp_semigroup_ch12 For \(s \in [0,t]\) and \(M = \sup _{u \in [0,t]} \| e^{uL}\| \),

\[ \| \widetilde{T}^{(n)}_s\| \le M\, \frac{(s\, \| \Delta \| \, M)^n}{n!}. \]

▼
def:exp_semigroup_ch12 Induction on \(n\). The base case uses the semigroup supremum bound. The inductive step bounds the integrand pointwise by \(M\| \Delta \| \cdot M(u\| \Delta \| M)^n/n!\) and integrates \(\int _0^s u^n\, du = s^{n+1}/(n+1)\), recovering the factorial.
▼

Corollary .20 Dyson series summability

summable_dysonTermthm:norm_dysonTerm_le_ch12 The Dyson–Phillips series \(\sum _n \widetilde{T}^{(n)}_t\) converges in operator norm for every \(t \ge 0\).

▼
thm:norm_dysonTerm_le_ch12, lem:summable_dyson_factorial_ch12 Compose the factorial norm bound (Theorem .19) with the Weierstrass M-test step (Lemma .18).
▼

Corollary .21 Factorial norm bound at the final time

norm_dysonTerm_le_atthm:norm_dysonTerm_le_ch12 For \(t \ge 0\), setting \(s=t\) in Theorem .19 gives

\[ \| \widetilde{T}^{(n)}_t\| \le M\, \frac{(t\, \| \Delta \| \, M)^n}{n!}, \]

where \(M = \sup _{u \in [0,t]} \| e^{uL}\| \).

▼
This is the special case \(s=t\) of Theorem .19.
▼

Theorem .22 Continuity of Dyson iterates

dysonTerm_continuousdef:exp_semigroup_ch12 Each Dyson iterate \(t \mapsto \widetilde{T}^{(n)}_t\) is continuous in the time parameter.

▼
def:exp_semigroup_ch12 By induction on \(n\). The base case is continuity of the matrix exponential. For the inductive step, the parametric integral \(t\mapsto \int _0^t e^{(t-s)L}\, \Delta \, \widetilde{T}^{(n)}_s\, ds\) is continuous by the continuous-parameter primitive theorem; the pasting lemma extends this to all of \(\mathbb {R}\) since \(\widetilde{T}^{(n+1)}_t = 0\) for \(t \le 0\).
▼

Theorem .23 Factorial bound on the Dyson remainder

norm_dysonRemainder_lethm:duhamel_formula_ch12, thm:norm_dysonTerm_le_ch12 For \(s \in [0,t]\), \(M = \sup _{u \in [0,t]}\| e^{uL}\| \), and \(M' = \sup _{u \in [0,t]}\| e^{uL'}\| \):

\[ \bigl\| T'_s - \textstyle \sum _{n {\lt} N}\widetilde{T}^{(n)}_s\bigr\| \le M'\, \frac{(s\, \| \Delta \| \, M)^N}{N!}. \]

dyson_series_eqthm:norm_dysonRemainder_le_ch12, cor:summable_dysonTerm_ch12 For \(t \ge 0\), the Dyson–Phillips series equals the perturbed semigroup:

\[ \sum _{n=0}^{\infty } \widetilde{T}^{(n)}_t = e^{tL'}. \]

▼

Together with the summability of the series (Corollary .20), this identifies the sum via uniqueness of limits.

GKSL/Lindblad generators

This section characterizes generators of semigroups of completely positive and trace-preserving maps. The central result is the GKSL theorem (Theorem .54), which shows that such generators have the standard Lindblad form.

Generator decomposition and conditional complete positivity

Definition .26 Generator decomposition

GeneratorDecompA generator decomposition consists of a completely positive map \(\phi : M_{d}(\mathbb {C}) \to M_{d}(\mathbb {C})\) and a matrix \(\kappa \in M_{d}(\mathbb {C})\), defining the linear map

\[ L(\rho ) = \phi (\rho ) - \kappa \rho - \rho \kappa ^\dagger . \]

This is [ Wol12 , Equation (7.14) ] .

Definition .27 Conditional complete positivity

IsCCPdef:generator_decomp A linear map \(L : M_{d}(\mathbb {C}) \to M_{d}(\mathbb {C})\) is conditionally completely positive (CCP) if it admits a generator decomposition, i.e. there exist a CP map \(\phi \) and a matrix \(\kappa \) such that \(L(\rho ) = \phi (\rho ) - \kappa \rho - \rho \kappa ^\dagger \). This is condition 1 of [ Wol12 , Proposition 7.2 ] .

Definition .28 Trace-annihilating map

IsTraceAnnihilatingA linear map \(L : M_{d}(\mathbb {C}) \to M_{d}(\mathbb {C})\) is trace-annihilating if \(\tr (L(\rho )) = 0\) for all \(\rho \in M_{d}(\mathbb {C})\). This is the infinitesimal version of trace preservation.

Definition .29 Trace constraint

GeneratorDecomp.isTraceConstraintdef:generator_decomp A generator decomposition \((\phi , \kappa )\) satisfies the trace constraint if \(\phi ^*(\mathbb {1}) = \kappa + \kappa ^\dagger \), where \(\phi ^*\) is the Hilbert–Schmidt adjoint. This is the condition in [ Wol12 , Equation (7.20) ] .

Theorem .30 Trace constraint implies trace-annihilating

GeneratorDecomp.traceAnnihilating_of_traceConstraintdef:generator_decomp, def:is_trace_annihilating, def:is_trace_constraint If \((\phi , \kappa )\) satisfies \(\phi ^*(\mathbb {1}) = \kappa + \kappa ^\dagger \), then \(L(\rho ) = \phi (\rho ) - \kappa \rho - \rho \kappa ^\dagger \) is trace-annihilating.

▼
Let \(\phi (\rho ) = \sum _i K_i \rho K_i^\dagger \). By the cyclic property of trace, \(\tr (L(\rho )) = \tr ((\sum _i K_i^\dagger K_i)\rho ) - \tr (\kappa \rho ) - \tr (\kappa ^\dagger \rho ) = \tr ((\kappa + \kappa ^\dagger )\rho ) - \tr (\kappa \rho ) - \tr (\kappa ^\dagger \rho ) = 0\).
▼

The Lindblad form

Definition .31 Lindblad form

LindbladFormA Lindblad form consists of a Hermitian matrix \(H = H^\dagger \) (the Hamiltonian) and a family of matrices \(\{ L_j\} _{j=0}^{r-1}\) (the Lindblad operators), defining the linear map

\[ L(\rho ) = i[\rho , H] + \sum _{j} \Bigl( L_j \rho L_j^\dagger - \tfrac {1}{2}\{ L_j^\dagger L_j, \rho \} _+ \Bigr), \]

where \([A,B] = AB - BA\) and \(\{ A,B\} _+ = AB + BA\). This is [ Wol12 , Equation (7.21) ] .

Theorem .32 Lindblad form is trace-annihilating

LindbladForm.isTraceAnnihilatingdef:lindblad_form, def:is_trace_annihilating Every Lindblad form defines a trace-annihilating linear map.

▼
The Hamiltonian part satisfies \(\tr (i[\rho ,H]) = 0\) by the cyclic property. Each dissipator term satisfies \(\tr (L_j \rho L_j^\dagger ) = \tr (L_j^\dagger L_j \rho ) = \tr (\rho L_j^\dagger L_j)\), so the three terms sum to zero.
▼

Theorem .33 Lindblad form equals generator decomposition

LindbladForm.toLinearMap_eq_generatorDecompdef:lindblad_form, def:generator_decomp A Lindblad form with Hamiltonian \(H\) and operators \(\{ L_j\} \) defines the same linear map as the generator decomposition \((\phi , \kappa )\) with \(\phi (\rho ) = \sum _j L_j \rho L_j^\dagger \) and \(\kappa = iH + \frac{1}{2}\sum _j L_j^\dagger L_j\). This is [ Wol12 , Equation (7.24) ] .

▼
Compute \(\kappa ^\dagger = -iH + \frac{1}{2}\sum _j L_j^\dagger L_j\) using \(H^\dagger = H\) and \((A^\dagger A)^\dagger = A^\dagger A\). Setting \(S = \sum _j L_j^\dagger L_j\), expand \(\phi (\rho ) - \kappa \rho - \rho \kappa ^\dagger = \sum _j L_j\rho L_j^\dagger - iH\rho - \tfrac {1}{2}S\rho + i\rho H - \tfrac {1}{2}\rho S\), which is \(i[\rho ,H] + \sum _j(L_j\rho L_j^\dagger - \tfrac {1}{2}L_j^\dagger L_j\rho - \tfrac {1}{2}\rho L_j^\dagger L_j)\).
▼

Theorem .34 Lindblad form is CCP

LindbladForm.isCCPdef:lindblad_form, def:is_ccp Every Lindblad form defines a conditionally completely positive map.

▼
thm:lindbladForm_eq_generatorDecomp By Theorem .33, the Lindblad form equals a generator decomposition with \(\phi \) CP.
▼
