# Issue #905/#934 — selector-word Route B composition (2026-04-26)

## Scope

Wave 17 Slot E adds a composition layer only.  It does **not** prove the full
issue-#934 finite selector theorem

```lean
IsCanonicalFormBNT μ A → ∃ S, HasBlockSelectorWords A S
```

and it does **not** derive the wrapping/open-chain boundary representation.  The
new theorems say that, once those two inputs are supplied at the matching length,
the already-merged Route B block split no longer needs an explicit
`WordTupleSpanTop A m` hypothesis: `IsCanonicalFormBNT μ A` plus
`HasBlockSelectorWords A S` supplies `WordTupleSpanTop A (1 + S)` by the selector
composition from PR #937.

## Lean declarations added

File: `TNLean/MPS/ParentHamiltonian/DegenerateGS.lean`

- `MPSTensor.chainGroundSpace_toTensorFromBlocks_le_iSup_of_bntSelectorWords`
  - Hypotheses: `IsCanonicalFormBNT μ A`, `HasBlockSelectorWords A S`, `1 < L`,
    `N ≥ L + 1`, and a boundary matrix representation commuting with every
    length-`1 + S` assembled word.
  - Conclusion: the assembled chain ground space is contained in the supremum of
    blockwise chain ground spaces.

- `MPSTensor.chainGroundSpace_toTensorFromBlocks_eq_iSup_of_bntSelectorWords`
  - Adds the already-proved forward inclusion to the preceding reverse inclusion,
    yielding the conditional block-splitting equality.

- `MPSTensor.parentHamiltonianGroundSpace_le_bntSpan_of_bntSelectorWords`
  - Parent-ground-space form: the same selector-word and boundary hypotheses place
    the assembled parent ground space in `bntSpan A N`.

All three proofs are thin compositions of
`MPSTensor.wordTupleSpanTop_of_isCanonicalFormBNT_of_blockSelectorWords` with the
merged Route B theorems ending in `_of_wordTupleSpanTop`.  The positive length
input is `0 < 1 + S`.

`Papers/2011.12127/TN-Review-main.tex` lines 2123--2128:

> For any $N\ge 2L_0$, the ground space of any parent Hamiltonian is exactly the
> vector space generated by a basis of normal tensors of the initial tensor $A$.

and line 2126:

> Following the same steps as following Eq.~\eqref{eq:4:initiate-intersection-proof}
> (inverting and re-growing tensors, where the inverse projects on the space of
> block-diagonal matrices, and restricting to boundary conditions $L$ and $R$
> which are themselves block-diagonal) ...

The Lean/blueprint additions formalize only the conditional composition toward
this block-diagonal boundary route.  They do not replace the paper-level selector
existence theorem or the boundary/regrowing argument.
