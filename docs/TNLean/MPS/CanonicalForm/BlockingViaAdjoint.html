    Helper lemmas: adjoint eigenvalues and primitivity #

    We need a small bridge between the peripheral spectrum of a linear map and that of its adjoint. For finite-dimensional complex inner product spaces, eigenvalues of E.adjoint are complex conjugates of eigenvalues of E.

    We then specialize this to Matrix (Fin D) (Fin D) ℂ equipped with the Frobenius inner product (induced by the identity matrix).

    source
    theorem MPSTensor.Matrix.charpoly_conjTranspose {n : Type u_1} [Fintype n] [DecidableEq n] (M : Matrix n n ℂ) :
    M.conjTranspose.charpoly = Polynomial.map (starRingEnd ℂ) M.charpoly

    The characteristic polynomial of the conjugate transpose is the coefficient-wise complex conjugate of the characteristic polynomial.

    We phrase this using Polynomial.map along starRingEnd ℂ (complex conjugation on coefficients).

    source
    theorem MPSTensor.Module.End.hasEigenvalue_adjoint_iff {V : Type u_1} [NormedAddCommGroup V] [InnerProductSpace ℂ V] [FiniteDimensional ℂ V] (E : V →ₗ[ℂ] V) (μ : ℂ) :
    Module.End.HasEigenvalue E μ ↔ Module.End.HasEigenvalue (LinearMap.adjoint E) (star μ)

    Eigenvalues of the adjoint are complex conjugates.

    This is proved via characteristic polynomials, using an orthonormal basis and the fact that the matrix of the adjoint is the conjugate transpose of the matrix.

        Main theorem: periodicity removal by blocking #

        This is the maintained Appendix-A blocking argument. Starting from a left-canonical / trace-preserving tensor, we pass to the conjugate-transposed Kraus family, use the adjoint-fixed-point peripheral closure theorem, then take a common power and transport primitivity back across the adjoint.
