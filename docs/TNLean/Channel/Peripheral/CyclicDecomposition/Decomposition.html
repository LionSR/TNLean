    Cyclic decompositions for irreducible Schwarz maps #

    This file combines the peripheral-unitary and cyclic-projection constructions to produce the cyclic decomposition promised by Wolf Theorem 6.6.

    theorem MPSTensor.exists_cyclic_decomposition_of_irreducible_schwarz {r D m : ℕ} [NeZero D] [NeZero m] (K : Fin r → MatrixAlg D) (hUnital : KadisonSchwarz.IsUnitalKraus K) (ρ : MatrixAlg D) (hρ : Matrix.PosDef ρ) (hρfix : Kraus.adjointMap K ρ = ρ) (hIrr : IsIrreducibleMap (transferMap K)) {γ : ℂ} (hγprim : IsPrimitiveRoot γ m) (hperiph : peripheralEigenvalues (transferMap K) = Set.range fun (j : Fin m) => γ ^ ↑j) :
    ∃ (U : ↥(Matrix.unitaryGroup (Fin D) ℂ)) (P : Fin m → MatrixAlg D), (transferMap K) ↑U = γ • ↑U ∧ (∀ (k : ℕ), (transferMap K) (↑U ^ k) = γ ^ k • ↑U ^ k) ∧ ↑U ^ m = 1 ∧ (∀ (k : Fin m), IsOrthogonalProjection (P k)) ∧ ∑ k : Fin m, P k = 1 ∧ ↑U = ∑ k : Fin m, γ ^ ↑k • P k ∧ ∀ (k : Fin m), (transferMap K) (P (k + 1)) = P k

    Wolf Theorem 6.6 for transfer maps of irreducible unital Schwarz maps, assuming the peripheral spectrum is generated by a primitive m-th root γ.
