    Projection-triangular trace lemma #

    This file provides a coordinate-free version of the “drop strict off-diagonal blocks” lemma.

    Let $A = (A_i)_{i \in \mathrm{Fin}\,d}$ be a family of $D\times D$ matrices and let $P$ be an (orthogonal) projection. If $A$ is block upper-triangular with respect to $P$, in the sense that $$(1-P) A_i P = 0\quad\text{for all }i,$$ then replacing each matrix by its block-diagonal part $$A_i \mapsto P A_i P + (1-P) A_i (1-P)$$ does not change any MPV coefficients trace (evalWord _ w) and hence yields a tensor with the same MPV family.

    This lemma is used in canonical-form reduction: after finding an invariant subspace and putting the tensor in an upper-triangular form, we may “drop the off-diagonal blocks” without changing MPVs.

        theorem MPSTensor.evalWord_diagPart_eq {d D : ℕ} (A : MPSTensor d D) (P : Matrix (Fin D) (Fin D) ℂ) (hP : IsOrthogonalProjection P) (hLower : ∀ (i : Fin d), (1 - P) * A i * P = 0) (w : List (Fin d)) :
        (A.diagPart P).evalWord w = P * A.evalWord w * P + (1 - P) * A.evalWord w * (1 - P)

        Word evaluation of diagPart A P equals the sum of the two diagonal compressions of evalWord A w.

        Formally, with Q = 1 - P: evalWord (diagPart A P) w = P * evalWord A w * P + Q * evalWord A w * Q.

        The proof uses:

        • orthogonality relations P*(1-P)=0 and (1-P)*P=0 (from idempotence of P), and
        • stability of the “lower-left block is zero” condition under word evaluation (lowerZero_evalWord).

        theorem MPSTensor.trace_eq_trace_diag_of_proj {D : ℕ} (P : Matrix (Fin D) (Fin D) ℂ) (hP : IsOrthogonalProjection P) (M : Matrix (Fin D) (Fin D) ℂ) :
        M.trace = (P * M * P).trace + ((1 - P) * M * (1 - P)).trace

        Trace decomposition with respect to an idempotent projection P.

        If P is idempotent, then the trace of any matrix M equals the sum of the traces of its “diagonal blocks” with respect to P and its complement 1-P.

        This is the coordinate-free analogue of the usual fact that the trace of a block upper-triangular matrix is the sum of the traces of its diagonal blocks.
