[3] says:    CFII is one-sided: the CPM E_k is trace-preserving (Eq TP)

             It is NOT generally unital (E_k(I)≠I in general; rather
             E_k(Λ_k)=Λ_k). The "unital" property is that the ADJOINT
             map E_k^*(X) = Σ A^{i†}_k X A^i_k satisfies E_k^*(I) = I
             (which IS the TP condition on E_k). [3]'s E_k itself is
             unital only if Λ_k = c·I.

             The slides' block-embedding trick creates a DOUBLY STOCHASTIC
             channel K(Y) = Σ K_i Y K_i† with K_i = diag(A'_i, B'_i),
             which IS both unital and trace-preserving IF the A'_i, B'_i
             are already in DS gauge (Σ A'^{i†} A'_i = I AND E(I) = I).
             
             This works when A'_i are obtained by the similarity
             A'_i = Λ^{-1/2} A_i Λ^{1/2} (so that both TP and Λ=I hold),
             making E' genuinely doubly stochastic.

             trace-preserving" without explaining that this requires the
             extra gauge step Λ^{-1/2} · Λ^{1/2} to make E' doubly
             stochastic (not just CFII). In [3], the proof works with
             the one-sided CFII and Λ≠I.

PART C: WHAT TRULY REQUIRES REFERENCES OUTSIDE [3]
================================================================================

1. KADISON-SCHWARZ INEQUALITY AND MULTIPLICATIVE DOMAIN
   The entire spectral gap section (slides "The spectral gap proof at a glance"
   through "Equal-or-orthogonal: the complete dichotomy") uses the
   channel-theoretic proof route via Kadison-Schwarz inequality and
   multiplicative domain. [3] does NOT use this route at all. [3]'s proof
   of the same result (Lem equalMPS) uses direct Cauchy-Schwarz on
   Hilbert-Schmidt inner products weighted by the CFII fixed point Λ.
   
   The KS route requires Wolf [Wolf2012Quantum] or equivalent (e.g.,
   Paulsen's book, Choi 1974). This is the PRIMARY deviation from [3]
   and is inherent to the Lean formalization's proof strategy.

2. SINGLE-BLOCK FT VIA SKOLEM-NOETHER
   The algebraic route (trace pairing → multiplicativity → simplicity →
   Skolem-Noether) is from Pérez-García et al. 2007 [Perez-Garcia2007Matrix],
   not from [3]. [3]'s single-block result comes as a consequence of
   Lem (equalMPS). The Skolem-Noether theorem itself is a standard algebra
   result (Mathlib).

3. Π-ALGEBRA DECOMPOSITION
   The decomposition of automorphisms of Π = ∏ Mat_{D_k}(C) into block
   permutations + inner automorphisms is not in [3]. This is standard
   Wedderburn/Artin-Wedderburn theory. [3] achieves the same effect via
   the BNT framework (Prop char-BNT) without naming the Π-algebra.

4. NEWTON-GIRARD IDENTITIES
   Not in [3]. The slide correctly notes this is "not used in the main proof
   but provides an independent algebraic route." [3]'s equivalent is
   Lem (Lem:app_simple) which directly asserts equal power sums ⟹ equal
   coefficients (citing [Gemma] for the proof).

5. EVANS-HØEGH-KROHN (Evans1978Spectral)
   Cited for "spectral properties of positive maps." [3] cites [Fan92]
   (= Fannes-Nachtergaele-Werner 1992) and [David2006] for CP map spectral
   properties. Evans 1978 is an alternative source for the same material.

6. QUANTUM WIELANDT (Sanz2010Quantum)
   [3] cites the quantum Wielandt theorem as [Wieland] (line 332) — same
   content, the slide's citation [Sanz2010Quantum] is the actual paper.

7. BLOCKING INFRASTRUCTURE + COUNTEREXAMPLE
   The "naive Vandermonde fails" counterexample and the detailed blocking
   infrastructure are formalization-specific contributions not in [3].

SUMMARY: The spectral gap proof section (KS → HS contraction → eigenvalue
rigidity → gauge equivalence) is the largest portion of the slides that
CANNOT be sourced to [3]. This is a deliberate choice of proof route in
the Lean formalization. The rest of the proof architecture (CF definition,
block separation, BNT/FT conclusion) has clear [3] counterparts, though
with structural differences (peeling vs. eventual-LI; strict ordering vs.
