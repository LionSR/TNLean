# CPSV16 Appendix A Overlap-Decay Faithfulness Audit

Audit date: 2026-05-11

Source: `Papers/1606.00608/MPDO-22-12-17-2.tex`, Appendix A, lines
1080--1133 and 1170--1192.

Scope: issue #1571.  This note audits the overlap-decay and unit-overlap
statements used in the CPSV16 Section II fundamental-theorem realignment.
The purpose is to check that blueprint `\leanok` pairings do not claim a
source theorem with a stronger Lean hypothesis set.

## Source Passage

The source Lemma `equalMPS` states that two normal matrix product vectors
generated by normal tensors have self-overlap tending to `1`; their mixed
overlap modulus tends to either `0` or `1`; and in the unit-modulus case the
bond dimensions agree and the tensors are gauge-phase equivalent.  The proof
first gauges the tensors into CFII, then studies the mixed transfer operator.
The corollary `eqV` packages the zero-or-phase alternative, and `Lem1` turns
asymptotic orthonormality into eventual linear independence.

## Blueprint Consequence

No blueprint tag needs to be removed in this slice.  The Chapter 8 entries
for the unit-overlap gauge theorem and spectral-radius lemma explicitly state
the common-bond-dimension restriction.  The rectangular dimension theorem is
the source-faithful Lean statement for the missing dimension conclusion.

The remaining source-level gap is not overlap decay.  It is the assembly of
CPSV16 Theorem II.1 and Corollary II.2 after block matching: matching the
coefficient multisets and forming the global block gauge
`Y = \oplus_j (I_{r_j} \otimes Y_j)`.  That work remains tracked by #1559,
#1562, and #1566.
