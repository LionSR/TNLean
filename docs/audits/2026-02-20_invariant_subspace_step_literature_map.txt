━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

MAIN LEMMA
  `MPSTensor.exists_twoBlock_decomp_of_lowerZero`

Hypothesis (abstract invariant-subspace / lower-zero condition):
  • A : MPSTensor d D       (a family A₀,…,A_{d-1} of D×D complex matrices)
  • P : Matrix (Fin D) (Fin D) ℂ   (an orthogonal projection on ℂ^D)
  • hP : IsOrthogonalProjection P  (i.e. P is Hermitian and P² = P)
  • hLower : ∀ i, (1 − P) · A i · P = 0   (the "lower block" is zero for every letter)

Conclusion (explicit 2-block MPV-equivalence):
  ∃ n m : ℕ  with  n + m = D,
    A₁ : MPSTensor d n,
    A₂ : MPSTensor d m,
  such that
    SameMPV₂  A  (twoBlockTensor A₁ A₂)
  where twoBlockTensor A₁ A₂ is the block-diagonal tensor
    (twoBlockTensor A₁ A₂) i  =  blockDiag(A₁ i, A₂ i)   (as a (n+m)×(n+m) matrix).

MATHEMATICAL RESTATEMENT:
  Let {Aᵢ}ᵢ be a finite family of D×D complex matrices (an MPS tensor) and let
  P be an orthogonal projection on ℂ^D such that (I−P) Aᵢ P = 0 for all i.
  Then there exist n, m with n+m=D and families of n×n (resp. m×m) complex
  matrices {A¹ᵢ} and {A²ᵢ} such that for every system size N and every
  configuration σ : {0,…,N−1}→{0,…,d−1}:

    Tr(Aσ₀ ··· Aσ_{N−1}) = Tr(A¹σ₀ ··· A¹σ_{N−1}) + Tr(A²σ₀ ··· A²σ_{N−1}).

  In other words, the MPS partition-function amplitudes of A coincide with those
  of the block-diagonal tensor blockDiag(A¹ᵢ, A²ᵢ).

  The two sub-families {A¹ᵢ} and {A²ᵢ} are the compressions of the unitarily
  conjugated tensor to the eigenspaces of P corresponding to eigenvalue 1 and 0,
  respectively.

PROOF ROUTE IN LEAN (file InvariantSubspaceDecomp.lean):
  Step 1. Diagonalise the Hermitian projection P:
          let U = P.eigenvectorUnitary (from Mathlib.Analysis.Matrix.Spectrum).
          Define Pdiag = U† P U = diag(f), where f(j) ∈ {0,1} for all j.
  Step 2. Conjugate A by U to get Aconj i = U† Aᵢ U.
          Prove SameMPV A Aconj via `sameMPV_conj_unitary`
          (trace cyclicity cancels U U† = I at each boundary).
  Step 3. Show (1−Pdiag) · Aconj i · Pdiag = 0
          (conjugation transforms the lower-zero hypothesis from P to Pdiag).
  Step 4. Apply `sameMPV_diagPart_of_lowerZero`
          (from ProjectionTriangularTrace.lean): this drops the off-diagonal
          block, giving SameMPV Aconj (diagPart Aconj Pdiag).
  Step 5. Split the basis index set Fin D into S = {j | f(j)=1} ⊕ T = {j | f(j)=0}
          using the canonical equivalence eST : Fin D ≃ S ⊕ T.
  Step 6. Extract block tensors A₁ (the 1-eigenspace block) and A₂ (0-eigenspace).
  Step 7. Prove MPV(diagPart Aconj Pdiag)(σ) = MPV(A₁)(σ) + MPV(A₂)(σ)
          via `evalWord_fromBlocks_diag` + `trace_fromBlocks_diag`.
  Step 8. Prove MPV(twoBlockTensor A₁ A₂)(σ) = MPV(A₁)(σ) + MPV(A₂)(σ)
          via `mpv_toTensorFromBlocks_eq_sum` (μ ≡ 1, r = 2).
  Chain: A ~MPV Aconj ~MPV diagPart(Aconj,Pdiag) ~MPV twoBlockTensor(A₁,A₂).

HELPER LEMMAS (same literature step):
  • `sameMPV_conj_unitary`           (InvariantSubspaceDecomp.lean, line 167):
      Conjugating all letters by a unitary does not change any MPV amplitude.
      [Uses trace cyclicity: Tr(U†M U) = Tr(M U U†) = Tr(M)]
  • `sameMPV_diagPart_of_lowerZero`  (ProjectionTriangularTrace.lean):
      (1−P) Aᵢ P = 0 ⇒ SameMPV A (P Aᵢ P ⊕ (I−P) Aᵢ (I−P)).
      [The key off-diagonal-drop step; see discussion in §(B) below]
  • `mpv_toTensorFromBlocks_eq_sum`  (FundamentalTheoremMulti.lean):
      MPV of a block-diagonal tensor = weighted sum of per-block MPVs.
      [Algebraic identity, no literature citation needed]

DEVIATION ANALYSIS (Perez-Garcia et al. vs. Lean):

  1. How the invariant projection is obtained.  The Perez-Garcia proof does NOT
     take P as a hypothesis; instead it constructs P from a rank-deficient
     positive fixed point X of the transfer map E(·) = Σᵢ Aᵢ (·) Aᵢ†, taking
     P_R to be the orthogonal projection onto supp(X).  It then argues that
     the invariance condition Aᵢ P_R = P_R Aᵢ P_R follows from the positivity
     of X and the channel equation X = Σᵢ Aᵢ X Aᵢ†.  The Lean lemma assumes
     (I−P) Aᵢ P = 0 as an abstract hypothesis (the invariant-subspace condition
     is given, not derived), making it strictly more general.

  2. Unitary conjugation route.  Perez-Garcia's proof uses a unitary U only at
     the very end (to diagonalise the right fixed point Λ).  The block-splitting
     step itself is done without explicit diagonalisation of P, using instead a
     direct trace-split algebraic argument.  The Lean proof uses unitary
     conjugation via the spectral theorem of Hermitian matrices (P = U diag(f) U†)
     as a first step, converting the abstract projection into an explicit
     diagonal one.  Both routes are mathematically equivalent but structurally
     different.

  3. Single step vs. full induction.  The paper's proof is a full inductive
     argument that iterates the two-block step until no further non-trivial
     invariant subspace exists.  The Lean lemma formalises exactly one step
     (one projector → two blocks), leaving the iteration to the caller.

DEVIATION ANALYSIS (Cirac et al. 1606.00608 vs. Lean):

  1. Single step vs. multi-step.  Section 2.3 describes an iterative procedure:
     given A, find an invariant subspace S₁ not containing any further invariant
     subspace, split, then recurse on Q₁ B Q₁ to find S₂ ⊆ S₁⊥, and so on,
     until after r steps there is no non-trivial invariant subspace left.  The
     Lean lemma does exactly one step: given one invariant projection P, output
     two blocks. The iterative structure is left to the caller.

  2. Irreducibility of resulting blocks.  The paper's iteration terminates at
     "normal tensors" (no non-trivial invariant subspace, unique peripheral
     eigenvalue 1).  The Lean lemma makes no irreducibility claim about A₁ or A₂.

  3. Orthogonality of P vs. existence of P.  The Lean proof takes P as an
     abstract orthogonal projector and starts immediately with the spectral
     theorem (U diagonalises P).  Cirac et al. construct P₁ as the orthogonal
     projector onto the smallest invariant subspace; the paper does not note
     that this P₁ is automatically orthogonal in the sense (I−P₁) B P₁ = 0
     (it is, because S₁⊥ has no B-invariant component within S₁, but this is
     asserted informally).  The Lean condition `(1-P) * A i * P = 0` is exactly
     `Q B P = 0`, which is the form used on TeX line 203: `Q₁ Bⁱ = Q₁ Bⁱ Q₁`.

DEVIATION ANALYSIS (De las Cuevas et al. 1708.00029 vs. Lean):

  1. Periodic vs. normal blocks.  The novel contribution of 1708.00029 is the
     "irreducible form" where each block A_j may be *periodic* (the transfer map
     E_{A_j} may have other eigenvalues of modulus 1 besides 1, so long as they
     are exactly the m_j-th roots of unity for some period m_j).  The Lean lemma
     does not require normality or aperiodicity of the resulting blocks, so Lean's
     version is compatible with both normal (Cirac 2017) and irreducible (De las
     Cuevas 2017) notions.

  2. The paper's main theorem.  The fundamental theorem of 1708.00029 (Theorem 1,
     giving a gauge relation ZA = YBY⁻¹ between tensors in irreducible form) is
     a more general version of the fundamental theorem for canonical forms.  The
     Lean project currently formalises the canonical-form version; 1708.00029 is
     the reference for extending to the periodic/irreducible case.

  3. Attribution.  The paper explicitly credits the invariant-subspace step to
     [Ci15] (1606.00608), making it clear that the block-splitting procedure is
     not a new contribution of 1708.00029 but a known construction adapted from
     the canonical-form literature.

CLOSEST CONTENT IN THE WOLF NOTES:

  (a) Chapter 6, Section 6.2 "Irreducible maps and Perron-Frobenius theory"
      (ch06_spectral_properties.pdf, p. 99–104; 1701036.pdf lines ~6270–6400).
      Theorem 6.2 (Irreducible positive maps): states that a positive map T is
      irreducible iff "If P is a Hermitian projector such that T(P Md P) ⊆ P Md P,
      then P ∈ {0,1}."
      This defines what it means for the transfer map to have NO non-trivial
      invariant subspace, which is the NEGATION of the hypothesis of the Lean
      lemma. The Wolf notes define irreducibility but do not prove the block-
      diagonal decomposition for reducible maps in the MPS/MPV context.

  (b) Section 7.1 "Continuous one-parameter semigroups" (1701036.pdf, lines ~7365–7440),
      Proposition 7.6 (Reducible quantum dynamical semigroups):
      "There is a Hermitian projector P s.t. for all t≥0: Tₜ(P Md P) ⊆ P Md P
       ⟺ There is a basis in which all Kraus operators Lⱼ and κ are block-upper-
       triangular, i.e., (1−P)Lⱼ P = (1−P)κP = 0 for some Hermitian projector P."
      This is formally analogous to the Lean hypothesis `(1-P) * A i * P = 0`
      but is in the SEMIGROUP / LINDBLAD GENERATOR context (continuous-time),
      not the MPS trace / MPV context.  The conclusion is about block-upper-
      triangular Kraus operators, not about MPV equivalence after dropping the
      off-diagonal block.  The trace-based MPV identity (SameMPV A diagPart(A,P))
      does not appear in the Wolf notes.

  CONCLUSION: The Wolf lecture notes are a standard reference for the definition
  of irreducibility of positive maps (Theorem 6.2, which underlies the concept
  of "normal tensor" = irreducible + primitive transfer map), but they do NOT
  contain the MPV-equivalence (invariant subspace → drop off-diagonal → same
  MPV amplitudes) that the Lean lemma formalises.  Do not cite Wolf for this step.

      the Lean conclusion: the diagonal-block restriction ⊕ₖ Pₖ Bⁱ Pₖ generates
      the same MPV family.  The Lean lemma formalises the r=2 single-step case.
    • Both 1708.00029 and the broader MPS community cite 1606.00608 (Section 2.3)
