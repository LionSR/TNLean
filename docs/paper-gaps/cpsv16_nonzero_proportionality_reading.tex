\section{Source phrase}

Cirac--P\'erez-Garc\'ia--Schuch--Verstraete state Theorem~\texttt{thm1}
in \cite{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv}, lines~1170--1192, for tensors whose
matrix-product vectors are ``proportional to each other''.  The displayed
source statement does not spell out whether the proportionality scalar at a
fixed length is required to be nonzero.

\section{Formal reading}

There are two possible formal readings of the phrase.  The weak scalar-multiple
reading is
\[
  \forall N,\ \exists c_N\in\mathbb C,\qquad
  V_A^{(N)}=c_N V_B^{(N)} .
  \tag{W}
\]
The projective reading is
\[
  \forall N,\ \exists c_N\in\mathbb C^\times,\qquad
  V_A^{(N)}=c_N V_B^{(N)} .
  \tag{P}
\]
The formal predicate \leanid{NonzeroProportionalMPV₂} uses \((P)\).  Thus the
scalar \(c_N\) is a point of \(\mathbb C^\times\), not merely a point of
\(\mathbb C\).

This distinction is used exactly in the block-selection step of
Theorem~\texttt{thm1}.  With BNT decompositions written
\[
  V_A^{(N)}
  =
  \sum_{j=1}^{g_A}\mu_j^N a_j(N),
  \qquad
  V_B^{(N)}
  =
  \sum_{\ell=1}^{g_B}\nu_\ell^N b_\ell(N),
  \tag{BNT}
\]
the proportionality identity becomes
\[
  \sum_{j=1}^{g_A}\mu_j^N a_j(N)
  =
  c_N\sum_{\ell=1}^{g_B}\nu_\ell^N b_\ell(N).
  \tag{$E_N$}
\]
In the no-tail fixed-block contradiction, for instance, \((E_N)\) reduces to
\[
  \sum_{j=1}^{g_A}\mu_j^N a_j(N)-c_N\nu_k^N b_k(N)=0.
  \tag{$C_k$}
\]
The linear-independence input then forces the selected coefficient to vanish:
\[
  c_N\nu_k^N=0.
  \tag{$Z_k$}
\]
The contradiction is
\[
  c_N\ne0,\qquad \nu_k\ne0
  \quad\Longrightarrow\quad
  c_N\nu_k^N\ne0,
  \tag{$NZ_k$}
\]
which is unavailable from the weak reading \((W)\).  If \(c_N=0\), the identity
\[
  V_A^{(N)}=0\cdot V_B^{(N)}
  \tag{0}
\]
does not identify projective MPV states and does not contradict \((Z_k)\).

Under the canonical-form hypotheses used in the source argument, a selected
normal block has
\[
  \|b_k(N)\|^2=\langle b_k(N),b_k(N)\rangle\longrightarrow 1,
  \tag{N}
\]
hence \(b_k(N)\ne0\) for all sufficiently large \(N\).  In the range in which
the source performs the block contradiction, the proportionality being used is
therefore projective proportionality of nonzero vectors.  The formal predicate
records this use-site reading, rather than adding a separate asymptotic
hypothesis to Theorem~\texttt{thm1}.

\section{Effect on the theorem}

The nonzero condition changes the formal predicate from \((W)\) to \((P)\), but
it does not change the intended conclusion of the source theorem.  It records
the conventional mathematical meaning of proportional nonzero vectors in the
source argument.  The weaker scalar-multiple predicate
\leanid{ProportionalMPV₂}, where \(c_N\) may vanish, remains available for
older auxiliary lemmas, but it is not used as the paper-faithful hypothesis for
the proportional Fundamental Theorem.

\section{Elimination plan}

No extra mathematical theorem is needed for the present projective reading of
CPSV16 lines~1170--1192.  If a literal weak-scalar reading is later required,
it should be formalized as a separate intermediate theorem.  The mathematical content
would be
\[
  (W)
  \quad+\quad
  V_A^{(N)}\ne0
  \quad+\quad
  V_B^{(N)}\ne0
  \quad\Longrightarrow\quad
  c_N\ne0
  \tag{Bridge}
\]
at the lengths used in the argument.  After this step one can derive
\leanid{NonzeroProportionalMPV₂} from the weak predicate before applying the
block-selection proof.
