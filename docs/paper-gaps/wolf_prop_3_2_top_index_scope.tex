\section{Source statement}

Proposition~3.2 of Wolf's \emph{Quantum Channels \& Operations}
(\path{Notes/WolfNoteTexSource/ch03_positive_not_completely.tex}, lines
153--179) bounds the expectation of a Hermitian operator
$\tau\in\mathcal{M}_{D'}\otimes\mathcal{M}_{D}$ in vectors of fixed Schmidt
rank. Writing $\tau=\sum_i\nu_i|\phi_i\rangle\langle\phi_i|$ with normalized
eigenvectors, $\rho_i=\operatorname{tr}_2|\phi_i\rangle\langle\phi_i|$, $\nu_0$
the smallest positive eigenvalue and $\nu$ the largest one, the infimum over
normalized vectors $\psi$ of Schmidt rank $n$ obeys
\[
  \inf_{\psi}\langle\psi|\tau|\psi\rangle
  \ge \nu_0 + \sum_{i:\nu_i\le 0}(\nu_i-\nu_0)\|\rho_i\|_{(n)},
\]
and, when a single eigenvalue $\nu_-$ is non-positive,
\[
  \inf_{\psi}\langle\psi|\tau|\psi\rangle
  \le \nu + (\nu_- - \nu)\|\rho_-\|_{(n)}.
\]
The Schmidt rank $n$ ranges over $1\le n\le D$, the dimension of the first
tensor factor, so the value $n=D$ is within the source's scope.

\section{Formalized statement}

The Lean theorems \path{Matrix.IsHermitian.spectral_lower_bound} and
\path{Matrix.IsHermitian.exists_le_spectral_upper_bound} establish these
bounds, in the per-vector form (every Schmidt-rank-$n$ vector satisfies the
lower bound; some Schmidt-rank-$n$ vector realizes the upper bound), under the
hypotheses $1\le n$ and $n<D$.

\section{The gap}

The formalization omits the top index $n=D$. At $n=D$ the Schmidt-rank
constraint is vacuous: every normalized vector qualifies, $\|\rho_i\|_{(D)}
=\operatorname{tr}\rho_i=1$, and both bounds collapse to the elementary
estimate $\nu_{\min}\le\langle\psi|\tau|\psi\rangle\le\nu_{\max}$.

The restriction is inherited from the maximal-overlap lemma
\path{Matrix.maximalSchmidtOverlap_eq_kyFanNorm}, whose own top-index gap is
recorded in \path{docs/paper-gaps/wolf_lemma_3_1_top_index_scope.tex}: that
lemma supplies the per-eigenvector overlap bound
$|\langle\phi_i|\psi\rangle|^2\le\|\rho_i\|_{(n)}$ only for $n<D$, because the
underlying Ky-Fan maximum principle
\path{Matrix.IsHermitian.kyFanNorm_eq_sup_trace} is stated for
rank-exactly-$n$ orthogonal projections with $n<D$.

\section{Elimination plan}

Extend the maximal-overlap lemma to the top index $n=D$ (its own elimination
plan adds the $k=D$ case to the Ky-Fan maximum principle, where the maximizing
projection is the identity). The spectral bounds then extend to $1\le n\le D$
by feeding that case into the same per-eigenvector overlap step; the algebraic
cores \path{Matrix.IsHermitian.spectral_lower_bound_core} and
\path{Matrix.IsHermitian.spectral_upper_bound_core} are already index-agnostic.
