\section{The source statement}

Cirac--P\'erez-Garc\'ia--Schuch--Verstraete 2016 establishes the following
structural lemma about overlaps of normal matrix product vectors
\cite[Lemma~\texttt{equalMPS}, line~1081]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv}:

\begin{quote}
    Given two NMPV $V_{a,b}$ generated by two NT with corresponding
    $D_\alpha\times D_\alpha$ matrices $A_\alpha^i$ ($\alpha=a,b$):
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item $\lim_{N\to\infty}\langle V_\alpha\,|\,V_\alpha\rangle = 1$,
        \item $\lim_{N\to\infty}|\langle V_b\,|\,V_a\rangle| = 0$ or $1$.
    \end{enumerate}
    In the latter case, $D_a=D_b$ and there exists a non-singular matrix $X$
    and a phase $\phi$ such that $A_b^i = e^{i\phi} X A_a^i X^{-1}$.
\end{quote}

The hypothesis is just that the two tensors are NT.  The conclusion has three
pieces: convergence of the self-overlap, the $0/1$ dichotomy, and a
\emph{structural gauge recovery} in the $1$ case.  No proportionality
hypothesis on the MPVs is required.

The proof in \cite[lines~1093--1117]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv} goes through the
cross-transfer-matrix
\[
    \mathbb E_{ab} = \sum_{i=1}^d A_a^i \otimes \overline{A_b^i},
\]
analysing its eigenvalue equation $\sum_i (A_b^i)^\dagger X A_a^i = \lambda X$
together with the trace-preserving relation and the strictly-positive
spectral fixed point $\Lambda_a$.  Cauchy--Schwarz applied to the resulting
trace inequality forces $|\lambda|<1$ or $|\lambda|=1$, and the latter case
yields $A_b^i X = \alpha X A_a^i$ with $|\alpha|=1$ and $X$ an isometry,
which after a dimension argument gives the structural conclusion.

\section{The Lean situation}

The TNLean codebase has the dichotomy and the same-bond-dimension gauge
recovery, split into multiple theorems.

The self-overlap convergence is faithfully formalized in
\leanid{MPSTensor.overlap\_tendsto\_one\_of\_peripheralPrimitive\_of\_irreducible}.\footnote{%
\path{TNLean/MPS/Overlap/PeripheralToSpectralGap.lean}, around line~506.}
The dichotomy in the decay direction is faithfully formalized in
\leanid{MPSTensor.mpvOverlap\_tendsto\_zero\_of\_irreducible\_TP}.\footnote{%
\path{TNLean/Spectral/SpectralGapNT.lean}, around line~290.}

There are now two gauge-recovery theorems.  The older theorem
\leanid{MPSTensor.gaugePhaseEquiv\_of\_proportionalMPV₂\_of\_overlap\_tendsto\_one\_of\_irreducible\_TP}\footnote{%
\path{TNLean/MPS/FundamentalTheorem/Proportional.lean}, around line~202.}
requires \leanid{ProportionalMPV₂}; it remains a correct proportionality-based
corollary.  The newer theorem
\leanid{MPSTensor.gaugePhaseEquiv\_of\_overlap\_norm\_tendsto\_one\_of\_irreducible\_TP}\footnote{%
\path{TNLean/MPS/FundamentalTheorem/Proportional.lean}, around line~292.}
removes that hypothesis: for two irreducible trace-preserving tensors of the
same bond dimension, the assumption
$\|\langle V^{(N)}(A)\,|\,V^{(N)}(B)\rangle\|\to 1$ implies
\leanid{GaugePhaseEquiv}.  Thus the proportionality-hypothesis deviation is
closed for the same-bond-dimension case.

The remaining discrepancy is the bond dimension.  The source concludes
$D_a=D_b$ in the $1$ case.  The current same-dimension theorem assumes a
common bond dimension at the outset, because \leanid{GaugePhaseEquiv} is a
same-bond-dimension predicate.

\section{Origin of the residual gap}

The proportionality-based theorem extracts the gauge-phase structure from MPV
proportionality plus overlap-decay control.  That route is mathematically
valid, but it is a specialization rather than the source's unconditional
gauge-recovery direction.

The newer same-dimension theorem follows the source's cross-transfer-matrix
route in the equal-dimension setting.  The missing part is the rectangular
dimension argument from the end of \cite[lines~1093--1117]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv}:
once the limiting case gives an isometry between the bond spaces, the normality
of the larger tensor rules out a proper kernel and forces the two bond
dimensions to be equal.

\section{Why this matters for the Fundamental Theorem proof}

The proof of Theorem~\texttt{thm1}
\cite[lines~1170--1192]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv} uses the dichotomy and the gauge
recovery on \emph{per-block} overlaps
$\langle V^{(N)}(B_k)\,|\,V^{(N)}(A_j)\rangle$.  The setup provides
proportionality of the \emph{total} tensors,
$V^{(N)}(A_{\mathrm{tot}}) = c_N V^{(N)}(B_{\mathrm{tot}})$, but not of the
individual blocks.

The proportionality-free same-dimension theorem is enough once equality of the
two relevant block dimensions is known.  If the proof reaches the gauge
recovery before establishing that equality, the rectangular dimension-recovery
direction is still needed.

\section{Resolution paths}

\paragraph{Completed: same-dimension \texttt{equalMPS} without proportionality.}
The formalization now derives the cross-transfer spectral-radius condition
from asymptotically unit-modulus overlap and then applies the existing
modulus-one rigidity theorem.  This proves the source's gauge recovery for
equal bond dimensions and does not assume MPV proportionality.

\paragraph{Remaining: rectangular dimension recovery.}
The remaining task is to formalize the argument that the $1$ case forces
$D_a=D_b$ before applying the same-dimension gauge-phase theorem.  This is the
content tracked by the rectangular dimension-recovery issue.

\section{Status: closure of the same-dimension gap}

The same-bond-dimension half of the gap is now closed.  The Lean theorem
\leanid{MPSTensor.gaugePhaseEquiv\_of\_overlap\_norm\_tendsto\_one\_of\_irreducible\_TP},
introduced in
\path{TNLean/MPS/FundamentalTheorem/Proportional.lean}, takes only the
hypotheses present in
\cite[\texttt{equalMPS}]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv} for that direction:
irreducibility, the trace-preserving relation, and
$\|\langle V^{(N)}(A)\,|\,V^{(N)}(B)\rangle\|\to 1$.  The theorem
\leanid{MPSTensor.mixedTransferSpectralRadius\_ge\_one\_of\_mpvOverlap\_norm\_tendsto\_one}
shows that this hypothesis forces the cross-transfer spectral radius
$\ge 1$, after which the existing rigidity theorem
\leanid{MPSTensor.modulus\_one\_eigenvalue\_implies\_gauge\_of\_irreducible\_TP}
in \path{TNLean/Spectral/SpectralGapNT.lean} delivers the gauge-phase
equivalence.  The cross-transfer-matrix Cauchy--Schwarz argument from
\cite[lines~1093--1117]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv} was already encoded inside
that rigidity theorem; only the transit from
``$|\text{overlap}|\to 1$'' (the source hypothesis) to ``spectral
radius $\ge 1$'' (the existing theorem's hypothesis form) was missing,
and that transit is the contrapositive of an existing decay theorem
chained through \leanid{trace\_mixedTransferMap\_pow\_eq\_mpvOverlap}.
No proportionality hypothesis is required.

The remaining residual is the \emph{rectangular} direction: CPSV16's
\texttt{equalMPS} also concludes $D_a = D_b$ when
$\|\langle V_b\,|\,V_a\rangle\|\to 1$.  The Lean theorem above presupposes
$D_a = D_b$ because \leanid{GaugePhaseEquiv} is a same-bond-dimension
predicate.  The dimension-recovery direction (deriving $D_a = D_b$ from
asymptotic unit-modulus overlap of tensors with possibly different bond
dimensions) is not yet formalized; \cite[lines~1117--1119]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv}
gives the dimension argument as a short consequence of $X$ being an
isometry, and the Lean codebase has the rectangular mixed transfer map
\leanid{mixedTransferMap₂} but not the dimension-equality conclusion.

\section{Conclusion}

The same-bond-dimension half of the gap is closed.  The Lean codebase
now contains the source-faithful gauge-phase recovery from
$\|\text{overlap}\|\to 1$ alone, matching the conclusion of
\cite[\texttt{equalMPS}]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv} when the bond dimensions
agree.  The dimension-recovery direction remains open and is the
remaining residual of this paper-gap; it is the rectangular analogue
of the cross-transfer-matrix argument and is tracked separately.

For the proof of Theorem~\texttt{thm1}, the same-bond-dimension version
suffices in any setting where $D_a = D_b$ is established before the
gauge recovery is invoked; otherwise the rectangular dimension-recovery
direction must be formalized first.
