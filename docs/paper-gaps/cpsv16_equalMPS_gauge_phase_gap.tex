\section{The source statement}

Cirac--P\'erez-Garc\'ia--Schuch--Verstraete 2016 establishes the following
structural lemma about overlaps of normal matrix product vectors
\cite[Lemma~\texttt{equalMPS}, line~1081]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv}:

\begin{quote}
    Given two NMPV $V_{a,b}$ generated by two NT with corresponding
    $D_\alpha\times D_\alpha$ matrices $A_\alpha^i$ ($\alpha=a,b$):
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item $\lim_{N\to\infty}\langle V_\alpha\,|\,V_\alpha\rangle = 1$,
        \item $\lim_{N\to\infty}|\langle V_b\,|\,V_a\rangle| = 0$ or $1$.
    \end{enumerate}
    In the latter case, $D_a=D_b$ and there exists a non-singular matrix $X$
    and a phase $\phi$ such that $A_b^i = e^{i\phi} X A_a^i X^{-1}$.
\end{quote}

The hypothesis is just that the two tensors are NT.  The conclusion has three
pieces: convergence of the self-overlap, the $0/1$ dichotomy, and a
\emph{structural gauge recovery} in the $1$ case.  No proportionality
hypothesis on the MPVs is required.

The proof in \cite[lines~1085--1117]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv} goes through the
cross-transfer-matrix
\[
    \mathbb E_{ab} = \sum_{i=1}^d A_a^i \otimes \overline{A_b^i},
\]
analysing its eigenvalue equation $\sum_i (A_b^i)^\dagger X A_a^i = \lambda X$
together with the trace-preserving relation and the strictly-positive
spectral fixed point $\Lambda_a$.  Cauchy--Schwarz applied to the resulting
trace inequality forces $|\lambda|<1$ or $|\lambda|=1$, and the latter case
yields $A_b^i X = \alpha X A_a^i$ with $|\alpha|=1$ and $X$ an isometry,
which after a dimension argument gives the structural conclusion.

\section{The Lean situation}

The TNLean codebase has the dichotomy and the gauge recovery, split into
multiple theorems and with one extra hypothesis.

The self-overlap convergence is faithfully formalized in
\leanid{MPSTensor.overlap_tendsto_one_of_peripheralPrimitive_of_irreducible}.\footnote{%
\path{TNLean/MPS/Overlap/PeripheralToSpectralGap.lean}, around line~506.}
The dichotomy in the decay direction is faithfully formalized in
\leanid{MPSTensor.mpvOverlap_tendsto_zero_of_irreducible_TP}.\footnote{%
\path{TNLean/Spectral/SpectralGapNT.lean}, around line~290.}

The structural gauge recovery in the $1$ case is the place where the gap
appears.  The available Lean theorem is
\leanid{MPSTensor.gaugePhaseEquiv_of_proportionalMPV2_of_overlap_tendsto_one_of_irreducible_TP},\footnote{%
\path{TNLean/MPS/FundamentalTheorem/Proportional.lean}, around line~202.}
which concludes \leanid{GaugePhaseEquiv} (i.e.\ the existence of an invertible
$X$ and a nonzero scalar $\zeta$ with $B^i = \zeta\,X A^i X^{-1}$ for all $i$),
but requires \leanid{ProportionalMPV2} as an additional hypothesis.

In short:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Source: ``$|\langle V_b\,|\,V_a\rangle|\to 1$ implies gauge-phase
        equivalence.''
    \item Lean: ``$|\langle V_b\,|\,V_a\rangle|\to 1$ \emph{together with}
        \leanid{ProportionalMPV2} implies gauge-phase equivalence.''
\end{itemize}

The Lean implication is strictly weaker.  It is consistent with the source's
conclusion under the additional proportionality hypothesis, but does not
recover the unconditional gauge-recovery direction of
\cite[\texttt{equalMPS}]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv}.

\section{Origin of the gap}

The Lean route to the gauge recovery is not the cross-transfer-matrix
eigenvalue analysis from \cite[\texttt{equalMPS}]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv}.
It instead uses
\leanid{MPSTensor.gaugePhaseEquiv_of_eventually_proportionalMPV2_of_overlap_decay},
which extracts the gauge-phase structure from MPV proportionality plus
overlap-decay control.  That route is mathematically valid and the
proportionality hypothesis is genuinely consumed in the argument, so the
formalized theorem is correct.  It is, however, a different theorem from
the source's \texttt{equalMPS}.

\section{Why this matters for the Fundamental Theorem proof}

The proof of Theorem~\texttt{thm1}
\cite[lines~1170--1192]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv} uses the dichotomy and the gauge
recovery on \emph{per-block} overlaps $\langle V^{(N)}(B_k)\,|\,V^{(N)}(A_j)\rangle$.
The setup of \texttt{thm1} provides proportionality of the \emph{total}
tensors, $V^{(N)}(A_{\mathrm{tot}}) = c_N V^{(N)}(B_{\mathrm{tot}})$, but not
of the individual blocks.  Applying the available Lean theorem at the
per-block level therefore demands a per-block
\leanid{ProportionalMPV2} input that the source-faithful argument does not
require and the surrounding setup does not directly supply.

Concretely: the source argument extracts gauge-phase equivalence between $B_k$
and some $A_{j_k}$ from the overlap dichotomy alone.  The Lean argument
needs the additional hypothesis that $V^{(N)}(B_k)$ and $V^{(N)}(A_{j_k})$
are themselves proportional MPVs, which is what the source proof is in the
process of establishing.

\section{Resolution paths}

\paragraph{Option~A: formalize \texttt{equalMPS} without the
proportionality hypothesis.}
This requires the cross-transfer-matrix Cauchy--Schwarz argument from
\cite[lines~1085--1117]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv}.  The proof is contained, uses
the trace-preserving relation, the strictly-positive fixed point of the
self-CPM, and a Cauchy--Schwarz inequality on the trace; it does not depend
on any proportionality hypothesis.  After this lemma is in place, the per-block
gauge recovery in the proof of Theorem~\texttt{thm1} is direct from the
overlap dichotomy.

\paragraph{Option~B: derive the per-block proportionality bridge inside
the Theorem~\texttt{thm1} proof.}
Under the hypotheses of Theorem~\texttt{thm1} one can attempt to show
that the per-block overlap $\langle V^{(N)}(B_k)\,|\,V^{(N)}(A_{j_k})\rangle\to 1$
together with the BNT block-orthonormality data imply per-block
proportionality $V^{(N)}(B_k) = c'_N V^{(N)}(A_{j_k})$.  This is plausible
because asymptotic orthonormality concentrates $V^{(N)}(B_k)$ and
$V^{(N)}(A_{j_k})$ on the same direction in the limit, but turning that
asymptotic concentration into a per-$N$ proportionality requires extra
analysis that is not present in
\cite[lines~1170--1192]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv}.

\paragraph{Option~C: use the Lean theorem within a different overall
proof structure.}
The proportionality-requiring Lean theorem is sufficient when the
surrounding argument can produce per-block proportionality through some
other route.  This would be a genuine deviation from the source's proof
sketch.

Option~A is the source-faithful resolution and is preferred.

\section{Conclusion}

The Lean codebase formalizes the dichotomy of \cite[\texttt{equalMPS}]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv}
together with a proportionality-conditional gauge recovery.  The
unconditional structural gauge recovery from
\cite[\texttt{equalMPS}, lines~1085--1117]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv} is not
formalized.  This omission propagates into the proof structure of
Theorem~\texttt{thm1}, where per-block gauge-phase equivalence is
needed without per-block proportionality.

The verdict is a genuine source-deviation: the Lean version requires an
extra hypothesis that the source proof does not.  The recommended fix is
to formalize the source's cross-transfer-matrix eigenvalue argument as a
separate Lean theorem and use it in place of the proportionality-requiring
form.
