\section{The source statement}

Cirac--P\'erez-Garc\'ia--Schuch--Verstraete 2016 establishes the following
structural lemma about overlaps of normal matrix product vectors
\cite[line~1081]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv}:

\begin{quote}
    Given two NMPV $V_{a,b}$ generated by two NT with corresponding
    $D_\alpha\times D_\alpha$ matrices $A_\alpha^i$ ($\alpha=a,b$):
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item $\lim_{N\to\infty}\langle V_\alpha\,|\,V_\alpha\rangle = 1$,
        \item $\lim_{N\to\infty}|\langle V_b\,|\,V_a\rangle| = 0$ or $1$.
    \end{enumerate}
    In the latter case, $D_a=D_b$ and there exists a non-singular matrix $X$
    and a phase $\phi$ such that $A_b^i = e^{i\phi} X A_a^i X^{-1}$.
\end{quote}

The hypothesis is just that the two tensors are NT.  The conclusion has three
pieces: convergence of the self-overlap, the $0/1$ dichotomy, and a
\emph{structural gauge recovery} in the $1$ case.  No proportionality
hypothesis on the MPVs is required.

The proof in \cite[lines~1093--1117]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv} goes through the
cross-transfer-matrix
\[
    \mathbb E_{ab} = \sum_{i=1}^d A_a^i \otimes \overline{A_b^i},
\]
analysing its eigenvalue equation $\sum_i (A_b^i)^\dagger X A_a^i = \lambda X$
together with the trace-preserving relation and the strictly-positive
spectral fixed point $\Lambda_a$.  Cauchy--Schwarz applied to the resulting
trace inequality forces $|\lambda|<1$ or $|\lambda|=1$, and the latter case
yields $A_b^i X = \alpha X A_a^i$ with $|\alpha|=1$ and $X$ an isometry,
which after a dimension argument gives the structural conclusion.

\section{Formal decomposition of the structural conclusion}

The TNLean codebase represents the structural part of this lemma by two
separate theorems: one rectangular dimension theorem and one
same-bond-dimension gauge theorem.

The formalization represents the same-bond-dimension part of the $1$ case by
separating two mathematical steps.  First, the spectral-radius theorem shows
that asymptotically unit-modulus overlap forces the cross-transfer spectral
radius to be at least \(1\).\footnote{%
This theorem is
\leanid{MPSTensor.mixedTransferSpectralRadius\_ge\_one\_of\_mpvOverlap\_norm\_tendsto\_one};
it is in \path{TNLean/MPS/FundamentalTheorem/OverlapConsequences.lean} and formalizes
a step in the proof of Lemma equalMPS,
\cite[lines~1093--1117]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv}.}
This is the contrapositive of the already formalized decay theorem for mixed
transfer spectral radius \(<1\).

Second, the modulus-one spectral theorem turns the modulus-one spectral
situation into gauge-phase equivalence by the Cauchy--Schwarz argument with the
positive fixed point.\footnote{%
This theorem is
\leanid{MPSTensor.modulus\_one\_eigenvalue\_implies\_gauge\_of\_irreducible\_TP};
it is in \path{TNLean/MPS/FundamentalTheorem/OverlapConsequences.lean}.}
The resulting same-dimension gauge-phase theorem has the source's overlap
hypothesis and no MPV proportionality hypothesis, but it is stated for two
tensors with the same bond dimension.\footnote{%
The theorem is
\leanid{MPSTensor.gaugePhaseEquiv\_of\_overlap\_norm\_tendsto\_one\_of\_irreducible\_TP};
it is in \path{TNLean/MPS/FundamentalTheorem/OverlapConsequences.lean}.}
In this same-dimension component the formal theorem changes the normal-tensor
hypothesis set in a controlled way: in CPSV16 a normal tensor has no
non-trivial invariant projector, and its associated completely positive map
has a unique peripheral eigenvalue, equal to its spectral radius and normalized
to one.  The spectral-radius and Cauchy--Schwarz argument used here drops this
peripheral-simplicity assumption and takes irreducibility together with
trace-preserving normalization as explicit hypotheses.  This is a
generalization of the same-bond-dimension gauge step, not a formalization of
the rectangular dimension conclusion.

The rectangular dimension-recovery theorem handles the case in which the two
normal tensors may have different bond
dimensions.  Under the left-canonical irreducible hypotheses used in
\cite[lines~1093--1117]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv}, it proves $D_a=D_b$ from
$\|\langle V^{(N)}(A)\,|\,V^{(N)}(B)\rangle\|\to 1$.  Its proof uses the
rectangular decay theorem: if the bond dimensions differed, then the mixed
overlap would tend to zero,
contradicting the assumed limiting modulus.
\footnote{\leanid{MPSTensor.dim\_eq\_of\_overlap\_norm\_tendsto\_one\_of\_irreducible\_TP}
and
\leanid{MPSTensor.mpvOverlap\_tendsto\_zero\_of\_dim\_ne\_of\_irreducible\_TP},
\path{TNLean/MPS/FundamentalTheorem/OverlapConsequences.lean}.}

The gauge-recovery theorem has the source hypothesis shape: for two
irreducible trace-preserving tensors
of the same bond dimension, the assumption
$\|\langle V^{(N)}(A)\,|\,V^{(N)}(B)\rangle\|\to 1$ implies
gauge-phase equivalence.  It derives the mixed-transfer spectral-radius
condition from the asymptotically unit-modulus overlap and then applies the
modulus-one rigidity theorem.
\footnote{\leanid{MPSTensor.gaugePhaseEquiv\_of\_overlap\_norm\_tendsto\_one\_of\_irreducible\_TP},
\leanid{MPSTensor.mixedTransferSpectralRadius\_ge\_one\_of\_mpvOverlap\_norm\_tendsto\_one},
\leanid{MPSTensor.modulus\_one\_eigenvalue\_implies\_gauge\_of\_irreducible\_TP},
and \leanid{GaugePhaseEquiv}.}
The spectral-radius theorem formalizes the corresponding step in the proof of
the source lemma, \cite[lines~1093--1117]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv}; the
gauge theorem formalizes the source statement in
\cite[lines~1080--1091]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv} together with its proof in
\cite[lines~1093--1117]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv}.

The source-facing record does not treat a theorem with an additional
proportional-MPV hypothesis as a version of the source lemma, because the source
does not assume proportional MPV families.\footnote{\leanid{ProportionalMPV₂}.}

\section{Why this matters for the Fundamental Theorem proof}

The proof cited in \cite[Theorem~II.1, lines~1170--1192]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv}
uses the dichotomy and the gauge recovery on \emph{per-block} overlaps
$\langle V^{(N)}(B_k)\,|\,V^{(N)}(A_j)\rangle$.  The setup provides
proportionality of the \emph{total} tensors,
$V^{(N)}(A_{\mathrm{tot}}) = c_N V^{(N)}(B_{\mathrm{tot}})$, but not of the
individual blocks.

In that proof, these two theorems can be applied to each non-decaying per-block
mixed overlap: the rectangular theorem first
identifies the relevant bond dimensions, and the square theorem then supplies
the gauge-phase equivalence.

\section{Verdict}

There is no remaining proportionality-hypothesis discrepancy in the
left-canonical irreducible formulation used by the proof.  Asymptotically
unit-modulus overlap gives \(D_a=D_b\), and in the resulting common bond
dimension gives gauge-phase equivalence.  These two components represent the
structural conclusion of the source lemma at the call sites in the proof of
\cite[Theorem~II.1, lines~1170--1192]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv}.
