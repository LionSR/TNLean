\section{The assertion}

The review \cite{Cirac2021Matrix} records that the AKLT matrix product state lies
in a non-trivial symmetry-protected topological phase, and that this phase ``can
be protected by multiple distinct physical symmetries: on-site $\mathrm{SO}(3)$
(with virtual symmetry given by a spin-$\tfrac12$ representation), on-site
$\mathbb{Z}_2\times\mathbb{Z}_2$ (the smallest subgroup of $\mathrm{SO}(3)$ still
exhibiting a non-trivial 2-cocycle), time-reversal, or reflection
symmetry.''\footnote{\cite{Cirac2021Matrix}, around line 1159 of the source.}
In each case the physical symmetry acts on the spin-$1$ site, while the
entanglement (virtual) degrees of freedom carry a projective representation whose
cohomology class is the order-two generator of $H^2$ of the symmetry group with
$\mathrm{U}(1)$ coefficients.

The strongest such statement is on-site $\mathrm{SO}(3)$: the spin-$1$
representation acts on the physical index, and the virtual index transforms in
the projective spin-$\tfrac12$ representation, whose class generates
$H^2(\mathrm{SO}(3),\mathrm{U}(1))\cong\mathbb{Z}_2$.

\section{The point at issue}

Two different statements must be separated: the on-site $\mathrm{SO}(3)$
\emph{symmetry}, and the cohomological \emph{classification} of the phase it
protects. They have very different formalization costs.

\paragraph{The symmetry.}
This statement is now formalized. For every rotation $R\in\mathrm{SO}(3)$ the
Cartesian AKLT tensor $A^i=\sigma^i$ twisted by $R$ is gauge equivalent to the
original, the gauge being an $\mathrm{SU}(2)$ preimage of $R$.\footnote{%
\leanid{aklt\_isOnSiteSymmetric\_SO3} in \doclink{TNLean/MPS/Examples/AKLTRotation}.}
The one substantive input is the surjectivity of the adjoint double cover
$\mathrm{SU}(2)\to\mathrm{SO}(3)$, that every rotation is realized by conjugating
the Pauli vector $(\sigma_x,\sigma_y,\sigma_z)$ by an $\mathrm{SU}(2)$ element.
It is proved by an explicit $ZXZ$ Euler-angle construction: each coordinate
rotation is the image of an explicit $\mathrm{SU}(2)$ generator, and an arbitrary
rotation is factored into coordinate rotations.\footnote{%
\leanid{spinHalfCover\_surjective\_onto\_SO3}.} The physical spin-$1$
representation is just the defining action of $\mathrm{SO}(3)$ on the three
Cartesian components, and the adjoint map $U\mapsto R(U)$ is a monoid
homomorphism, so its image is automatically a subgroup; hitting the Euler
generators therefore yields surjectivity with no appeal to Lie-group topology, to
the Clifford spin group, or to a quaternion bridge (none of which the library
provides).

\paragraph{The classification.}
The claim that the resulting projective phase is the order-two generator of
$H^2(\mathrm{SO}(3),\mathrm{U}(1))\cong\mathbb{Z}_2$ is a different matter
entirely. It requires the continuous (Borel, or Lie-group) cohomology of
$\mathrm{SO}(3)$, equivalently its Schur multiplier. The group cohomology in the
library is purely algebraic and treats $\mathrm{SO}(3)$ as a discrete group, so
it computes the wrong object; as of this writing there is no Schur multiplier and
no projective-representation interface, and the nascent continuous-cohomology
framework has no computations and is not yet reconciled with the discrete theory.
This piece is
a substantial development on its own.

\section{The formalized statement}

The formalization establishes the full on-site $\mathrm{SO}(3)$ symmetry of the
Cartesian AKLT tensor $A^i=\sigma^i$: the physical site carries the spin-$1$
(defining) representation, and for each rotation the bond index is conjugated by
a spin-$\tfrac12$ $\mathrm{SU}(2)$ preimage. The finite
$\mathbb{Z}_2\times\mathbb{Z}_2$ subgroup, which the review singles out as the
smallest subgroup of $\mathrm{SO}(3)$ carrying a non-trivial $2$-cocycle, is
formalized separately on the $|m\rangle$-basis tensor with the explicit
anticommuting gauges $i\sigma_y$ and
$\sigma_z$.\footnote{\leanid{aklt\_isOnSiteSymmetric\_Z2Z2} in
\doclink{TNLean/MPS/Examples/AKLT}.}

In both cases the bond representation is projective: rotations that commute have
$\mathrm{SU}(2)$ preimages that need not commute, and that sign obstruction is the
content of the symmetry-protected phase. For $\mathbb{Z}_2\times\mathbb{Z}_2$ the
obstruction is the explicit anticommutator $i\sigma_y\,\sigma_z=-\sigma_z\,i\sigma_y$;
for the full $\mathrm{SO}(3)$ it is the non-triviality of the double cover.

\section{Conclusion}

The on-site $\mathrm{SO}(3)$ symmetry asserted by \cite{Cirac2021Matrix},
together with the surjectivity of the spin-$\tfrac12$ cover that it rests on, is
now formalized. What remains unformalized is the \emph{cohomological
classification} of the resulting projective phase as the order-two generator of
$H^2(\mathrm{SO}(3),\mathrm{U}(1))\cong\mathbb{Z}_2$: this needs the continuous
group cohomology of $\mathrm{SO}(3)$ (equivalently its Schur multiplier), which
the library does not provide. The blueprint therefore tags the symmetry and the
cover-surjectivity against their formal declarations, and records the
cohomological classification as an untagged remark.
