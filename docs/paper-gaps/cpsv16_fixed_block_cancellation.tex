\section{Source passage}

This note concerns the proof of CPSV16 Theorem~\texttt{thm1}, lines
1181--1185 of \cite{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv}.  The paper fixes a block
\(k_0\in\mathrm{Fin}\,r_B\) and writes
\begin{align}
    \forall j,\;
    \langle V^{(N)}(B_{k_0}),V^{(N)}(A_j)\rangle
    \xrightarrow[N\to\infty]{} 0
\end{align}
as a contradiction to eventual proportionality of the two full MPV families.
Under the BNT decomposition, the proportionality hypothesis is
\begin{align}
    \sum_j \mu_j^N\,V^{(N)}(A_j)=c_N\sum_k \nu_k^N\,V^{(N)}(B_k),
\end{align}
and isolating the selected block gives
\begin{align}
    \sum_j \mu_j^N\,V^{(N)}(A_j)-c_N\nu_{k_0}^N\,V^{(N)}(B_{k_0})
    =c_N\sum_{k\neq k_0}\nu_k^N\,V^{(N)}(B_k).
\end{align}
Lemma~\texttt{Lem1} in \cite{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv} says that, from the left-hand
decay, the family
\begin{align}
    \operatorname{LI}\!\left(
    \left\{V^{(N)}(A_j)\right\}_{j<r_A}\cup\left\{V^{(N)}(B_{k_0})\right\}
    \right)
\end{align}
is linearly independent for large \(N\), so the selected block cannot be
absorbed by the others without contradiction. \cite[lines~1181--1185]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv}

\section{Formal boundary}

The formal boundary in \cite{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv} is narrower:
\begin{align}
    \forall^\mathrm{cof} N,\;
    \operatorname{LI}\!\left(
    \left\{V^{(N)}(A_j)\right\}_{j<r_A}\cup\left\{V^{(N)}(B_{k_0})\right\}
    \right).
\end{align}
From this, formalized declarations of finite-weight eventual LI are available on
both sides:
\leanid{MPSTensor.eventually\_linearIndependent\_all\_left\_single\_right\_of\_all\_overlaps\_decay\_CFBNT}
and
\leanid{MPSTensor.eventually\_linearIndependent\_all\_right\_single\_left\_of\_all\_overlaps\_decay\_CFBNT}.
The exact contradiction step is still open:
\begin{align}
    \bigl(\forall j,\; \langle V^{(N)}(B_{k_0}),V^{(N)}(A_j)\rangle\to 0\bigr)
    \Rightarrow \bot
\end{align}
The two blocked theorems are
\leanid{MPSTensor.fixed\_right\_all\_overlaps\_decay\_false\_of\_eventuallyNonzeroProportionalMPV2\_CFBNT}
and

which are the source-faithful fixed-block cancellations \cite[lines~1181--1185]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv}.

The no-tail cases are already proved:

without extracting the exact finite-coefficient identity needed in the same step.

Conditional tail-peeling declarations exist, but they require stronger LI than the

These are algebraically correct and useful in future induction steps, but they
are not faithful replacements for the source sentence at lines~1181--1185.

\section{Elimination plan}

To remain faithful, the two missing theorems must be derived only from the
source input in \cite{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv}:
\begin{align}
    \forall^\text{cof}N,\;
    \mathsf{Hyp}_{\text{LI}}(N),
    \quad
    \mathsf{Hyp}_{\text{prop}}(N)
    \Rightarrow
    \forall^\text{cof}N,\;
    \forall j,\;
    \langle V^{(N)}(B_{k_0}),V^{(N)}(A_j)\rangle\not\to 0.
\end{align}
A plausible formal route is an induction that removes matched leading blocks
\begin{align}
    a_0:=V^{(N_0)}(A_{j_0}), \quad b_0:=V^{(N_0)}(B_{k_0}),\\
    (a_0,b_0)\mapsto (a_1,b_1)\mapsto\cdots
\end{align}
while carrying forward eventual proportionality on stripped tails and using the
two leading-contradiction lemmas at each step.  A direct quotient isolation
argument is another option.
\footnote{Either route must avoid importing residual linear independence assumptions
    that are not present in \cite[lines~1181--1185]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv}.}
