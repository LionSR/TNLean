\section{Source step in \leanid{thm1}}

The step appears in \cite[Theorem~\leanid{thm1}, lines~1181--1185]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv}.

Let \(A,B\) be in canonical form with BNT decompositions
\begin{align}
    V^{(N)}(A) &=\sum_{j=1}^{g_A}\mu_j^N a_j(N), &
    V^{(N)}(B)=\sum_{\ell=1}^{g_B}\nu_\ell^N b_\ell(N),
\end{align}
for all \(N\), and
\begin{align}
    a_j(N) &:=V^{(N)}(A_j),\qquad b_\ell(N):=V^{(N)}(B_\ell).
\end{align}

The source hypothesis is total proportionality:
\begin{align}
    \exists c_N\in\mathbb C,\qquad
    \sum_{j=1}^{g_A}\mu_j^N a_j(N) &=
    c_N\sum_{\ell=1}^{g_B}\nu_\ell^N b_\ell(N).
\end{align}
Assume for a fixed \(k\),
\begin{align}
    \langle b_k(N),a_j(N)\rangle &\to 0\quad\forall j\in\{1,\dots,g_A\}.
\end{align}
If every \(j\) has this asymptotic orthogonality, then
\begin{align}
    \langle b_k(N),V^{(N)}(A)\rangle &\to0,
\end{align}
which contradicts source proportionality of \(V^{(N)}(A)\) and \(V^{(N)}(B)\) by
\cite[Corollary~\leanid{eqV}]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv}. Hence some \(j_k\) satisfies
\begin{align}
    b_k(N) &\sim e^{i\phi_{j_k}N}\,a_{j_k}(N)\quad(N\to\infty),
\end{align}
and thus \(g_a\ge g_b\). Symmetrically \(g_b\ge g_a\), so \(g_a=g_b\).

The part we need to scrutinize is the sentence that turns \((\mathrm{D}_k)\) plus
\cite[Lemma~\leanid{Lem1}]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv} into a contradiction.
Lemma~\leanid{Lem1} states:
For large \(N\), \(\langle V_j|V_{j'}\rangle\to\delta_{j,j'}\) and linear independence of \(\{V_j\}\) both hold.
\begin{align}
    \langle V_j|V_{j'}\rangle &\to\delta_{j,j'}.
\end{align}

\section{Exact contradiction algebra}

From \((\mathrm{P}_N)\) with the \(b_k\)-term separated, we obtain
\begin{align}
    \sum_{j=1}^{g_A}\mu_j^N a_j(N)-c_N\nu_k^N b_k(N) &=
    c_N\sum_{\ell\ne k}\nu_\ell^N b_\ell(N).
\end{align}
and inner pairing with \(a_{j'}(N)\) gives
\begin{align}
    \langle a_{j'}(N),\sum_{j=1}^{g_A}\mu_j^N a_j(N)\rangle
     &=\mu_{j'}^N\,\|a_{j'}(N)\|^2+c_N\sum_{\ell\ne k}\nu_\ell^N\langle a_{j'}(N),b_\ell(N)\rangle,\\
    \mu_{j'}^N\,\|a_{j'}(N)\|^2
     &=\langle a_{j'}(N),c_N\sum_{\ell\ne k}\nu_\ell^N b_\ell(N)\rangle
\end{align}
in the tail-free case, the right side vanishes for fixed \(j'\) because \(\langle a_{j'}(N),b_\ell(N)\rangle\to0\) for all \(\ell\ne k\), yielding \(c_N\nu_k^N=0\) after coefficient extraction.
Lemma~\leanid{Lem1} for side \(b_k\) gives linear independence of
\begin{align}
    \{a_1(N),\dots,a_{g_A}(N),b_k(N)\}
\end{align}
for \(N\gg0\), and therefore it allows coefficient extraction in any relation of the form
\begin{align}
    \sum_{j=1}^{g_A}\alpha_j a_j(N)+\beta\,b_k(N) &=0.
\end{align}
That is,
\begin{align}
    \operatorname{LinIndep}\{a_j(N),b_k(N)\}
    \Longrightarrow
    \left(\sum_{j=1}^{g_A}\mu_j^N a_j(N)-c_N\nu_k^N b_k(N)=0\right)
    \Longrightarrow c_N\nu_k^N=0.
\end{align}
The proof in \cite{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv} does not prove this directly, because
the displayed identity has the right-hand residual tail \(c_N\sum_{\ell\ne k}\nu_\ell^N b_\ell(N)\), not \(0\).

\begin{align}
    \sum_{\ell\ne k}\nu_\ell^N b_\ell(N)\in
    \operatorname{span}\{a_j(N)\}_{j=1}^{g_A}
    \Longrightarrow
    \exists f_N,\
    f_N(a_j(N))=0,\ f_N(b_\ell(N))=0\ (\ell\ne k),\ f_N(b_k(N))\neq0.
\end{align}

So the missing implication is
\begin{align}
    \sum_{\ell\ne k}\nu_\ell^N b_\ell(N)\in
    \operatorname{span}\{a_j(N)\}_{j=1}^{g_A}
\end{align}
or equivalently the existence of a functional \(f_N\) with
\begin{align}
    f_N(a_j(N)) &=0,\quad f_N(b_\ell(N))=0\ (\ell\ne k),\quad f_N(b_k(N))=1.
\end{align}
This is strictly stronger than Lemma~\leanid{Lem1}. The cited step does not provide it.
\begin{align}
    \sum_{\ell\ne k}\nu_\ell^N b_\ell(N)\in
    \operatorname{span}\{a_j(N)\}_{j=1}^{g_A}
\end{align}

\section{Why dominant-block arguments do not close the gap}

If \(k\) is leading in \(B\) and one divides by the leading weight, the source-style tail estimate is
\begin{align}
    c_N\sum_{\ell=1}^{g_B}\left(\frac{\nu_\ell}{\nu_k}\right)^N\langle b_\ell(N),b_k(N)\rangle,
\end{align}
with \(\langle b_k(N),b_k(N)\rangle\to1\) and \(|\nu_\ell/\nu_k|\le 1\).
For \(k\) not leading there is no guarantee that terms with \(|\nu_\ell/\nu_k|>1\) are absent.
The projection can therefore fail to isolate \(b_k\) without a stronger residual-tail premise.

\section{Left-right symmetric version}

Swapping \(A,B\) gives the analogous condition
\begin{align}
    \langle a_i(N),V^{(N)}(B)\rangle &=0
    \Longrightarrow
    \langle a_i(N),\sum_{\ell=1}^{g_B}\nu_\ell^N b_\ell(N)\rangle=0.
\end{align}

From
\begin{align}
    \langle a_i(N),b_\ell(N)\rangle\to0\quad\forall\ell
\end{align}
the source argument would isolate one \(a_i\) in the tail decomposition.
Its required linear-independence input is
\begin{align}
    \operatorname{LinIndep}\{a_i(N),b_1(N),\dots,b_{g_B}(N)\},
\end{align}
again only for one fixed \(i\). The same residual-tail strengthening is still absent in
\cite[Theorem~\leanid{thm1}, line~1182]{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv}.

\section{Current formal status}

The formal declarations matching the fixed-block cancellation line are
\leanid{MPSTensor.fixed\_right\_all\_overlaps\_decay\_false\_of\_eventuallyNonzeroProportionalMPV2\_CFBNT}
and
\leanid{MPSTensor.fixed\_left\_all\_overlaps\_decay\_false\_of\_eventuallyNonzeroProportionalMPV2\_CFBNT}.
Their currently used conditional lemmas assume exactly the tail-freeing hypotheses.
The conditional inputs are
\begin{align}
    \operatorname{LinIndep}\{a_j,b_\ell:\ell\ne k\}\cup\{b_k\},
\end{align}
and
\begin{align}
    \operatorname{LinIndep}\{a_i,b_1,\dots,b_{\cdot-1},b_{\cdot+1},\dots,b_{g_B}\}.
\end{align}
These are not derivable from Lemma~\leanid{Lem1} in the cited source step.
\begin{align}
    g_a &=g_b.
\end{align}

Issue~\#1607 tracks the gap: proving arbitrary fixed-block cancellation from only the source linear-independence family.

\section{Impact}

In the no-tail base case (when the right or left side has no residual blocks), the algebra above simplifies to coefficient extraction and is already formalized.
The remaining difficulty is the full residual tail for arbitrary blocks, which requires a new source-faithful cancellation argument.
