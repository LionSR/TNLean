\section{Source passage}

This note concerns the proof of CPSV16 Theorem~\texttt{thm1}, lines
1181--1185 of \cite{Cirac2016MPDO_arXiv}.  The proof fixes a block \(B_k\) in
one BNT family and says that it is impossible for
\[
  \langle V^{(N)}(B_k),V^{(N)}(A_j)\rangle \to 0
  \quad\text{for every }j,
\]
because otherwise the total MPV families generated by \(A\) and \(B\) could not
be proportional, by Lemma~\texttt{Lem1}.  The same argument with \(A\) and
\(B\) interchanged gives the opposite block-count inequality.

\section{Formal boundary}

Lemma~\texttt{Lem1} directly gives the following local conclusion: for all
sufficiently large lengths, the family consisting of all \(A_j\) together with
the single fixed vector \(B_k\) is linearly independent.  This is now recorded
formally for the fixed-right and fixed-left cases.

This local linear independence is not, by itself, the whole contradiction.
The proportionality identity is an equality of full weighted sums,
\[
  \sum_j \mu_j^N V^{(N)}(A_j)
  =
  c_N\sum_\ell \nu_\ell^N V^{(N)}(B_\ell).
\]
After the selected term \(B_k\) is separated, the other \(B\)-summands remain
on the same side of the equation.  They are not part of the linearly
independent family supplied by the fixed-block application of
Lemma~\texttt{Lem1}.  Therefore a formal proof still needs an isolation, or
cancellation, argument showing that the selected \(B_k\)-summand cannot be
cancelled by the remaining \(B\)-summands under the BNT hypotheses and
proportionality of the total MPV families.

\section{Formal declarations}

The two named proof obligations are
\[
\begin{aligned}
&\leanid{MPSTensor.fixed\_right\_all\_overlaps\_decay\_false\_of\_eventuallyNonzeroProportionalMPV₂\_CFBNT},\\
&\leanid{MPSTensor.fixed\_left\_all\_overlaps\_decay\_false\_of\_eventuallyNonzeroProportionalMPV₂\_CFBNT}.
\end{aligned}
\]
They are statements of the cancellation step implicit in the source passage,
not strengthened variants of the source theorem.  The formal proof obligation
is tracked by issue~\#1607.

The no-tail base cases, where the selected side has no other summands, are
already formalized.  In those cases ordinary coefficient extraction from the
eventual linear independence supplied by Lemma~\texttt{Lem1} forces a nonzero
selected coefficient to vanish.  The corresponding declarations are
\[
\begin{aligned}
&\leanid{MPSTensor.fixed\_right\_all\_overlaps\_decay\_false\_of\_eventuallyNonzeroProportionalMPV₂\_CFBNT\_finOne},\\
&\leanid{MPSTensor.fixed\_left\_all\_overlaps\_decay\_false\_of\_eventuallyNonzeroProportionalMPV₂\_CFBNT\_finOne}.
\end{aligned}
\]

The leading-block cases are also formalized.  They are discharged by the
dominant projection contradiction already available for eventually nonzero
proportional BNT families:
\[
\begin{aligned}
&\leanid{MPSTensor.fixed\_right\_leading\_all\_overlaps\_decay\_false\_of\_eventuallyNonzeroProportionalMPV₂\_CFBNT},\\
&\leanid{MPSTensor.fixed\_left\_leading\_all\_overlaps\_decay\_false\_of\_eventuallyNonzeroProportionalMPV₂\_CFBNT}.
\end{aligned}
\]

\section{Elimination plan}

The intended elimination is to prove the two fixed-block contradiction
declarations from the BNT hypotheses, eventual nonzero proportionality, and the
already formalized fixed-block Lemma~\texttt{Lem1} inputs.  A proof may use an
induction through matched block removal, using the leading-block
contradictions above as the case in which the selected block has become
dominant, or a direct argument isolating the selected summand in the quotient
by the span of the opposite family.  It must not assume global linear
independence of all remaining blocks, since that is not a consequence of the
BNT hypotheses once a block on one side may be phase-equivalent to a block on
the other.
